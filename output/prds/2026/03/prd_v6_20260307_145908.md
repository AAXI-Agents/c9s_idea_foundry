# Executive Summary

**Problem Statement**
Tier-1 brand marketing teams and global social media agencies face a critical bottleneck: scaling founder-led and creator-led video content across fast-paced platforms like TikTok and Facebook Reels without requiring the physical presence of the talent. While consumer-grade AI video generators promise scale, enterprise adoption is blocked by three severe, non-obvious risks:
1. **IP & Digital Rights Management (DRM):** Utilizing an AI avatar of a creator whose legal contract has expired invites immediate, multi-million dollar copyright infringement lawsuits.
2. **Cost Predictability:** Iterating on high-fidelity video drafts iteratively via expensive generation models causes rapid API cost spirals, destroying campaign margins.
3. **Brand Safety:** Autonomous AI script rewrites optimizing for TikTok audio trends risk violating strict enterprise Brand Tone of Voice (ToV), leading to catastrophic PR crises. 

Furthermore, current tools force Account Managers to context-switch into heavy, siloed web UIs, introducing unacceptable friction into high-velocity agency workflows. A robust enterprise solution must evolve from a simple generative tool into a financially governed, legally secure orchestration platform.

**Target Audience & Key Stakeholders**
* **Primary End-Users:** Account Managers and Social Strategists. They live in Slack, prioritize rapid execution, and need to produce localized, trend-relevant video drafts without leaving their primary communication hub.
* **Administrative & Governance Stakeholders:** Agency Directors, Legal Counsel, and Procurement Teams. They require strict API budget quotas, immutable audit logs, deterministic DRM enforcement, and centralized calendar management.

**Proposed Solution & Key Differentiators**
The **Enterprise Agentic Avatar Orchestration & Governance Platform** is an event-driven, AI-powered video synthesis ecosystem. It abstracts the complexity of prompt engineering and compliance behind a high-velocity Slack integration (`/avatar-gen`), powered by a modern, containerized **FastAPI** backend and governed by **MongoDB Atlas**. 

The platform’s core differentiator is its **CrewAI** multi-agent orchestration layer, functioning as an autonomous production crew with deterministic governance:
* **Trend & Brand Safety Agent:** Rewrites user prompts to optimize for social media pacing while strictly enforcing brand-specific ToV guardrails to prevent profanity or off-brand messaging.
* **Platform & DRM Compliance Agent:** Maps mandatory platform AIGC disclosure toggles to the API payload and queries a Time-to-Live (TTL) creator contract database in MongoDB Atlas. If a creator's digital likeness rights have expired, it triggers an immediate hard-block on generation.
* **Production Orchestrator Agent:** Handles downstream API calls to video/voice synthesis models and manages complex multi-language localization routing and caption safe-zones.

Users iterate on low-resolution previews via Slack interactive Block Kit messages. Only upon final approval is a high-fidelity render executed, preserving API budgets. A **React + TypeScript** administrative dashboard acts as the command center for Agency Directors to configure environment-variable-driven feature toggles, oversee workspace budget quotas, and review interaction logs stored seamlessly in MongoDB.

**Business Impact & Success Criteria**
The platform will fundamentally alter campaign economics by achieving the following SMART criteria:
1. **Workflow Velocity:** Deliver the initial Slack-based, low-resolution preview video asynchronously within **< 3 minutes** from the initial `/avatar-gen` command.
2. **Compliance Integrity:** Achieve a **100% automated application rate** for platform-required AIGC tags and **zero IP breaches** via the MongoDB-backed DRM hard-blocker.
3. **Operational Efficiency:** Deliver a measurable **60% reduction** in end-to-end campaign execution time compared to traditional manual video production and editing workflows.
4. **System Reliability:** The FastAPI REST infrastructure must sustain **99.9% uptime**, gracefully handling concurrent async generation requests across hundreds of agency workspaces without dropping Slack webhook events or degrading UX.

## Problem Statement

Global social media agencies and Tier-1 brands are under immense pressure to produce high volumes of trend-relevant, localized video content across fast-paced platforms like TikTok and Facebook Reels. Currently, teams rely either on slow, expensive physical shoots—often requiring 48 to 72 hours for a single turnaround—or they attempt to bridge the gap using disjointed, consumer-grade AI avatar generators. Both approaches are fundamentally broken at the enterprise level. While standard AI tools offer theoretical scalability, they lack the governance, financial controls, and seamless workflow integration required by large-holding companies. 

This status quo introduces four severe, non-obvious risks to the enterprise:

*   **Severe DRM & Legal Vulnerabilities:** Existing AI avatar platforms are completely decoupled from agency procurement and legal systems. If a team generates a video using the cloned likeness of a creator whose contract expired 24 hours ago, the agency is immediately exposed to severe IP infringement lawsuits. Additionally, failing to correctly map platform-mandated AIGC (AI-Generated Content) disclosure tags or ignoring "Safe-Zone" caption overlays—which leads to critical text being obscured by platform UI and subsequent technical rejection—exposes the brand to regulatory penalties and shadow-banning.
*   **Unmanaged API Cost Spiraling:** Consumer-grade tools allow for unfettered generation without distinguishing between a "cheap" draft and an "expensive" final render. When Account Managers iteratively prompt and re-render a video draft multiple times to get the perfect result, they are forced to process full, high-fidelity generations every time. Consequently, the underlying API costs for video and voice synthesis models spiral out of control, quickly destroying campaign margins and rendering cost predictability impossible.
*   **Catastrophic Brand Safety Risks:** Attempting to adapt content to rapidly moving social trends using standard LLMs often results in unacceptable script modifications. An AI aggressively rewriting a script to match the pacing of a viral TikTok audio trend without strict adherence to a brand's Tone of Voice (ToV) can easily introduce off-brand slang or restricted keywords, risking a catastrophic PR crisis.
*   **Workflow Friction and Context Switching:** Social Strategists and Account Managers execute campaigns natively within collaborative environments like Slack. Forcing these users to context-switch into heavy, isolated web applications to draft, download, share, and gather approvals for AI video content creates massive bottlenecks and drastically reduces operational velocity compared to their primary communication streams.

**Business Impact:** 
The lack of integrated governance transforms AI video generation from a competitive advantage into a severe liability. Agencies are currently forced into a lose-lose scenario: either bottleneck campaign execution through slow, manual legal and budget reviews, or move quickly using siloed AI tools and risk massive financial overages and IP litigation.

**Why Now?**
The half-life of a social media trend is currently measured in hours, not weeks. To remain competitive, brands must capitalize on trends instantly and localize them globally. As enterprise adoption of generative AI accelerates, the market urgency has shifted from simply "generating video" to orchestrating it safely. Without an automated, legally compliant, and financially governed workflow, Tier-1 brands cannot safely scale their social media presence using AI.

## Functional Requirements

### 1. Slack Conversational Interface (Execution Layer)

**FR-001: Video Generation Trigger & Input Validation**  
*   **Priority:** SHALL  
*   **Description:** The system must validate and process generation requests via a dedicated Slack slash command.  
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**  
    *   **Given** a user is in an authorized Slack channel,  
    *   **When** they execute `/avatar-gen [platform] @[creator_handle] "[intent_text]"`,  
    *   **Then** the FastAPI backend validates the inputs: `[platform]` must strictly match `ENUM(tiktok, reels)`, `creator_handle` must match regex `^[a-zA-Z0-9._]{1,30}$`, and `intent_text` must be between 10 and 500 characters.  
    *   **Then** upon successful validation, the backend returns an ephemeral "Generation Started" message (HTTP 202) and initiates the CrewAI pipeline.  

**FR-002: Agentic Heartbeat Notifications**  
*   **Priority:** SHALL  
*   **Description:** The system must provide interim status updates in the Slack thread to prevent duplicate triggers during the asynchronous processing window.  
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**  
    *   **Given** a generation task is running in the CrewAI pipeline,  
    *   **When** the task transitions between internal agents (e.g., from Brand Safety Agent to DRM Compliance Agent),  
    *   **Then** the FastAPI backend updates the Slack thread with a transient Block Kit status message (e.g., "⏳ *DRM Compliance check in progress...*").  

**FR-003: Asynchronous Draft Delivery & External Link Generation**  
*   **Priority:** SHALL  
*   **Description:** The system must deliver low-res results and a secure external link for stakeholder review.  
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**  
    *   **Given** a successful low-res render,  
    *   **When** the Production Orchestrator completes the task,  
    *   **Then** the system posts a Slack thread containing: (1) The rewritten script, (2) A Brand Safety Risk Score (0-100), (3) A watermarked MP4 preview, and (4) A 12-character unique Approval Hash URL generated by FastAPI.  

**FR-004: Interactive Block Kit Workflow & Revision Loop**  
*   **Priority:** SHALL  
*   **Description:** The system must handle internal "Revise" requests and route external web decisions back to the thread.  
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**  
    *   **Given** a draft thread in Slack,  
    *   **When** a user clicks the "Revise" Block Kit button,  
    *   **Then** the system prompts for a text-based "Revision Instruction" and restarts the CrewAI pipeline from the Trend Agent stage.  

### 2. CrewAI Orchestration Engine (Agentic Layer)

**FR-005: Trend & Brand Safety Agent Reasoning**  
*   **Priority:** SHALL  
*   **Description:** An agent evaluates the prompt against Tone of Voice (ToV) and restricted keyword lists.  
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**  
    *   **Given** a sanitized script input,  
    *   **When** the agent identifies a ToV violation or restricted keyword,  
    *   **Then** it must provide a JSON "Reasoning Chain" document explaining the violation for the audit log, suggest an alternative compliant phrasing, and output the revised payload.  

**FR-006: Platform & DRM Compliance Agent (Contract Hard-Block)**  
*   **Priority:** SHALL  
*   **Description:** Hard-block generation if creator likeness rights have expired in MongoDB.  
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**  
    *   **Given** a validated creator handle,  
    *   **When** the agent queries MongoDB and `contract_expiration_date` < `ISO.utcnow()`,  
    *   **Then** the agent sends a `BLOCK_REQUEST` event to FastAPI, immediately halts synthesis, and posts a "DRM Violation: Contract Expired" message in the Slack thread.  

**FR-007: Automated AIGC Disclosure & Safe-Zone Enforcement**  
*   **Priority:** SHALL  
*   **Description:** Apply mandatory metadata and visual bounding boxes based on the target platform.  
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**  
    *   **Given** a valid target platform (e.g., `tiktok`),  
    *   **When** the Production Orchestrator generates captions and visual overlays,  
    *   **Then** it enforces a platform-specific coordinate mask (e.g., Bottom 25% and Right 15% clear of text) and injects `is_aigc=true` into the video metadata.  

### 3. Administrative Governance & API Specifications (React/FastAPI Layer)

**FR-008: API Endpoint Definitions (FastAPI)**  
*   **Priority:** SHALL  
*   **Description:** The system must expose the following REST endpoints for Slack and frontend integration.  

| Method | Path | Request Schema | Response Schema | Persona |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| `POST` | `/api/v1/slack/events` | `SlackEventPayload` | `202 Accepted` | Sarah |
| `GET` | `/api/v1/approval/{hash}` | `Path Parameter: hash` | `HTML Web View` | David |
| `POST` | `/api/v1/approval/{hash}/submit` | `{ decision: Enum(Go, NoGo), comments: str }` | `200 OK` | David |
| `GET` | `/api/v1/admin/creators` | `None` | `List[CreatorTTLRecord]` | Elena |
| `PATCH` | `/api/v1/admin/config` | `{ workspace_id: str, high_fi_limit: int }` | `200 OK` | Marcus |

**FR-009: External Stakeholder Approval Loop**  
*   **Priority:** SHALL  
*   **Description:** Process external "Go/No-Go" decisions and pipe them into Slack.  
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**  
    *   **Given** a submitted decision from `/api/v1/approval/{hash}/submit`,  
    *   **When** the FastAPI backend processes the payload,  
    *   **Then** it asynchronously posts the decision and comments as a new Block Kit message in the original Slack thread.  

**FR-010: Workspace Budget Quota Configuration**  
*   **Priority:** SHALL  
*   **Description:** Restrict high-fidelity renders based on React-configured limits.  
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**  
    *   **Given** a workspace has reached its monthly `high_fi_limit`,  
    *   **When** a user clicks "Approve & Render" in Slack,  
    *   **Then** the system returns a "Quota Exceeded" Block Kit alert, logs the rejection, and does *not* call the synthesis API.  

**FR-011: Creator Likeness Inventory Management**  
*   **Priority:** SHALL  
*   **Description:** Provide a CRUD interface for creator contract management in the React Dashboard.  
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**  
    *   **When** Elena updates an expiration date via the dashboard UI,  
    *   **Then** the system performs an `upsert` in the MongoDB Atlas `creators` collection within < 500ms, immediately enforcing the new TTL on the next Slack request.  

**FR-012: Agent Reasoning & Interaction Audit Logging**  
*   **Priority:** SHALL  
*   **Description:** Persistence of agent reasoning chains for enterprise compliance.  
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**  
    *   **Given** a generation lifecycle concludes (success or failure),  
    *   **Then** the system writes a non-blocking JSON document to the `audit_logs` collection including: the raw Slack payload, all CrewAI JSON reasoning chains, the final brand safety score, and the user's final approval/rejection timestamp.

## Non-Functional Requirements

### 1. Performance & Latency

**NFR-001: Asynchronous Acknowledgment Latency**  
*   **Target:** The FastAPI backend MUST acknowledge Slack `/avatar-gen` commands and interactive Block Kit clicks within 2.5 seconds (p99).  
*   **Rationale:** Slack’s Events API enforces a strict 3.0-second timeout. Failure to acknowledge within this window results in user-facing timeout errors. The CrewAI processing must be offloaded to a background task queue immediately after validation.

**NFR-002: Workflow Velocity (Time-to-Preview)**  
*   **Target:** The system MUST deliver the initial low-resolution preview video to the Slack thread in under 180 seconds (3 minutes) for 95% of generation requests.  
*   **Rationale:** Ensures high-velocity execution for the High-Velocity Executor persona. The combined latency of CrewAI reasoning and low-res synthesis must stay within this threshold to prevent user abandonment.

**NFR-003: Database Query Latency (DRM Hard-Block)**  
*   **Target:** The MongoDB Atlas query to verify the `expiration_date` of a creator's TTL contract MUST execute and return a clearance state in < 50ms (p95).  
*   **Rationale:** DRM verification is a synchronous blocking check before the expensive synthesis API is called; it must not delay the overall pipeline.

### 2. Scalability & Concurrency

**NFR-004: Concurrent Generation Throughput**  
*   **Target:** The system MUST support a minimum of 500 concurrent Slack generation triggers per minute across the multi-tenant architecture without dropping requests or exceeding the SLA in NFR-002.  
*   **Rationale:** Handles peak agency hours and breaking social trends where hundreds of Account Managers may trigger the system simultaneously.

**NFR-005: Audit Data Volume Management & Retention**  
*   **Target:** The MongoDB `audit_logs` collection MUST support up to 50,000 document writes per day (approx. 50GB/month).  
*   **Retention:** Audit logs MUST be retained in the primary "hot" collection for 12 months, after which they are automatically offloaded to cold storage.  
*   **Rationale:** Prevents performance degradation of the React dashboard while satisfying the Compliance persona’s legal auditing requirements.

### 3. Reliability & Availability

**NFR-006: System Uptime**  
*   **Target:** The FastAPI backend and React dashboard MUST sustain 99.9% uptime (excluding scheduled maintenance).  
*   **Rationale:** Agency Account Managers operate globally across different time zones. The tool must be highly available to ensure campaign execution is never blocked.

**NFR-007: Graceful Degradation & API Resilience**  
*   **Target:** The system MUST implement circuit breakers for all 3rd-party LLM and Video Synthesis APIs. If a provider returns a 429 (Rate Limit) or 5xx error, the system MUST execute an exponential backoff strategy and notify the Slack thread of the delay within 30 seconds.  
*   **Rationale:** Prevents "silent failures" and protects the system from upstream provider instability, directly addressing the pain point of opaque generative AI errors.

### 4. Security & Compliance

**NFR-008: External Approval Link Security**  
*   **Target:** Secure External Approval Links generated for Brand Stakeholders MUST utilize a cryptographically secure, non-sequential hash (UUIDv4) and MUST expire exactly 72 hours after generation.  
*   **Rationale:** Protects highly confidential, unreleased brand campaign content from URL guessing or scraping by unauthorized actors.

**NFR-009: Data Encryption & Residency**  
*   **Target:** All creator data, agency budgets, and proprietary scripts MUST be encrypted at rest within MongoDB Atlas (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.2+). The system MUST support multi-region data residency configuration to ensure agency data stays within specified legal jurisdictions (e.g., EU-West-1 for GDPR).  
*   **Rationale:** Mandatory standard for global Tier-1 holding companies to comply with international data protection laws (GDPR, CCPA).

**NFR-010: Automated PII Scrubbing**  
*   **Target:** The system MUST asynchronously scrub identifiable Personally Identifiable Information (PII) from the user's `intent_text` before persistence in the MongoDB `audit_logs` collection.  
*   **Rationale:** Minimizes legal exposure for the agency by preventing the unintentional collection and long-term storage of user or consumer data in system logs.

### 5. Usability & Agentic Governance

**NFR-011: Dashboard Accessibility**  
*   **Target:** The React + TypeScript administrative dashboard MUST comply with WCAG 2.1 Level AA standards.  
*   **Rationale:** Ensures all administrators and legal personnel can manage governance workflows regardless of visual or motor impairments, meeting standard enterprise procurement requirements.

**NFR-012: Agentic Guardrails**  
*   **Target:** The CrewAI Orchestrator MUST terminate any individual agentic reasoning chain that exceeds 10 discrete processing steps or 60 seconds of compute time, returning an "Orchestration Complexity Error" to the user.  
*   **Rationale:** Prevents system hangs and token-cost spiraling caused by LLM hallucination loops during the prompt-rewriting phase.

## Edge Cases

### 1. DRM & Contract Lifecycles

**EC-001: Contract Expiration During Generation Queue**
*   **Scenario:** An Account Manager triggers a high-fidelity render. The Creator’s TTL contract is valid at the millisecond the Slack command is issued, but due to queue depth, the synthesis call occurs 4 hours later, by which time the contract has expired.
*   **System Behavior:** The Production Orchestrator Agent MUST perform a secondary, synchronous `TTL Check` against MongoDB Atlas immediately *before* dispatching the payload to the synthesis API. If expired, it MUST abort and post a "Contract Expired During Queue" error to Slack.

**EC-002: Retroactive Contract Revocation**
*   **Scenario:** Legal revokes a creator's likeness rights in the React Dashboard while a video link is currently sitting with a Client for review.
*   **System Behavior:** The FastAPI endpoint serving the External Approval Web View MUST query the MongoDB TTL database on every page load. If revoked, the endpoint MUST return a 403 Forbidden page, immediately invalidating all previously generated secure links for that creator.

**EC-003: DRM Database Unavailability**
*   **Scenario:** The MongoDB Atlas cluster is unreachable or the query times out while checking creator likeness rights.
*   **System Behavior:** The system MUST "fail-closed." In the absence of a confirmed valid contract (e.g., a connection timeout), the CrewAI Platform & DRM Agent MUST halt the workflow and return a "Compliance System Unavailable: Generation Paused for Security" message to the initiating user.

### 2. Budget & Quota Management

**EC-004: Race Conditions on Budget Quotas**
*   **Scenario:** A workspace has 1 high-fidelity render remaining. Two users click "Approve & Render" at the exact same millisecond.
*   **System Behavior:** MongoDB Atlas MUST use optimistic locking (via versioning) on the `workspace_budgets` document. Only the first transaction will succeed; the second will fail the version check, and FastAPI will return a "Quota Exceeded: Concurrent request detected" message.

**EC-005: Generation/Persistence Failure Post-Quota Deduction**
*   **Scenario:** A user requests a render; the system deducts "1" from the quota. Subsequently, the synthesis API crashes, or the final MP4 fails to upload to the persistent storage layer.
*   **System Behavior:** The CrewAI orchestrator MUST wrap the synthesis and storage process in a transactional saga. If any step fails (5xx error or upload timeout), a compensatory transaction MUST execute to refund the "1" render to the workspace quota before notifying the user.

### 3. Agentic Behavior & Brand Safety

**EC-006: Hallucinated "Safe" Alternatives**
*   **Scenario:** The Trend Agent rewrites a script to avoid a restricted keyword but hallucinates and changes the factual nature of the product announcement (e.g., changing "Summer" to "Winter").
*   **System Behavior:** The system MUST surface both the *original* user intent and the *Agent-rewritten* script side-by-side in the Slack thread using Block Kit diff-formatting. The user MUST explicitly click a "Confirm Script" button before synthesis, ensuring a human-in-the-loop catches semantic hallucinations.

**EC-007: Stalemates Between Agents**
*   **Scenario:** The Trend Agent and DRM Agent enter a continuous rejection loop (e.g., Brand ToV requires a term that Legal has blacklisted).
*   **System Behavior:** The CrewAI Orchestrator MUST terminate the loop after 10 iterations (as per NFR-012). The system will post a "Stalemate Error" to Slack, displaying the conflicting rules to the user and requiring a manual prompt rewrite.

**EC-008: Initial Input Token Overflow**
*   **Scenario:** A user inputs an excessively long intent script which, when combined with system prompts, exceeds the LLM context window.
*   **System Behavior:** FastAPI MUST perform a pre-flight token count check. If the payload exceeds 80% of the model's limit, it MUST reject the request immediately with an ephemeral Slack message: "Payload too large. Please shorten your intent."

### 4. Asynchronous UX & Distributed Integrity

**EC-009: Concurrent Editing in Slack Threads**
*   **Scenario:** While Sarah is typing a "Revise" instruction, another user clicks "Approve & Render" on her draft.
*   **System Behavior:** FastAPI MUST lock the state of a generation thread to the user who initiated the command. If another user attempts an action, Slack will return an ephemeral warning: "Only the initiator (@Sarah) can approve or revise this draft."

**EC-010: Deep Thread Context & Token Limits**
*   **Scenario:** A user iterates 25 times in one thread; the historical context window exceeds the LLM's limit.
*   **System Behavior:** The system MUST implement a rolling context window, passing only the *original intent* and the *last 3 revision instructions* to the CrewAI payload. At iteration 20, the Block Kit message SHALL include a warning to start a new command for better results.

**EC-011: Orphaned Task Cleanup**
*   **Scenario:** A user deletes the Slack message thread or the channel is archived while agents are mid-processing.
*   **System Behavior:** FastAPI MUST listen for Slack `message_deleted` and `channel_archive` events. Upon receipt, it SHALL broadcast a `SIGTERM` to the specific background worker process associated with that `thread_ts` to prevent runaway API costs.

**EC-012: Cross-Stakeholder Approval Conflict**
*   **Scenario:** Sarah (Internal) clicks "Approve & Render" in Slack, but David (External Client) simultaneously clicks "Reject" via the web preview link before the high-fidelity render starts.
*   **System Behavior:** The system MUST treat the external stakeholder's "No-Go" as the terminal overriding state. FastAPI MUST intercept the conflict, cancel the rendering task, refund the workspace quota, and post David's rejection comments to the Slack thread, disabling the original "Approve & Render" button.

## Error Handling

### 1. Validation & Input Errors

**ERR-001: Malformed Slack Slash Command**
*   **Trigger:** Invalid platform enum (e.g., `[instagram]`) or creator handle regex failure in the initial Slack command.
*   **System Handling:** FastAPI intercepts the request prior to CrewAI invocation.
*   **User-Facing Output:** Ephemeral Slack message: "❌ **Invalid Input**: Please use `[tiktok, reels]` and a valid `@creator`. Example: `/avatar-gen [tiktok] @creatorX 'Hello!'`"
*   **Logging:** MongoDB `audit_logs`: `Validation_Error_400`.

**ERR-002: Context Window / Payload Overflow**
*   **Trigger:** User intent text + Tone of Voice (ToV) instructions exceed the LLM's maximum token limits.
*   **System Handling:** FastAPI pre-flight token count check fails; request is dropped.
*   **User-Facing Output:** Ephemeral Slack message: "⚠️ **Payload Too Large**: Your script combined with brand guidelines exceeds limits. Please shorten your intent."
*   **Logging:** MongoDB `audit_logs`: `Token_Limit_Exceeded_413`.

### 2. Governance & Policy Enforcements (Hard-Blocks)

**ERR-003: Creator Contract (TTL) Expired**
*   **Trigger:** `expiration_date` < `current_date` in the MongoDB Atlas creator collection.
*   **System Handling:** Immediate `SIGTERM` to the agentic pipeline process. The rendering API is bypassed.
