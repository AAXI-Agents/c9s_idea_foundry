## Executive Summary

**Problem Statement**  
Current AI video generators produce static, commodity content that completely fails to address the severe operational friction marketing agencies face regarding multi-tenant brand compliance, multi-tiered stakeholder approvals, and human-in-the-loop (HITL) error correction. This missing orchestration layer forces enterprise marketing teams into bottlenecked, manual workflow loops that erode profit margins, risk brand safety, and delay campaign execution.

**Target Audience & Key Stakeholders**  
*   **VPs of Social Media Strategy / Agency Directors (Primary):** Suffer from profit margin erosion due to manual edit cycles and slow operational velocity; their primary goal is scaling output while proving concrete ROI.
*   **Creative Directors (Secondary):** Struggle with brand voice consistency and the risk of generating static, "commodity" AI outputs; they require precise control over final brand aesthetics. 
*   **Technical Ops Leads (Secondary):** Tasked with the complex management of multi-tenant agent configurations, system prompts, and ensuring verifiable consent for sensitive assets like voice clones.
*   **Client Stakeholders (External):** Require frictionless, immediate visibility into final content for sign-off without needing to adopt or learn new enterprise software.

**Proposed Solution**  
"Agentic Studio" is an enterprise-grade B2B MarTech platform operating as an autonomous, scalable video content pipeline. The solution bypasses standalone utility AI by leveraging a **CrewAI multi-agent architecture** (Brand Strategist, Copywriter, Post-Production, and Orchestrator agents) to autonomously generate, optimize, and assemble dynamic social video content. 

The core architecture strictly separates concerns: heavy binary media files (MP4s, raw audio) are hosted in a scalable cloud object storage bucket (e.g., AWS S3), while **MongoDB Atlas** serves exclusively as the flexible document store for metadata pointers, multi-tenant brand configurations, user profiles, and audit trails.

The workflow relies on strict state transitions: *Generated -> Internal Review -> Client Review -> Published*. Agency teams manage profiles via a secure **React + TypeScript** frontend. Here, a strict "verifiable consent" gate ensures compliance: users must pass a multi-factor approval—uploading identity verification (e.g., signed PDF agreement) coupled with an asynchronous voice-matching confirmation recorded through the frontend—before a 'Voice Clone' status transitions to 'Active' in MongoDB.

To eliminate review bottlenecks, a high-performance **FastAPI** backend powers an asynchronous, multi-tiered Slack approval workflow via interactive Block Kit messages. If an automated render fails brand standards, users click a "Request Edit" button in Slack that deep-links to the React-based HITL Advanced Editor at the exact timestamp of the error. Crucially, closing the workflow loop, once edits are saved in the React frontend, the system automatically updates the asset state from 'Editing' back to 'Internal Review' and triggers a new FastAPI webhook payload to push an updated Block Kit review message to the internal Slack channel.

**Technical Constraints & System Fallbacks**  
*   **Performance Scale:** The FastAPI and CrewAI pipeline must successfully process up to 50 concurrent video rendering jobs with a P95 generation latency of <120 seconds. 
*   **Data Isolation:** Multi-tenant data isolation must be enforced at the database level via MongoDB Atlas partitioning.
*   **Verifiable Audit Trail:** The MongoDB interaction history tracking Slack approvals and edits must be immutable, enforced via a strict append-only/write-only collection pattern.
*   **Error Fallbacks:** In the event of agent hallucination, SSML generation failure, or Slack API downtime, the system will execute a graceful fallback by automatically triggering an email notification to assigned stakeholders with a secure direct link to the React dashboard.

**Key Business Value & Expected Impact**  
*   **Operational Velocity:** Shifting approvals entirely to an asynchronous Slack workflow will reduce end-to-end content approval turnaround time to under 5 minutes from the completion of the video render.  
*   **Airtight Compliance:** Guarantees 0 compliance rejections from social platforms through automated C2PA (Coalition for Content Provenance and Authenticity) metadata injection and the append-only MongoDB audit trail.  
*   **Agentic Efficiency:** Success will be measured internally by tracking the "Intervention-to-Generation Ratio," targeting a human intervention rate of <15% within 90 days of launch.
*   **Proven ROI:** To prove campaign value, programmatic injection of Dynamic Creative Optimization (DCO) metadata and UTM parameters is embedded securely within the C2PA file header and applied as optional visual overlays (lower-thirds), enabling frictionless A/B testing downstream.

**Risks & Dependencies**  
*   **Storage & API Dependencies:** The system relies on third-party cloud object storage (S3) for asset hosting and LLM provider uptime (OpenAI/Anthropic) for CrewAI logic. *Mitigation:* FastAPI circuit breakers and local, lightweight model fallbacks will maintain basic drafting capabilities during external LLM outages.
*   **Regulatory Shifts:** Social platform AI labeling requirements change frequently. *Mitigation:* C2PA injection logic will be built as a decoupled microservice, allowing for rapid codec and metadata updates without requiring full core platform redeployment.

**Timeline & Phasing Indication**  
*   **Phase 1 (Months 1-2) - Core Pipeline:** Deployment of the React + TypeScript configuration hub, AWS S3 integration, MongoDB Atlas schema design (append-only audit trails), verifiable consent gating, and foundational CrewAI workflows.  
*   **Phase 2 (Months 3-4) - Orchestration & Fallbacks:** Implementation of the FastAPI-driven Slack approval routing, state re-entry logic from the HITL Advanced Editor, error-handling fallbacks, and C2PA header metadata injection.  
*   **Phase 3 (Month 5) - Optimization & Launch:** Finalization of DCO metadata injection, concurrent load stress-testing (50 job threshold), and MVP beta rollout.

## Problem Statement

**Current State**  
The generative AI video landscape is heavily saturated with standalone, utility-driven tools (e.g., avatar generators, text-to-speech engines) designed primarily for single-user, single-brand use cases. While these tools successfully lower the barrier to generating raw video files, they fail entirely to address the operational realities of B2B digital marketing agencies. Currently, when an agency attempts to scale AI video production across a multi-tenant client portfolio, the actual generation of the video accounts for less than 10% of the lifecycle. The remaining 90% is consumed by a fragmented, highly manual content supply chain involving isolated editing software, decoupled email/Slack approval chains, and manual metadata tagging for ad platforms. The industry treats AI video as a commodity output rather than an orchestrated workflow.

**Core Pain Points**  
Based on industry analysis and continuous feedback from VP-level social strategy and operational leads, marketing agencies suffer from four critical systemic frictions:

*   **Multi-Tenant Brand Safety Risks & HITL Disconnect:** Generative AI natively hallucinates. It mispronounces niche brand names, ignores visual safe zones, and hallucinates visual artifacts. Currently, standalone generator tools act as "black boxes" lacking programmatic control over Speech Synthesis Markup Language (SSML). Consequently, when a phonetic error occurs or an AI video violates a brand guideline, the Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) correction requires abandoning the generation tool entirely. The user must download the asset, edit it in a traditional NLE (Non-Linear Editor), or attempt a full manual re-recording. This disconnect nullifies the speed advantages of AI and entirely strips away the "AI intelligence layer"—turning a dynamic, data-rich output into a static file that cannot be programmatically iterated upon for future variations.
*   **Approval Bottlenecks & "Latency Stacking":** Enterprise stakeholder reviews are multi-tiered (Internal Creative Director -> External Client -> Compliance). Currently, agencies route raw MP4 files via Google Drive links or chaotic Slack threads. Feedback is disconnected from the asset timeline, causing severe version control issues. Furthermore, this fragmented routing introduces "Latency Stacking"—where every manual handoff between isolated tools adds 10 to 15 minutes of idle time to the workflow, severely delaying time-to-market.
*   **The "Dumb Asset" ROI Gap:** Standard AI tools export static MP4 files stripped of context. They do not natively support programmatic Dynamic Creative Optimization (DCO) metadata or UTM parameter injection. As a result, media buyers are forced to manually tag and structure hundreds of hook variations in Meta or TikTok Ads Manager, making downstream A/B testing and ROI attribution highly labor-intensive and prone to human error.
*   **Unverifiable Compliance, Configuration Sprawl, & Audit Liabilities:** The enterprise use of verified voice clones and digital avatars carries immense legal and reputational risk. Managing 10+ different client prompt libraries, system instructions, and voice-matching keys across fragmented tools creates a "configuration sprawl" that inevitably leads to human error in brand-voice mapping. Furthermore, major social platforms are aggressively enforcing AI labeling policies. Agencies lack an automated way to inject cryptographic C2PA (Coalition for Content Provenance and Authenticity) disclosure metadata into their exports. Worse, they lack immutable audit trails proving exactly which user approved a specific iteration of a synthetic asset, exposing agencies to severe regulatory fines (e.g., FTC actions) and platform bans.

**Impact Quantification**  
The absence of an orchestrated pipeline translates directly into measurable business losses:
*   **Margin Erosion:** Agencies currently spend an average of 45 to 60 minutes on manual edits, stakeholder routing, and platform tagging for a single 30-second AI-generated video. This creates a highly skewed labor-to-asset cost ratio (e.g., spending $50 in agency labor to process a video that cost only $2 in AI compute). At scale (e.g., 500+ videos per month across 10 clients), this manual overhead devours agency profit margins and limits the volume of content a strategist can manage.
*   **Client Churn Risk:** A single unapproved brand violation (e.g., a mispronounced core product name) or a compliance strike from a social platform due to missing AI disclosures severely damages agency-client trust. Agency internal data suggests up to 15% of client churn is linked to preventable brand-safety violations or delays in campaign turnaround. For an average agency, the loss of a 3-client portfolio due to these operational failures equates to a massive loss in Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR).

**Why Now? (Market Timing & Urgency)**  
Two converging market forces make solving this operational gap urgent. First, the algorithmic shift on platforms like TikTok, Reels, and YouTube Shorts demands an unprecedented, high-velocity volume of hyper-personalized, A/B-tested content—a volume impossible to achieve with manual production workflows. Second, regulatory frameworks and platform policies surrounding deepfakes and AI provenance are transitioning from optional best practices to strict mandates. Agencies must adopt an auditable, compliant, and deeply integrated AI workflow today, or risk being completely priced out of the market by faster competitors while simultaneously risking severe platform penalties and FTC fines for their clients.

## Functional Requirements

### Epic 1: Multi-Tenant Brand Configuration & Verifiable Consent

**FR-001: Multi-Tenant Brand Profile Provisioning**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The React + TypeScript frontend MUST allow users to create and manage multi-tenant brand profiles. The system SHALL store these profiles in MongoDB Atlas using tenant-specific document partitioning to ensure strict data isolation.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   *Given* an authenticated user with the `Tech_Ops` or `Agency_Director` role is logged into the React frontend,
    *   *When* they submit a new brand configuration (typography, safe zones, tone guidelines),
    *   *Then* the system MUST create a distinct tenant record in MongoDB Atlas and return a unique `tenant_id`.
*   **API Contract:**
    *   **Endpoint:** `POST /api/v1/tenants`
    *   **Request Schema:** `{"name": "string", "brand_guidelines": {"typography": "string", "safe_zones": "object", "tone": "string"}}`
    *   **Response Schema:** `201 Created` | `{"tenant_id": "uuid", "status": "active"}`

**FR-002: Verifiable Consent Gating for Voice Clones**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The system SHALL enforce a Multi-Factor Approval (MFA) gate before activating any synthetic voice asset. A voice clone asset MUST remain in a `Pending` state until identity verification is complete.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   *Given* a user attempts to upload a source audio file for voice cloning via the React frontend,
    *   *When* they upload the audio file but do NOT provide both a signed PDF agreement AND an asynchronous frontend-recorded voice match,
    *   *Then* the MongoDB document state MUST remain `Pending` and the CrewAI agents SHALL NOT have access to the clone.
    *   *When* both verification artifacts are successfully submitted and validated by an Admin (`Tech_Ops`),
    *   *Then* the system updates the state to `Active`.
*   **API Contract:**
    *   **Endpoint:** `POST /api/v1/tenants/{tenant_id}/voice-clones/verify`
    *   **Request Schema:** `multipart/form-data` (Files: `pdf_agreement`, `voice_match_audio`)
    *   **Response Schema:** `200 OK` | `{"asset_id": "uuid", "verification_state": "Active"}`

### Epic 2: CrewAI Multi-Agent Video Generation Pipeline

**FR-003: Autonomous Multi-Agent Orchestration & SSML Validation**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The FastAPI backend SHALL trigger a CrewAI workflow where the Orchestrator Agent assigns tasks to the Brand Strategist (Context), Copywriter (Scripting), and Post-Production (Assembly) agents based on a strictly typed JSON payload to prevent AI hallucination.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   *Given* a validated campaign brief,
    *   *When* the generation is triggered,
    *   *Then* the system MUST pass a structured JSON object to the Brand Strategist containing `voice_profile_id`, `visual_safe_zones`, and mandatory creative boundaries (`core_message`, `cta`, `duration_seconds`, `style`).
    *   *And* the Copywriter MUST generate platform-specific SSML.
    *   *And* the system MUST validate the generated SSML syntax against the rendering engine's schema to prevent render crashes before passing it to the Post-Production agent.
    *   *And* the Post-Production agent MUST assemble the S3-hosted raw assets into a composed video file.
*   **API Contract:**
    *   **Endpoint:** `POST /api/v1/generations/kickoff`
    *   **Request Schema:** 
        ```json
        {
          "tenant_id": "uuid",
          "target_platforms": ["tiktok", "reels"],
          "brief": {
            "core_message": "string",
            "cta": "string",
            "duration_seconds": 30,
            "style": "cinematic|fast-paced"
          }
        }
        ```
    *   **Response Schema:** `202 Accepted` | `{"job_id": "uuid", "status": "processing", "webhook_url": "string"}`

**FR-004: Dynamic Creative Optimization (DCO) Metadata Injection**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The Post-Production Agent SHALL programmatically inject DCO tags and UTM parameters into the final video assembly process as standardized XMP data to guarantee downstream platform ingestion.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   *Given* a video render is in the final assembly stage,
    *   *When* UTM and DCO parameters are provided in the campaign brief,
    *   *Then* the agent MUST embed these parameters securely as a JSON object within the exported MP4's XMP (Extensible Metadata Platform) headers.
    *   *And* the agent MAY generate optional lower-third visual overlays reflecting the hook variation.

### Epic 3: Asynchronous Slack Approval State Machine

**FR-005: Internal Slack Routing & Asynchronous Acknowledgment**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The FastAPI backend SHALL route generated assets to a pre-configured internal Slack channel using interactive Block Kit messages. The system MUST acknowledge Slack interaction webhooks asynchronously to prevent Slack API timeout errors.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   *Given* the CrewAI pipeline completes a render,
    *   *When* the asset state updates to `Internal Review`,
    *   *Then* FastAPI MUST post a Block Kit message containing a watermarked video preview, "Approve for Client", and "Request Edit" buttons.
    *   *When* a user clicks a button,
    *   *Then* FastAPI MUST return a `200 OK` or `202 Accepted` to Slack within 3 seconds, queuing the actual state processing asynchronously.
*   **API Contract:**
    *   **Endpoint:** `POST /slack/interactions`
    *   **Request Schema:** Standard Slack Block Actions payload.
    *   **Response Schema:** `200 OK` (Empty body to ACK Slack)

**FR-006: Multi-Tiered Client Review Handoff**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The system SHALL manage the state transition from Internal to Client review without requiring manual file transfers.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   *Given* an asset is in `Internal Review` state,
    *   *When* an authorized user (e.g., `Creative_Director`) clicks "Approve for Client" in the internal Slack channel,
    *   *Then* the system MUST update the MongoDB asset state to `Client Review` and push a new Block Kit message (with "Approve" and "Reject" buttons) to the configured Slack Connect channel for external review.

### Epic 4: Human-In-The-Loop (HITL) Advanced Editor Workflow

**FR-007: Timestamp-Aware Deep Linking**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** When an edit is requested via Slack, the system SHALL generate a secure, temporary deep-link to the React Advanced Editor, initialized at the specific timestamp of the error.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   *Given* a reviewer clicks "Request Edit" in Slack and inputs a timestamp (e.g., "00:15") into the resulting Slack modal,
    *   *When* the modal is submitted,
    *   *Then* the system MUST generate a signed URL and reply to the Slack thread with a direct link to the React editor, loaded exactly at 00:15.

**FR-008: SSML and Asset State Reconciliation**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The React frontend MUST allow modification of SSML and B-roll pointers. Upon saving, the system SHALL automatically re-enter the approval state machine.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   *Given* a user modifies SSML text or swaps an S3 B-roll pointer in the HITL Advanced Editor,
    *   *When* the user clicks "Save & Re-Render",
    *   *Then* the system MUST transition the asset state from `Editing` back to `Internal Review`.
    *   *And* the system MUST trigger a FastAPI webhook payload to post an updated Block Kit review message to the original Slack thread.
*   **API Contract:**
    *   **Endpoint:** `PATCH /api/v1/assets/{asset_id}/edit`
    *   **Request Schema:** `{"ssml_overrides": [{"timestamp": "float", "new_ssml": "string"}], "b_roll_swaps": [{"timestamp": "float", "s3_key": "string"}]}`
    *   **Response Schema:** `200 OK` | `{"job_id": "uuid", "new_state": "Internal Review"}`

### Epic 5: Compliance Logging & C2PA Export Validation

**FR-009: Immutable Append-Only Audit Trail**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The system SHALL record all asset state changes, Slack interactions, and HITL edits in a dedicated, append-only MongoDB collection.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   *Given* any state transition or Slack button click occurs,
    *   *When* the backend processes the event,
    *   *Then* it MUST write an immutable document to the `audit_logs` collection containing the `user_id`, `timestamp`, `action`, `previous_state`, and `new_state`.
    *   *And* the system SHALL NOT expose any HTTP methods (e.g., DELETE, PUT) that allow modification of existing audit log documents.

**FR-010: C2PA Metadata Injection on Publish**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** Prior to the final file export, the FastAPI backend SHALL invoke a decoupled microservice to inject cryptographic C2PA provenance metadata into the final MP4 asset.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   *Given* an asset state transitions from `Client Review` to `Published`,
    *   *When* the final MP4 is compiled for delivery,
    *   *Then* the system MUST inject the C2PA header containing the agency identifier, AI generation disclosure, and DCO parameters before uploading the final asset to the designated 'Published' S3 bucket.
*   **API Contract:**
    *   **Endpoint:** `POST /api/v1/assets/{asset_id}/publish`
    *   **Request Schema:** `{"approved_by": "uuid", "slack_thread_ts": "string"}`
    *   **Response Schema:** `200 OK` | `{"final_s3_url": "string", "c2pa_verified": true}`

### Epic 6: Security, Role-Based Access, & Resilience Guardrails

**FR-011: Persona-Based Authorization Gates (RBAC)**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The system SHALL enforce strict Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) checks on all state-transitioning endpoints based on the mapped User Persona type (`Tech_Ops`, `Agency_Director`, `Creative_Director`, `Client_Stakeholder`).
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   *Given* a user with the `Client_Stakeholder` role (e.g., Elena),
    *   *When* they attempt to call the `/api/v1/tenants` provisioning endpoint or internal HITL endpoints,
    *   *Then* the system MUST return a `403 Forbidden` error.
    *   *Given* a user with the `Creative_Director` role (e.g., Marcus),
    *   *When* they click "Approve for Client" in the internal Slack channel,
    *   *Then* the system MUST validate their role and permit the state transition to `Client Review`.

**FR-012: Graceful Multi-Channel Fallback**
*   **Priority:** SHOULD
*   **Description:** In the event of an external dependency outage (e.g., Slack API limits/downtime or LLM provider timeouts), the system SHALL execute a graceful fallback to ensure workflows are not permanently stalled.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   *Given* a video render has successfully reached the `Internal Review` state,
    *   *When* the FastAPI system attempts to push the Block Kit payload but receives a timeout or `5xx` error from the Slack API,
    *   *Then* the system MUST trigger an SMTP-based notification email to the assigned `Creative_Director`.
    *   *And* the email MUST contain a secure, direct link to review the asset within the React dashboard, bypassing the Slack dependency.

**FR-013: Client Rejection Loopback Logic**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** If a Client Stakeholder rejects an asset via the external Slack Connect channel, the system SHALL systematically route it back for internal correction while logging mandatory feedback.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   *Given* an asset is currently in the `Client Review` state,
    *   *When* a user clicks the "Reject" button in Slack and submits the mandatory feedback modal,
    *   *Then* the system MUST immediately transition the asset state back to `Internal Review`.
    *   *And* the system MUST append the rejection reason to the immutable MongoDB `audit_logs` collection and trigger a notification alerting the assigned `Creative_Director` of the requested changes.
*   **API Contract:**
    *   **Endpoint:** `POST /api/v1/assets/{asset_id}/reject`
    *   **Request Schema:** `{"rejected_by": "uuid", "reason": "string"}`
    *   **Response Schema:** `200 OK` | `{"new_state": "Internal Review", "logged": true}`

**FR-014: Concurrent Job Queue Management**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The FastAPI backend SHALL enforce a strict concurrency limit on active CrewAI/Rendering jobs to guarantee performance SLA targets and prevent system resource exhaustion.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   *Given* the system currently has 50 rendering jobs in the `Processing` state,
    *   *When* a 51st generation request is received via the API,
    *   *Then* the system MUST queue the job in a `Queued` state rather than executing it immediately.
    *   *And* the system MUST automatically transition the oldest `Queued` job to `Processing` only when an active slot becomes available.

## Non-Functional Requirements

### 4.1 Performance & Scalability

*   **NFR-001: Video Rendering Throughput (Concurrency)**
    *   **Description:** The FastAPI backend and CrewAI orchestration pipeline MUST concurrently process up to 50 active video rendering jobs across the multi-tenant architecture without queuing delays or dropping requests.
    *   **Measurement:** Load testing MUST demonstrate 0 dropped requests when 50 concurrent `POST /api/v1/generations/kickoff` requests are executed simultaneously.
*   **NFR-002: Generation Latency (P95)**

A "standard request" is strictly defined as a video under 60 seconds in duration rendered at 1080p/30fps resolution.
    *   **Measurement:** System telemetry MUST log a P95 generation latency of <120 seconds measured over a rolling 7-day period.

    *   **Measurement:** Automated penetration testing MUST confirm that queries authenticated under Tenant A cannot retrieve documents, prompts, or S3 pointers belonging to Tenant B.
*   **NFR-005: Immutable Audit Logging**
    *   **Description:** To satisfy FTC and platform compliance audits regarding synthetic media generation, all user interactions, approvals, and HITL edits MUST be recorded in an append-only MongoDB collection.

    *   **Measurement:** 100% of files transitioning to the `Published` state MUST pass structural validation for C2PA headers using the standard CAI (Content Authenticity Initiative) SDK prior to final S3 upload.
*   **NFR-012: Encryption of Sensitive Assets**
    *   **Description:** Identity verification documents (signed PDFs) and biometric voice source files MUST be encrypted both in transit and at rest due to their high-risk PII nature.
    *   **Measurement:** 100% of S3 objects residing in the `/identity-verification/` and `/voice-clones/` prefixes MUST be encrypted at rest using AES-256 with unique, tenant-scoped AWS KMS keys. Transit MUST be secured via TLS 1.3.
*   **NFR-014: Sensitive Data Retention Limits**
    *   **Description:** To mitigate biometric and PII liability, raw identity verification artifacts MUST NOT be stored indefinitely.
    *   **Measurement:** The system MUST execute a scheduled purge of raw PDF and voice-match files from AWS S3 within 24 hours of successful verification by the `Tech_Ops` admin. Only the immutable verification status and a cryptographic hash of the event SHALL remain in MongoDB.

*   **NFR-009: Slack API Fallback Routing**

## Edge Cases

### 5.1 Concurrency & Race Conditions

*   **EC-001: Concurrent Slack Approvals on a Single Asset**
    *   **Scenario:** Multiple authorized stakeholders simultaneously click "Approve for Client" on the same interactive Block Kit message.
    *   **System Behavior:** The FastAPI backend MUST utilize optimistic concurrency control within MongoDB Atlas. The first request successfully updates the document state and increments the document version. The second request, detecting a version mismatch, MUST return an ephemeral message in Slack stating: "This asset has already been approved by [User Name]."
*   **EC-002: Edit Request During Active Generation State**
    *   **Scenario:** A user rapidly clicks a stale "Request Edit" deep link in Slack for an asset already sent back into the `Generated` (rendering) state by another user's previous edit.
    *   **System Behavior:** The React frontend MUST query the MongoDB asset state upon load. If the state is `Generated` (locked), the HITL Advanced Editor MUST render in read-only mode, disabling SSML and B-roll modifications, and display a banner indicating: "This asset is currently rendering. Edits are locked until completion."
*   **EC-003: Concurrent HITL Edits in React Dashboard**
    *   **Scenario:** Two Creative Directors simultaneously open the React dashboard and attempt to save overlapping modifications to the same SSML script block.
    *   **System Behavior:** The `PATCH /api/v1/assets/{asset_id}/edit` endpoint MUST require a `version_id` parameter. If the version in MongoDB has mutated since the frontend session was initialized, the FastAPI backend MUST reject the save action with a `409 Conflict` HTTP status. The frontend MUST then prompt the user to refresh the asset and re-apply their edits to prevent silent data overwrites (last-write-wins anomaly).

### 5.2 State Contradictions & Workflow Interruptions

*   **EC-004: Revocation of Voice Clone Consent Mid-Render**
    *   **Scenario:** A `Tech_Ops` admin revokes the verifiable consent (transitioning a voice clone from `Active` to `Pending`) while the CrewAI Post-Production agent is actively rendering a batch of videos using that specific clone.
    *   **System Behavior:** The CrewAI Orchestrator Agent MUST check the voice clone's validation status in MongoDB at the exact initiation of the audio-generation step for *each* video. If consent is found to be revoked mid-batch, the Orchestrator MUST instantly abort the remaining queued jobs, update their state to `Failed: Consent Revoked`, and trigger a FastAPI webhook to notify the requester via Slack.
*   **EC-005: Client Rejection Without Context (Empty Payload)**
    *   **Scenario:** A Client Stakeholder clicks "Reject" in the external Slack channel but attempts to bypass validation by closing the mandatory Block Kit modal or submitting an empty text field.
    *   **System Behavior:** The FastAPI `/slack/interactions` endpoint MUST reject the payload if the `reason` string is empty or under 5 characters. The MongoDB asset state MUST remain locked in `Client Review`, and the Slack bot MUST push an ephemeral error to the client stating: "A written reason is required to reject an asset."
*   **EC-011: Post-Render Consent Revocation**
    *   **Scenario:** A `Tech_Ops` admin revokes a voice clone's consent while previously rendered assets utilizing that clone are sitting in the `Internal Review` or `Client Review` states.
    *   **System Behavior:** When a voice clone transitions to `Pending`, the system MUST cascade this state change. FastAPI MUST immediately query MongoDB for any un-published assets utilizing that clone ID. The system MUST invalidate the signed S3 access URLs for those specific assets, transition their document state to `Suspended`, and push an automated Slack notification to the assigned `Creative_Director` indicating a brand-safety lock.

### 5.3 Data Anomalies & Boundary Conditions

*   **EC-006: AI Hallucination: SSML Exceeds Video Duration Limits**
    *   **Scenario:** The Copywriter Agent hallucinates an excessively long script, generating SSML that results in a 90-second audio track for a campaign brief strictly constrained to 30 seconds.
    *   **System Behavior:** Before passing the payload to the render engine, the Orchestrator Agent MUST validate the estimated audio duration. If the duration exceeds the defined brief limit by more than 10%, the Orchestrator MUST autonomously reject the draft, append the failure to the MongoDB audit log, and force the Copywriter Agent to re-draft the SSML up to a maximum of 3 retry attempts before falling back to a human notification.
*   **EC-007: Extreme B-Roll Resolution Swap**
    *   **Scenario:** In the HITL Advanced Editor, a Creative Director swaps a standard 1080p S3 B-roll pointer with an 8K raw uncompressed video file, creating a massive memory spike.
    *   **System Behavior:** The React frontend MUST intercept the save action by validating the target S3 object metadata. If the file pointer references a file exceeding 4K resolution or 1GB in total size, the frontend MUST block the `PATCH` request and prompt the user: "B-roll asset exceeds maximum resolution/size limits. Please select an optimized proxy file."
*   **EC-008: C2PA Metadata Payload Bloat**
    *   **Scenario:** A campaign brief contains an unusually high number of DCO tags and UTM parameters, causing the generated JSON object injected into the MP4's C2PA XMP header to exceed the 64KB byte limit.
    *   **System Behavior:** The FastAPI C2PA microservice MUST calculate the byte size of the payload prior to file injection. If it exceeds 64KB, the system MUST automatically truncate non-essential DCO tags (prioritizing the retention of core UTM source/medium tags) and append a warning to the MongoDB `audit_logs` collection.

### 5.4 Infrastructure & API Constraints

*   **EC-009: Render-to-S3 Upload Timeout**
    *   **Scenario:** The CrewAI Post-Production agent successfully completes a large video render, but the FastAPI worker node experiences a network timeout while attempting to upload the final binary file to the AWS S3 `Published` bucket.
    *   **System Behavior:** The system MUST NOT transition the MongoDB asset state to `Internal Review` without a valid, resolvable S3 URI. FastAPI MUST utilize a retry policy with exponential backoff (maximum 3 attempts). If all attempts fail, the state MUST transition to `Failed: Storage Timeout`, and the `audit_logs` MUST securely record the local file path of the orphaned render to allow for manual recovery by a `Tech_Ops` admin.
*   **EC-010: Slack Block Kit Payload Size Exceeded**
    *   **Scenario:** A generated video contains an extensive AI-generated description, causing the resulting review payload to exceed Slack's hard 3,000-character Block Kit limit, which would otherwise result in a silent API rejection.
    *   **System Behavior:** Prior to dispatching the webhook, the FastAPI backend MUST validate the string length of the message payload. If it exceeds 2,500 characters, the backend MUST truncate the description text, append an ellipsis `(...)`, and rely on the securely shortened S3 deep-link URL to ensure the Slack API returns a `200 OK`.
*   **EC-012: Mid-Flow Agent Resumption**
    *   **Scenario:** The multi-agent workflow successfully completes Stage 1 (Brand Strategist) and Stage 2 (Copywriter), but the Stage 3 Post-Production render engine crashes due to an infrastructure outage.
    *   **System Behavior:** To conserve LLM API tokens and reduce latency stacking, the FastAPI orchestration layer MUST implement task-level checkpointing in MongoDB Atlas. Upon system recovery, the workflow MUST NOT restart from Stage 1; it MUST retrieve the saved `task_output` from Stage 2 and directly resume the Stage 3 Post-Production task.

## Error Handling

### 6.1 CrewAI & LLM Integration Errors

*   **ERR-001: LLM Provider Timeout (504 Gateway Timeout)**
    *   **Trigger:** External LLM provider fails to respond to a CrewAI agent within 10 seconds.
    *   **System Action:** FastAPI triggers a circuit breaker. Workflow falls back to a lightweight, locally hosted LLM (e.g., Mistral-7B) to maintain basic drafting capabilities.
    *   **User Notification:** System appends a `Fallback used` flag to the asset metadata. The React dashboard displays a flag to the `Creative_Director`: "Generated using fallback model due to external outage. Review carefully."
    *   **Logging:** `WARN` in MongoDB `audit_logs` with latency measurement and prompt snapshot.

*   **ERR-002: Agent Hallucination / Format Violation (400 Bad Request)**
    *   **Trigger:** Agent generates SSML that violates the rendering engine's syntactical schema.
    *   **System Action:** Orchestrator Agent intercepts payload, extracts the schema error, and reprompts the agent. Maximum of 3 retries. **Token Protection Constraint:** If the retry loop consumes more than 3x the initial token estimate for the task, the Orchestrator MUST freeze the job to prevent margin erosion.
    *   **User Notification:** If retries fail or the token limit is breached, asset state = `Failed`. Email alert to `Technical_Ops_Lead` with a direct link to the prompt configuration in the React dashboard.
    *   **Logging:** `ERROR` in MongoDB, capturing the hallucinated payload, token usage count, and validation traceback.

### 6.2 FastAPI & Slack Orchestration Errors

*   **ERR-003: Slack Webhook Delivery Failure (5xx / 429 Rate Limit)**
    *   **Trigger:** FastAPI attempts to push Block Kit message; Slack returns an error or rate limit.
    *   **System Action:** Exponential backoff retry (1s, 5s, 15s). Terminal failures route the payload to a Dead Letter Queue (DLQ).
    *   **User Notification:** For internal channel failures, notify the `Creative_Director` via email. **For external Slack Connect failures (Client Review), the system MUST notify the `Agency_Director` ONLY via email**, rather than the client, to preserve a frictionless external user experience and allow the agency lead to manage client communications.
    *   **Logging:** DLQ event recorded in MongoDB with Slack error payload.

*   **ERR-004: Stale Slack Interaction Timeout**
    *   **Trigger:** User clicks a button on an asset whose state has already mutated (version mismatch).
    *   **System Action:** FastAPI rejects the state transition based on the MongoDB `version_id`.
    *   **User Notification:** Ephemeral Slack message: "Action failed: The state of this asset has changed since this message was posted."
    *   **Logging:** `INFO` log in audit trail tracking rejected concurrency.

*   **ERR-009: API Gateway / Rate Limit Exhaustion (429 Too Many Requests)**
    *   **Trigger:** System exceeds the LLM provider's tier-rate limits or FastAPI receives a 429 from a supporting microservice.
    *   **System Action:** Implement a "Jittered Linear Backoff" strategy. The Orchestrator MUST pause the task queue for the specific tenant causing the spike for 60 seconds before retrying.
    *   **User Notification:** Internal dashboard status updates to "Throttled: Resuming in 60s."
    *   **Logging:** Logged as `INFO` with the `tenant_id` and header `Retry-After` value.

### 6.3 React Frontend & Asset Processing Errors

*   **ERR-005: Verifiable Consent Validation Failure (403 Forbidden)**
    *   **Trigger:** User attempts to activate a voice clone without mandatory identity artifacts.
    *   **System Action:** Endpoint rejects payload; asset remains `Pending` (agent-inaccessible).
    *   **User Notification:** Red banner in React: "Consent Verification Failed. Signed PDF and voice-match recording are mandatory."
    *   **Logging:** Failed attempt permanently logged in append-only audit trail for compliance.

*   **ERR-006: C2PA Metadata Injection Failure (500 Internal Server Error)**
    *   **Trigger:** C2PA microservice fails to inject cryptographic headers during the transition to `Published`.
    *   **System Action:** Halt S3 upload to the 'Published' bucket. Transition the asset state to `Publishing Halted` (an internal-only sub-state of `Internal Review`).
    *   **User Notification:** 
        *   **External:** No notification sent (preserves client trust).
        *   **Internal:** Critical alert to `Technical_Ops_Lead` and `Agency_Director` via Slack/Dashboard indicating compliance gate failure.
    *   **Logging:** Logged as `CRITICAL` in MongoDB, capturing the codec/header error.

### 6.4 Persistence & Storage Errors

*   **ERR-007: S3 Object Storage Timeout (504 Gateway Timeout)**
    *   **Trigger:** FastAPI worker times out while streaming MP4 to S3.
    *   **System Action:** Automatic retry via multipart upload protocol. Terminal failure marks job as `Failed: Storage Timeout`.
    *   **User Notification:** React status: "Render complete, but storage failed. Technical team notified."
    *   **Logging:** Local file path of orphaned render recorded in `audit_logs` for manual `Tech_Ops` recovery without LLM re-generation costs.

*   **ERR-008: Database Persistence Failure (503 Service Unavailable)**
    *   **Trigger:** MongoDB Atlas connection timeout or cluster unavailability during an audit log write or state transition.
    *   **System Action:** The FastAPI worker node MUST redirect state changes and audit logs to an encrypted local JSON buffer (SSD). Each entry MUST include a nanosecond-precision `client_side_timestamp` and a `sequence_id`. The system enters a "Degraded" mode.
    *   **User Notification:** Dashboard banner for internal users: "System in Degraded Mode. State changes are being queued."
    *   **Logging:** The local buffer is monitored by a background task and is flushed via a FIFO (First-In-First-Out) queue to MongoDB Atlas immediately upon restoration of the database heartbeat, preserving append-only integrity.

## Dependencies

### 9.1 Technical Infrastructure & Storage

*   **MongoDB Atlas (Data Persistence):** The system relies entirely on MongoDB Atlas as the primary document store for all state transitions, append-only audit trails, and multi-tenant brand configurations. **Requirement:** The selected cluster tier MUST support "Change Streams" to enable the required real-time state updates for the React dashboard.
*   **AWS S3 (Blob Storage):** The system depends on highly scalable object storage to host the heavy binary media files (raw video assets, B-roll, MP4 renders). **Requirement:** To meet the <120s P95 generation latency constraint (NFR-002), the S3 buckets and the FastAPI rendering worker nodes MUST reside in the same AWS Region (e.g., `us-east-1`) and utilize VPC S3 Endpoints to minimize inter-region data transfer latency.
*   **MongoDB Atlas Vector Search:** The system utilizes MongoDB's native Vector Search capability for the CrewAI Brand Strategist agent to retrieve contextual brand guidelines via RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation). **Constraint:** This intentionally removes the dependency on external vector databases (like Pinecone), keeping AI context strictly local to the primary persistence layer for security.
*   **Local Fallback Hosting:** The system depends on isolated, dedicated GPU compute resources (either on-demand cloud instances or reserved on-premise hardware) to host the lightweight local LLM (e.g., Mistral-7B) required for the API circuit breaker fallback (NFR-008).
*   **Identity Processing Engine:** The system depends on a robust OCR and document parsing service (e.g., AWS Textract) to extract and validate signature fields from the "signed PDF" artifacts uploaded during the Verifiable Consent gating process (FR-002).

### 9.2 Third-Party APIs & Services

*   **Slack API (Orchestration Interface):** The core human-in-the-loop workflow has a hard dependency on Slack's Block Kit UI framework and Events API. **Constraint:** The system requires a dedicated, approved enterprise Slack App with specific OAuth scopes enabled (`chat:write`, `interactivity`).
*   **LLM Providers (Cognitive Engine):** The CrewAI multi-agent architecture depends on external foundation models (e.g., OpenAI GPT-4o or Anthropic Claude 3.5 Sonnet). **Requirement:** API accounts MUST be provisioned at "Tier 3" or higher to guarantee the rate limits necessary to support 50 concurrent generation jobs without throttling.
*   **AI Synthesis APIs (Audio & Video):** The actual synthesis relies on specialized third-party APIs (e.g., ElevenLabs for voice cloning, HeyGen for avatar rendering). **Requirement:** These providers MUST support asynchronous Webhook callback payloads to notify the FastAPI backend upon render completion, ensuring the main FastAPI threads are not blocked during long render cycles.
*   **Email Service Provider (SMTP):** The system depends on an enterprise email delivery service (e.g., AWS SES or SendGrid) to successfully execute the graceful fallback notifications required when Slack or the UI is unreachable.

### 9.3 Regulatory & Platform Standards

*   **C2PA SDK & Signing Identity:** The metadata microservice depends on the open-source Coalition for Content Provenance and Authenticity (C2PA) library (e.g., `c2pa-rs` or Python bindings). Crucially, the system depends on a partnership with a trusted Certificate Authority (CA) and a secure Key Management Service (AWS KMS) to hold the private keys required to digitally sign the exported MP4 manifests.
*   **Platform Ad-Manager APIs:** The business goal of "Proven ROI" through automated DCO/UTM injection depends entirely on the schema stability of downstream social platforms (Meta, TikTok Ads Manager) regarding how they ingest, read, and surface XMP metadata from uploaded synthetic creatives.

| **Synthesis APIs (e.g., ElevenLabs)** | Rendering Bottleneck / Timeout | Medium | High | Jobs are strictly batched and processed in parallel up to the 50-job limit. The React UI displays a real-time "Rendering Progress" state to manage user expectations. |

| **C2PA Microservice / SDK** | Codec Conflict or Format Rejection | Medium | Medium | Built as a decoupled microservice, allowing rapid, isolated updates to the metadata injection logic without requiring a full redeployment of the core FastAPI rendering platform. |

### 9.5 Cross-Functional & Operational

*   **Media Buying Teams (Downstream):** The realization of the DCO metadata ROI goal depends on the agency's human media buyers correctly mapping the injected DCO tags when constructing A/B tests. **Mitigation:** The Agency Director persona (Sarah) will receive a weekly "Metadata Utilization Report" dashboard view to actively audit media buyer compliance.

If this operational dependency is delayed, user onboarding velocity will stall.

### 10.1 Business & User Assumptions

*   **A-001: Slack Connect Ubiquity:** We assume that the target external persona (Client Stakeholders) and internal personas (Creative Directors, Tech Ops) utilize Slack as their primary daily communication tool, and that their respective organizations permit the use of "Slack Connect" channels for cross-organization workflow routing.
*   **A-002: Documented Brand Guidelines:** We assume that enterprise marketing agencies possess formalized, quantifiable brand guidelines (e.g., exact hex codes, defined tone-of-voice descriptors, strict visual safe-zone measurements) that can be translated into structured parameters within the MongoDB multi-tenant profiles. If guidelines are purely subjective, the AI agents will fail to execute accurately.
*   **A-003: Value of DCO over Static Assets:** We assume that the media buying teams downstream from this product value Dynamic Creative Optimization (DCO) metadata and UTM parameters enough to utilize them in ad platforms, validating the "Proven ROI" business goal. 
*   **A-004: Tolerable Latency:** We assume that a P95 generation latency of <120 seconds is an acceptable waiting period for the internal agency teams before the first Slack Block Kit review notification is triggered.
*   **A-012: Fallback Accessibility:** We assume that in cases where Slack Connect is prohibited by strict client IT security policies, the SMTP email fallback (NFR-009) provides a frictionless enough UX to maintain the overarching <5 minute turnaround target.

### 10.2 Technical & Architectural Assumptions

*   **A-005: RAG and Context Accuracy:** We assume that the MongoDB Atlas Vector Search semantic retrieval (RAG) mechanism is precise enough to provide the CrewAI agents with the specific subset of brand guidelines required for a task, without introducing hallucinations due to contradictory or overwhelming context chunks.
*   **A-006: Asynchronous System Viability:** We assume that the FastAPI asynchronous polling system will successfully manage the multi-threaded load of 50 concurrent video rendering jobs without dropping webhook payloads or experiencing memory exhaustion on the worker nodes.
*   **A-007: Append-Only Efficacy:** We assume that utilizing MongoDB Atlas with strict database-level restrictions to create an append-only/write-only `audit_logs` collection will satisfy the legal and compliance requirements for "immutable audit trails" without requiring a blockchain or distributed ledger technology.
*   **A-008: XMP Metadata Ingestion:** We assume that standard social ad platforms (Meta, TikTok Ads Manager) and standard video players will natively read and respect the C2PA XMP metadata injected into the MP4 headers by the FastAPI backend, rather than aggressively stripping it upon upload or compression.

### 10.3 Legal & Operational Assumptions

*   **A-009: Legality of Voice Cloning & Biometric Accuracy:** We assume that the agency holds the legal right to synthesize an individual's voice, provided the "signed PDF agreement" and "asynchronous voice-matching confirmation" are passed. Furthermore, we assume the voice-matching engine has a near-zero False Acceptance Rate (FAR) that satisfies legal evidentiary standards, and that a 24-hour data purge policy satisfies biometric privacy laws (e.g., Illinois BIPA, GDPR).
*   **A-010: Manual Intervention Floor:** We assume that generative AI is not yet capable of 100% zero-shot accuracy for enterprise brands, validating the core architectural necessity of the React-based HITL (Human-in-the-Loop) Advanced Editor and the Slack fallback routing.
*   **A-011: Regulatory Consistency:** We assume that the C2PA standard will remain the dominant, accepted framework for AI provenance labeling by major social media platforms during the initial rollout phases.
