# Product Requirements Document

## Executive Summary

## Executive Summary

**Problem Statement**
In the hyper-competitive MarTech and Creator Economy sectors, adapting personalized short-form video content across disparate social channels creates a severe operational bottleneck. Marketing teams and social media managers are forced into agonizing manual workflows, requiring constant context-switching across isolated AI generation tools (like HeyGen and ElevenLabs) to manually adapt a single core message to the distinct cultural norms, aspect ratios, and pacing of various platforms. 

**Target Audience**
The primary users are digital agencies, enterprise marketing teams, and high-volume social media managers who manage multi-channel brand presences and require highly scalable, compliant, and localized video production.

**Proposed Solution**
The CrewAI-Orchestrated Multi-Channel Video Avatar Platform operates as an autonomous virtual production studio. Initiated as a highly strategic Slack-based Minimal Viable Product (MVP), the platform allows users to create personalized avatars by uploading 5-10 reference photos and a voice sample. Users submit a single text concept via Slack, prompting a multi-agent CrewAI pipeline to dynamically generate platform-optimized scripts for TikTok, YouTube Shorts, Threads, and X. The system enforces strict Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) integration via Slack Block Kit: users review and approve the cheaply iterated Gemini scripts directly in Slack. Only upon explicit approval does the system trigger the expensive asynchronous voice and video rendering pipelines.

**Key Differentiators**
* **Workflow Unification:** Completely eliminates context-switching by abstracting complex multi-tool video generation behind intuitive, asynchronous Slack commands.
* **Enterprise Compliance & IP Management:** Implements strict Likeness Revocation protocols within the MongoDB Atlas data layer, allowing administrators to instantly purge employee or influencer reference media and voice clones upon their departure.
* **Copyright-Safe Metadata Extraction:** Utilizes yt-dlp to analyze trending reference videos for structural metadata (pacing, hook frameworks, keyword density) stored in an Obsidian-backed knowledge graph, immediately discarding raw media to prevent copyright infringement.
* **Dynamic Contextual Personalization:** Injects platform-specific "Mood Parameters" into API payloads, dynamically adjusting the avatar’s voice pitch and micro-expressions to match the unique energetic or professional culture of the target platform.
* **Pre-Flight Visual Validation:** Employs Clip + ViT integration to validate the framing, lighting, and resolution of user-uploaded reference images before they reach HeyGen, dramatically reducing avatar modeling failure rates.

**Business Value & Success Criteria**
By offloading execution to a dedicated CrewAI workforce, the platform transforms a grueling production chore into a seamless conversational workflow. The core business success metric is reducing the manual effort required for multi-channel video creation from an average of **four hours down to under fifteen minutes** of active human input. Technically, the system's decoupled FastAPI and RabbitMQ event-driven architecture must achieve a **sub-5% failure rate** across all third-party API orchestrations, supported by intelligent dead-letter queue retries.

**Timeline & Phasing**
Phase 1 focuses entirely on delivering the Slack-based MVP to capture immediate B2B adoption within existing client approval environments. To ensure rapid time-to-market and control costs, the MVP intentionally excludes complex spatial video editing (e.g., drag-and-drop cropping), relying instead on predefined, high-quality rendering templates. Phase 2 will expand the platform into a full-fledged React and TypeScript web application once the underlying agentic orchestration and data layers are proven at scale.

## Problem Statement

**The Current State of Multi-Channel Content Production**
Digital agencies and enterprise marketing teams currently operate as manual clearinghouses for AI-generated assets. To maintain a presence on platforms like TikTok, YouTube Shorts, Threads, and X, social media managers must navigate a highly fragmented ecosystem. The workflow requires manually daisy-chaining prompts and data across isolated LLMs for script generation, ElevenLabs for voice synthesis, and HeyGen for video rendering. Because this process is entirely browser-based, teams are forced to constantly context-switch between collaborative workspaces (like Slack or email, where approvals happen) and isolated production dashboards. This disconnect leads to severe "data siloing," where the rationale for a specific creative direction is lost during the transition between tools, and approvals are significantly delayed.

**Key Pain Points**
*   **The "Context-Switching Tax" & Fragmented Orchestration:** Operating as a manual integrator across multiple AI applications creates a high cognitive load. Small user errors—such as applying the wrong voice profile to a script or failing to adjust the aspect ratio for X versus TikTok—often go unnoticed until the final render, necessitating a complete, manual restart of the production pipeline and a loss of momentum.
*   **"Garbage-In, Garbage-Out" Financial Waste:** Without automated pre-flight visual checks, users frequently upload low-resolution, poorly framed, or improperly lit reference photos. In current workflows, these flaws are only detected *after* the expensive, asynchronous video rendering process is complete. Since most AI video providers charge non-refundable credits per render attempt, this results in significant financial waste and lost production hours.
*   **The Platform Literacy Barrier & Ephemeral Insights:** Native "moods" and cultural hooks vary wildly across social graphs. Manually adapting a single core message to the high-energy pacing of TikTok versus the text-centric nature of X requires deep platform literacy. Currently, "trending" knowledge is ephemeral; insights gained from one viral video are rarely captured or structured, forcing managers to "guess" at successful hook frameworks for every new campaign rather than relying on a persistent creative intelligence system.
*   **Unmanaged IP & Compliance Vulnerabilities:** Relying on disconnected SaaS tools provides organizations with no centralized "kill switch" for brand assets. If an influencer or employee terminates their contract, administrators face a likeness management nightmare, struggling to identify and manually purge reference media and voice clones stored across disparate third-party accounts. Furthermore, manual competitive research often involves the risky local downloading and storage of copyrighted media just to analyze pacing and hooks.

**Impact Quantification**
*   **Operational Stagnation:** The manual orchestration required to produce, QA, and render a single multi-channel campaign currently consumes an average of **four hours of active human input** (including script iteration, asset synchronization, and error correction).
*   **Linear Scaling Limitation:** Content production capacity is currently tied 1:1 to headcount. Because of the sheer complexity of the manual tool-chain, agencies cannot increase content volume without a proportional increase in expensive social media manager roles, effectively destroying the potential ROI of AI-assisted production.

**Why Now?**
As AI video quality successfully crosses the "uncanny valley," the competitive advantage in the Creator Economy has shifted entirely from the ability to *generate* content to the ability to *orchestrate* it at scale. With "content velocity" now serving as the primary algorithmic signal for market dominance, marketing teams that remain trapped in manual, browser-based workflows will be rendered obsolete by competitors utilizing agentic orchestration to iterate, adapt, and publish at machine speed.

## Functional Requirements

### **1. Workspace & Account Initialization**

**FR-001: Slack App Installation & Authentication**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The system shall allow users to install the application into their Slack workspace via standard OAuth v2 flow, automatically provisioning an organizational account in MongoDB Atlas linked to the installing user's persistent email/Gmail account.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   Given a user clicks the Slack install link, When they authorize the application, Then an organizational record is created in MongoDB (`organization_id`) and the Slack bot is added to the workspace.

**FR-002: Organizational Media Channel Configuration**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The system shall allow administrators to create and manage "Media Channels" via a Slack Slash command or a lightweight configuration modal. A Media Channel represents a specific brand presence and maps to a predefined list of target platforms (TikTok, YouTube Shorts, Threads, X).
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   Given an authenticated admin creates a channel, When they define the "Target Platforms," Then the record in MongoDB MUST include the `client_id` and `organization_id` foreign keys to ensure strict multi-tenant data isolation.

### **2. Avatar Onboarding & Validation**

**FR-003: Asynchronous Media Upload (Images)**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The system shall accept an upload of 5 to 10 reference photos via a Slack thread interface to model a personalized video avatar.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   Given a user initiates the `/avatar-onboard` command, When they upload a batch of 5-10 images, Then the FastAPI webhook ingests the images and stores them in a transient `onboarding_bucket` for validation.

**FR-004: Pre-Flight Visual Validation (Clip + ViT)**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The system shall evaluate uploaded reference images using Clip + ViT to validate framing, lighting, and resolution before passing data to the HeyGen API.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   Given an uploaded image batch, When validation runs, Then any image with a resolution < 512x512px or a CLIP face-detection confidence score < 0.7 is rejected, and the user receives specific feedback in Slack.

**FR-005: Voice Sample Ingestion**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The system shall accept an audio file upload via Slack to be used as the foundational voice clone in ElevenLabs.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   Given a validated image batch, When the user uploads an MP3/WAV file, Then the system routes the file to ElevenLabs to generate a Voice ID, linking it to the user's Avatar Profile in MongoDB.

### **3. Agentic Content Pipeline**

**FR-006: Concept Ingestion & Intent Classification**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The system shall accept a raw text prompt (concept) via Slack, immediately returning a 202 Accepted response to prevent Slack timeout, while triggering the CrewAI background pipeline via RabbitMQ.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   Given a user types `/avatar-generate [Text Concept]`, When submitted, Then Slack immediately displays "Processing...", and an event is published to RabbitMQ.

**FR-007: Competitive Metadata Extraction**
*   **Priority:** SHOULD
*   **Description:** The system shall utilize `yt-dlp` via a CrewAI agent to temporarily analyze trending reference videos matching keywords, extract structural metadata (pacing formulas, hook frameworks) into an Obsidian knowledge graph, and immediately discard the raw media files.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   Given a generation trigger, When the research agent runs, Then hook metadata is saved to the knowledge graph, and all local `.mp4` files are purged within the same execution cycle.

**FR-008: Channel-Optimized Script Variants**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The system shall utilize Gemini within a CrewAI flow to generate two distinct creative script variants for each platform (TikTok, YouTube Shorts, Threads, X) based on the concept and the Obsidian knowledge graph.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   Given a validated concept, When the script agent completes, Then the user is presented with two distinct "Creative Directions" (e.g., Educational vs. Hype) per target platform to choose from.

### **4. Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) Workflow**

**FR-009: Isolated Interactive Script Review**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The system shall present generated scripts via Slack Block Kit. For cross-organizational approvals (Agency to Enterprise), requests shall route securely via Slack Connect, ensuring the Enterprise Approver only sees the final drafted request.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   Given the scripts are generated and internally vetted by the Operator, When posted to the Client Approval channel, Then the Approver sees buttons for "Approve," "Refine," and "Cancel" securely mapped to their specific `organization_id`.

**FR-010: Conversational Refinement**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The system shall support iterative refinement within the Slack thread. If a user rejects a script and provides feedback, Gemini will iterate on that specific script without restarting the entire pipeline.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   Given a user clicks "Refine" and provides feedback, When processed, Then the system returns an updated script variant in the thread for re-approval.

### **5. Asynchronous Rendering & Delivery**

**FR-011: Voice & Video API Orchestration**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The system shall trigger ElevenLabs (audio) and HeyGen (video) APIs *only* after explicit HITL approval of the scripts via Slack.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   Given an approved script, When the user clicks "Approve & Render," Then the system dispatches the render job to RabbitMQ.

**FR-012: Dynamic Mood Parameter Injection**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The system shall dynamically inject Mood Parameters into API payloads via a configurable schema in MongoDB (e.g., pitch, speed, and expression per platform).
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   Given a render job for TikTok, When the API payload is constructed, Then the system includes the TikTok-specific pitch and micro-expression values retrieved from the database schema rather than relying on hard-coded variables.

**FR-013: Final Asset Delivery via Slack**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The system shall post the final rendered video files back to the originating Slack thread upon completion.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   Given a completed render webhook from HeyGen, When received, Then the FastAPI server posts the video file/link directly to the user's Slack thread.

### **6. Compliance & System Management**

**FR-014: Centralized Likeness Revocation**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The system shall provide a mechanism to instantly purge an Avatar Profile's biometric data from the system and all third-party APIs (HeyGen, ElevenLabs).
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   Given a revocation command is executed, When processed, Then all reference photos and voice clones are permanently deleted from MongoDB and the third-party providers.

**FR-015: Resiliency & DLQ Alerting**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The system shall monitor the RabbitMQ Dead Letter Queue (DLQ) and third-party API response codes for operational observability.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   Given a rendering failure (e.g., HeyGen API timeout), When retry logic is exhausted, Then the system MUST post a formatted error summary and DLQ alert to the designated Admin/Ops Slack Channel.

## Non-Functional Requirements

### 1. Performance & Latency

**NFR-001: Synchronous Slack Response Latency**
*   **Target:** < 2,500 milliseconds (p99).
*   **Description:** To comply with Slack's mandatory Event API webhook timeout, the FastAPI backend MUST acknowledge incoming user commands and return an immediate `HTTP 200 OK` (along with a "Processing..." visual indicator) in under 3 seconds. All heavy AI processing MUST be strictly offloaded to the asynchronous RabbitMQ queue.

**NFR-002: Agentic Pipeline Processing Latency**
*   **Target:** < 45 seconds (p90) from prompt submission to script presentation.
*   **Description:** The CrewAI pipeline (comprising yt-dlp metadata extraction, Obsidian knowledge retrieval, and Gemini script generation) MUST generate and surface the initial Block Kit script drafts to the user for HITL approval in under 45 seconds to maintain conversational momentum.

**NFR-003: Total Asynchronous Fulfillment Latency**
*   **Target:** < 15 minutes (p90) from HITL approval to final asset delivery.
*   **Description:** While the rendering process is external and asynchronous, the orchestration layer MUST monitor job status. If the combined ElevenLabs + HeyGen rendering cycle exceeds 20 minutes, the system MUST trigger a "Delayed Fulfillment" notification to the user via Slack and log the event as a performance degradation.

**NFR-004: Video Rendering Throughput**
*   **Target:** Support up to 50 concurrent video rendering jobs per tenant.
*   **Description:** The RabbitMQ architecture MUST be capable of dispatching and tracking up to 50 simultaneous asynchronous rendering requests to the HeyGen and ElevenLabs APIs without exhausting local server thread pools.

### 2. Reliability & Resilience

**NFR-005: System Availability & Recovery**
*   **Target:** 99.9% Uptime; RTO < 4 hours; RPO < 1 hour.
*   **Description:** The FastAPI backend and RabbitMQ orchestration layer MUST maintain 99.9% availability. On-call engineering MUST be alerted automatically via PagerDuty (or equivalent) if the Slack bot fails to respond to heartbeats for > 5 minutes.

**NFR-006: Pipeline Failure Rate (SLA)**
*   **Target:** < 5% failure rate across all third-party API orchestrations.
*   **Description:** The complete end-to-end pipeline (from text prompt to final video delivery) MUST successfully complete 95% of the time. This accounts for transient network errors, API rate limits, and LLM timeouts.

**NFR-007: Rate Limit Orchestration**
*   **Target:** 0% failure rate due to external 429 errors.
*   **Description:** The RabbitMQ dispatcher MUST implement "Leaky Bucket" rate-limiting logic. Requests to ElevenLabs/HeyGen MUST be throttled at the system level to match provider tiers, ensuring that no tenant can monopolize global API capacity and cause "noisy neighbor" failures for others.

**NFR-008: Asynchronous Retry & Dead Letter Queue (DLQ)**
*   **Target:** 3 automated retries with exponential backoff.
*   **Description:** If a third-party API (Gemini, HeyGen, or ElevenLabs) times out or returns a 5xx error, the RabbitMQ worker MUST automatically retry the payload up to three times using exponential backoff (e.g., 2s, 4s, 8s). If the third retry fails, the message MUST be routed to a Dead Letter Queue (DLQ) for manual inspection, and the user MUST be notified via Slack.

**NFR-009: Graceful LLM Degradation**
*   **Target:** 100% fallback to default templates upon yt-dlp/Obsidian failure.
*   **Description:** If the yt-dlp trending metadata extraction fails or times out, the Gemini script generation agent MUST gracefully degrade by falling back to static, predefined platform hook templates rather than failing the entire script generation process.

### 3. Security, Compliance & Data Privacy

**NFR-010: Biometric Data Encryption at Rest**
*   **Target:** 100% of biometric assets encrypted via AES-256.
*   **Description:** All reference photos, processed avatar models, and voice clone samples stored in MongoDB Atlas or cloud buckets MUST be encrypted at rest using AES-256. Encryption keys MUST be rotated annually and managed via a dedicated Secret Manager.

**NFR-011: Strict Multi-tenant Context Isolation**
*   **Target:** 100% logical isolation of agent memory.
*   **Description:** The CrewAI "Memory" and "Knowledge" components MUST be keyed by `organization_id`. The system MUST prevent any cross-tenant data leakage at the application layer, ensuring that Gemini/CrewAI never utilizes reference data or script history from one organization to inform the generation of another.

**NFR-012: Content Safety Guardrails**
*   **Target:** 0% bypass of LLM safety filters.
*   **Description:** The system MUST strictly enforce Gemini’s built-in safety filters for hate speech, harassment, and dangerous content. Any prompt or generated script violating these parameters MUST be blocked before reaching the HITL approval stage.

**NFR-013: Likeness Revocation SLA**
*   **Target:** < 5 minutes from admin trigger to global purge.
*   **Description:** When an administrator initiates a "Likeness Revocation" protocol, the system MUST cascade the delete commands through MongoDB, the ElevenLabs Voice API, and the HeyGen Avatar API, fully purging the target biometric data within 5 minutes.

**NFR-014: Copyright-Safe Media Ephemerality**
*   **Target:** 0 seconds of persistent `.mp4` storage.
*   **Description:** Any third-party video media downloaded by the yt-dlp agent for competitive analysis MUST be processed in memory or on a volatile RAM disk. Raw video files MUST be immediately and permanently purged from the system the moment structural metadata is extracted. Under no circumstances should copyrighted third-party media be persisted to MongoDB or cloud storage.

**NFR-015: Tenant-Level API Cost Guardrails**
*   **Target:** 0% budget overruns per organization.
*   **Description:** The system MUST implement a "Circuit Breaker" for costs. Administrators MUST be able to set a daily/monthly dollar cap on third-party API spend. The RabbitMQ dispatcher MUST verify credit availability before triggering HeyGen/ElevenLabs payloads.

**NFR-016: API Key Management & Secret Injection**
*   **Target:** 100% of third-party API keys stored securely.
*   **Description:** Raw API keys for external services (HeyGen, ElevenLabs, Gemini) MUST never be hardcoded or logged. They MUST be managed via a secure secrets manager and injected into the FastAPI/CrewAI workers at runtime as environment variables.

**NFR-017: Configurable Data Residency**
*   **Target:** 100% adherence to regional deployment configurations.
*   **Description:** To support enterprise compliance, the MongoDB Atlas clusters and FastAPI deployment regions MUST be configurable (e.g., EU-West, US-East) to strictly support regional data residency requirements specified during organization onboarding.

### 4. Data Quality & Observability

**NFR-018: Image Validation Strictness (Clip + ViT)**
*   **Target:** 0% bypass rate for the validation gateway.
*   **Description:** The pre-flight visual validation system MUST be rigidly enforced. No user-uploaded image batch can be forwarded to the HeyGen modeling API unless every image passes the minimum ViT lighting heuristics and Clip resolution checks. Failed images MUST halt the workflow and prompt the user for replacements.

**NFR-019: Observability & Audit Logging**
*   **Target:** 90-day retention for all system interactions.
*   **Description:** All system events—including Slack command invocations, HITL approvals, LLM prompt inputs/outputs, and third-party API response codes—MUST be logged to MongoDB. These logs MUST be structured to allow for API cost attribution per user and LLM fine-tuning data extraction. No raw PII or biometric media should be included in these text logs.

### 5. Accessibility

**NFR-020: Accessibility Targets**
*   **Target:** WCAG 2.1 Level AA Compliance.
*   **Description:** All final video outputs SHOULD include auto-generated, burn-in captions (standard for short-form video). The future Phase 2 Web Interface MUST strictly adhere to WCAG 2.1 Level AA to support enterprise-level accessibility requirements.

## Edge Cases

### 1. Asynchronous Workflow & State Edge Cases

**EC-001: Mid-Render Likeness Revocation**
*   **Scenario:** A user triggers a video render for Avatar A. While the job is processing asynchronously in the HeyGen/ElevenLabs queue, an administrator executes the Likeness Revocation protocol for Avatar A.
*   **Impact:** A video could be delivered featuring an employee who has just had their consent revoked, creating a severe legal compliance breach.
*   **Expected System Behavior:** The Likeness Revocation protocol MUST emit a high-priority "kill" signal via RabbitMQ. The system MUST perform a selective "Deep Purge": it MUST instantly cancel any pending or actively processing jobs associated with the revoked `avatar_id` in the third-party APIs. It MUST permanently delete all biometric assets (raw media, identifying hashes) from MongoDB and `safe_harbor_logs`, and discard transient RabbitMQ messages in the "Retry" state. However, to maintain NFR-019 (Audit Logging) integrity, non-biometric event metadata (timestamp, user_id, credit_cost) MUST be retained, replacing the `avatar_id` with a `REVOKED_IDENTITY` string. Any "Soft-Reserved" credits associated with cancelled jobs MUST be instantly rolled back to the organization's available balance.

**EC-002: "Stale" Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) Approvals**
*   **Scenario:** The system generates a script and posts it to Slack via Block Kit. The user takes 48 hours to click "Approve." In the interim, the underlying ElevenLabs or HeyGen API keys have been rotated, or the user's monthly budget limit has been reached.
*   **Impact:** The system attempts to process an approved script but fails immediately due to outdated context, creating unresolvable Dead Letter Queue (DLQ) loops.
*   **Expected System Behavior:** Slack Block Kit approval buttons MUST have an enforced Time-to-Live (TTL) of 12 hours. If clicked after the TTL, the system MUST reject the action, dynamically update the Slack message to read "Approval Window Expired," and prompt the user to re-generate the script using current system state/budgets.

**EC-003: Race Condition on Concurrent Script Refinements**
*   **Scenario:** A user generates a script in a Slack thread. Two different users in that same thread (e.g., an Agency Manager and an Enterprise Director) click the "Refine" button simultaneously and submit conflicting feedback to Gemini.
*   **Impact:** The CrewAI flow receives conflicting context, potentially causing the LLM to hallucinate a merged, nonsensical script.
*   **Expected System Behavior:** The system MUST implement optimistic locking on the specific `script_draft_id` in MongoDB. The first user to click "Refine" locks the draft state. If a second user attempts to submit a refinement before the first is processed, they receive a Slack ephemeral message stating: "Another user is currently refining this script. Please wait."

**EC-004: Post-Trigger Credit Exhaustion**
*   **Scenario:** An organization has enough third-party API credits for exactly one video. Two users approve separate renders simultaneously via Slack. Both pass the initial gateway check. User A's job completes first, draining the balance to zero.
*   **Impact:** User B's job completes in HeyGen, but the system cannot settle the transaction or proceed, leading to data inconsistency and orphaned renders.
*   **Expected System Behavior:** The system MUST implement a "Soft-Reserve" lock in MongoDB the moment an "Approve & Render" button is clicked. The estimated credit cost is moved to a "Pending" state. Any subsequent checks for other users MUST subtract these pending credits from the available total, rejecting the second trigger with an "Insufficient Funds" Slack alert before hitting the external API.

**EC-005: Partial Batch Fulfillment**
*   **Scenario:** A user approves a multi-channel render. The TikTok and X jobs succeed, but the YouTube Shorts render fails due to a transient provider error.
*   **Impact:** The user is left wondering if any content was created, potentially triggering a duplicate full-batch render and wasting credits.
*   **Expected System Behavior:** The RabbitMQ orchestrator MUST track the status of each platform in the batch independently. As webhooks return successfully, the system MUST post the individual videos to the Slack thread incrementally. If a specific platform fails, the system MUST post a specific error message for that platform (e.g., "Shorts render failed") alongside a Block Kit button to "Retry Shorts Only."

**EC-006: Divergent Creative Direction Selection**
*   **Scenario:** Alex is presented with two creative directions for TikTok and two for X. He approves the "Educational" variant for TikTok but the "Hype" variant for X in the same interaction cycle.
*   **Impact:** Potential context mismatch in the final render payload, leading to cross-platform script leakage where the wrong audio track is applied to a video.
*   **Expected System Behavior:** The system MUST treat every creative direction as an independent state object in MongoDB. The "Approval" interaction payload MUST include a strict platform-to-direction mapping (e.g., `{"tiktok": "uuid_A", "X": "uuid_B"}`). The RabbitMQ worker MUST strictly validate that the rendering prompt sent to HeyGen/ElevenLabs matches the direction UUID approved for that specific social platform.

### 2. Media Ingestion & API Boundary Conditions

**EC-007: Audio Clone Duration Violation**
*   **Scenario:** The user provides a text script that Gemini successfully optimizes for YouTube Shorts. However, the resulting generated audio from ElevenLabs is 63 seconds long, exceeding the strict 60-second limit for YouTube Shorts.
*   **Impact:** The final video is rejected by the target social media platform upon upload, breaking the "platform-optimized" promise.
*   **Expected System Behavior:** The system MUST intercept the ElevenLabs audio payload before passing it to HeyGen. If the `audio_duration` exceeds the hard limits of the target platform (e.g., 60s for Shorts), the system MUST halt the pipeline, notify the user in Slack that the audio exceeds limits, and offer an AI-shortened version of the script to approve.

**EC-008: Obfuscated or Synthetic Image Uploads**
*   **Scenario:** A user attempts to bypass the "personal avatar" constraint by uploading 5-10 images of a 3D-rendered character or a heavily deepfaked illustration.
*   **Impact:** HeyGen’s API terms of service prohibit synthetic avatars under certain tiers, potentially leading to immediate account suspension and a platform-wide outage.
*   **Expected System Behavior:** The Clip + ViT pre-flight validation MUST include a liveness/synthetic-detection heuristic. If the confidence score for "synthetic/AI-generated" exceeds 80%, the batch MUST be rejected with a Slack notification stating: "Only real human photographic reference images are currently supported."

**EC-009: Voice Biometric SNR Validation**
*   **Scenario:** A user uploads a 30-second voice sample recorded in a noisy environment (e.g., a crowded airport).
*   **Impact:** The ElevenLabs Voice ID becomes "polluted" with background noise, making all future videos sound unprofessional and robotic.
*   **Expected System Behavior:** The FastAPI onboarding pipeline MUST include an automated audio analysis step. If the Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR) is below 15dB or if digital clipping is detected in >10% of the sample, the system MUST reject the file before sending it to ElevenLabs and prompt the user to "Record in a quiet environment" with a visual noise-floor guide in Slack.

**EC-010: yt-dlp "Zero Metadata" Returns**
*   **Scenario:** A user submits a niche B2B concept prompt. The CrewAI agent triggers yt-dlp to scrape reference videos, but returns zero relevant videos or videos with no extractable pacing data.
*   **Impact:** The Obsidian knowledge graph receives a null payload, causing the Gemini script generator to fail or hallucinate.
*   **Expected System Behavior:** The CrewAI orchestrator MUST recognize the null return from the research agent. It MUST immediately fallback to generic, pre-approved structural templates stored in MongoDB (e.g., "Standard B2B Problem/Solution Hook") to ensure the user still receives a high-quality draft without delay.

### 3. Interface & Platform Boundary Conditions

**EC-011: Slack Thread Context Limits**
*   **Scenario:** A user utilizes the "Conversational Refinement" feature heavily, replying to the Slack thread 25 times to tweak a script.
*   **Impact:** The conversational context payload sent to the Gemini API exceeds its maximum token window, resulting in a hard crash.
*   **Expected System Behavior:** The `SlackInterpretMessageTool` MUST implement a rolling window context strategy. If the thread exceeds 10 refinement iterations, the system MUST summarize the previous context into a compressed "state prompt" before appending the newest user request, preserving the creative direction while staying within token bounds.

**EC-012: Slack Block Kit Overflow**
*   **Scenario:** Gemini generates multiple "High-Energy" creative directions for TikTok that are extremely long. Combined with the other platform scripts, the total message payload exceeds Slack's hard 50-block limit.
*   **Impact:** The Slack API returns a `400 Bad Request`, and the user never sees their generated scripts.
*   **Expected System Behavior:** The system MUST calculate the Block Kit count before posting. If the count exceeds 45 blocks, the system MUST automatically split the delivery into multiple threaded replies (e.g., "TikTok Scripts" in the main post, "X Scripts" in the first reply) to ensure successful message delivery.

**EC-013: Slack Bot File Upload Limits**
*   **Scenario:** HeyGen returns a high-resolution 60-second video render that is 600MB, exceeding the reliable bot upload threshold for Slack.
*   **Impact:** Slack returns an error upon webhook delivery, and the user receives no content despite being charged API credits.
*   **Expected System Behavior:** The FastAPI callback handler MUST inspect the `content-length` of the HeyGen asset. If the file exceeds 512MB, the system MUST bypass the direct Slack file upload. Instead, it MUST upload the file to an AES-256 encrypted organizational bucket and post a Slack Block Kit message with a "Secure Download" link that automatically expires in 24 hours to comply with media ephemerality constraints (NFR-014) and data encryption standards (NFR-010).

**EC-014: Target Deletion Mid-Flow (Orphaned Callbacks)**
*   **Scenario:** A user triggers a rendering job that takes 15 minutes. While the job is processing, the user deletes the originating Slack channel, or an admin uninstalls the Slack bot.
*   **Impact:** The render completes successfully, but the FastAPI webhook handler receives a 404/Forbidden when attempting to post the final video back to the user, creating an orphaned asset.
*   **Expected System Behavior:** The FastAPI callback handler MUST verify the existence/permissions of the `slack_channel_id` before posting. If the target is invalid, the system MUST route the final asset URL to a "Safe Harbor" administrative log in MongoDB and attempt to DM the Marketing Ops Lead with the retrieved asset and the error context.

**EC-015: Cross-Workspace Permission Rejection**
*   **Scenario:** Alex triggers a render for Sarah via Slack Connect. Sarah’s Enterprise Admin has disabled the "External App File Upload" policy.
*   **Impact:** Asset delivery fails silently or results in an unhandled Slack API error after expensive APIs have already been triggered.
*   **Expected System Behavior:** Upon HITL approval but *prior* to external API triggering, the system MUST verify the bot’s capability to post to the target channel (via `auth.test` and `conversations.info`). If the check fails (e.g., `missing_scope`), the system MUST halt the pipeline and display a Slack Block Kit alert to Alex: "Action Required: The client's Slack workspace is blocking file uploads. Please contact the administrator or use a 'Secure Link' delivery method."

**EC-016: Secure Download Link Expiration & Access**
*   **Scenario:** A user attempts to access a "Secure Download" link (generated via EC-013) 48 hours after delivery.
*   **Impact:** The user expects the file, but it has been purged to comply with NFR-014 (Ephemerality).
*   **Expected System Behavior:** The link handler MUST serve a custom "Asset Expired" landing page that explains the 24-hour security policy and directs the user to re-render the content if needed. Under no circumstances should the system attempt to "recover" a purged asset.

## Error Handling

### 1. Input Validation Errors

**ERR-001: Invalid Media Ingestion (Images)**
*   **Error Condition:** User-uploaded reference photos fail Clip + ViT validation (resolution < 512px, poor lighting, or synthetic/AI detection confidence > 80%).
*   **System Action:** FastAPI rejects the payload; logs `INVALID_MEDIA`. No database write occurs.
*   **User Resolution:** Ephemeral Slack Block Kit message: "Image [X] rejected: [Reason]. Please ensure well-lit, real human photographs and re-upload."

**ERR-002: Malformed Concept Prompt**
*   **Error Condition:** `/avatar-generate` prompt is empty, > 1,500 characters, or violates safety filters.
*   **System Action:** `SlackInterpretMessageTool` intercepts; logs `BAD_REQUEST` or `SAFETY_VIOLATION`.
*   **User Resolution:** Thread reply: "Your prompt could not be processed. Please ensure it is between 10 and 1,500 characters and adheres to brand safety guidelines."

**ERR-003: Invalid Media Ingestion (Audio)**
*   **Error Condition:** Audio sample fails SNR (< 15dB) or digital clipping (> 10%) thresholds.
*   **System Action:** FastAPI rejects the file; logs `AUDIO_QUALITY_LOW`.
*   **User Resolution:** Ephemeral Slack message: "Audio rejected: Too much background noise or distortion. Please record in a quiet environment and re-upload."

### 2. AI Orchestration & Agent Errors

**ERR-004: yt-dlp Metadata Extraction Failure**
*   **Error Condition:** IP rate-limits or zero keyword matches during research.
*   **System Action:** Trigger Graceful Degradation (NFR-009); log `NON_FATAL_AGENT_TIMEOUT`.
*   **User Resolution:** Silent fallback. User receives a high-quality draft generated from static platform-optimized templates.

**ERR-005: Gemini Context Window Overflow**
*   **Error Condition:** Conversational refinement thread exceeds token limits (>10 iterations).
*   **System Action:** Trigger auto-summarization of history. If summarization fails to clear limits after 2 attempts, halt process and log `TERMINAL_CONTEXT_EXHAUSTION`.
*   **User Resolution:** Automatic self-healing (5-8s delay). If terminal, Slack message: "Thread context limit reached. Please start a new generation request for further tweaks."

### 3. Third-Party API & Rendering Errors

**ERR-006: Third-Party API Timeout / 5xx Error**
*   **Error Condition:** HeyGen or ElevenLabs returns 5xx or times out.
*   **System Action:** 3x Exponential Backoff Retry (2s, 4s, 8s). On final failure, move to DLQ; log `PROVIDER_UNAVAILABLE`. Instantly rollback "Soft-Reserved" credits in MongoDB.
*   **User Resolution:** Slack message: "Rendering provider delay. Your credits have been refunded. Please try again in a few minutes." Notify Jordan (Ops).

**ERR-007: Tenant Budget Exhaustion (402 Error)**
*   **Error Condition:** Organization exceeds its hard-capped API budget.
*   **System Action:** Dispatcher throws `INSUFFICIENT_FUNDS`; aborts job.
*   **User Resolution:** Block Kit alert: "Rendering aborted: Organizational API budget exceeded. Contact your administrator to increase the limit."

### 4. Infrastructure, Compliance & Delivery Errors

**ERR-008: Slack Webhook Delivery Failure (Orphaned Asset)**
*   **Error Condition:** FastAPI callback receives 404/Forbidden (deleted channel/bot uninstalled).
*   **System Action:** Route asset URL to `safe_harbor_logs` in MongoDB.
*   **User Resolution:** Auto-DM to Marketing Ops Lead (Jordan) with the asset and the error context.

**ERR-009: Likeness Revocation Race Condition**
*   **Error Condition:** Admin revokes avatar while render is in queue.
*   **System Action:** Consume RabbitMQ kill signal; force `JOB_ABORTED_COMPLIANCE`. Execute deep purge.
*   **User Resolution:** Slack message to user: "Render aborted: Avatar profile is no longer available due to compliance revocation. Credits refunded."

**ERR-010: Integration Permission Block**
*   **Error Condition:** Pre-render check detects client workspace blocks file uploads.
*   **System Action:** Halt pipeline before API trigger; log `PERMISSIONS_LOCKED`.
*   **User Resolution:** Alert Alex (Operator): "Client workspace blocks file uploads. Switch to 'Secure Link' delivery mode in settings."

**ERR-011: Secure Download Link Expiration**
*   **Error Condition:** User clicks link > 24 hours after delivery.
*   **System Action:** Route handler detects expired token; blocks access.
*   **User Resolution:** Redirect to custom "Asset Expired" landing page explaining the 24-hour security purge.

**ERR-012: Event Publication Failure**
*   **Error Condition:** The FastAPI gateway cannot establish a connection to RabbitMQ during the initial `/slack/kickoff` handshake.
*   **System Action:** The system intercepts the standard 202 Accepted response within the 3-second Slack timeout window and forces a synchronous 500 error to prevent a "ghost" request state.
*   **User Resolution:** Ephemeral Slack message: "System is currently unable to process requests. Please try again in a few minutes." Notify Jordan (Ops) of broker failure.

## Success Metrics

To evaluate the viability and performance of the Slack-based MVP and its underlying agentic architecture, success will be measured across four core dimensions. All metrics shall be instrumented via MongoDB event logging and RabbitMQ status tracking from the first day of launch.

**1. Core Business & Efficiency Metrics**
*   **Human Operational Efficiency:** Reduce the **Total Interaction Session Duration** (defined as the cumulative delta: the sum of time between a Slack event being delivered and the user's subsequent interaction via button click or text reply) for a standard 4-platform batch from the 4-hour manual baseline to **< 15 minutes**.
*   **System Fulfillment Latency:** Achieve a P90 delivery time of **< 15 minutes** for the asynchronous rendering pipeline, measured from the moment of the HITL "Approve & Render" click to the final asset delivery in the Slack thread.
*   **Cost-Efficient Generation:** Maintain an average third-party API cost (Gemini + ElevenLabs + HeyGen) of **under $2.00 per platform asset** or **under $8.00 per standard 4-channel campaign batch**, validated via automated MongoDB cost-attribution logs.

**2. Technical Reliability & Performance**
*   **Orchestration Success Rate:** Maintain a **sub-5% terminal failure rate** across the end-to-end multi-agent pipeline. A failure is only considered "terminal" if it persists after the 3x RabbitMQ dead-letter queue retry logic.
*   **UI/UX Responsiveness:** The FastAPI gateway must acknowledge Slack commands within **< 2.0 seconds** (P99) to satisfy Slack's strict webhook timeout and eliminate redundant user retry events.
*   **Validation Efficacy:** The Clip + ViT gateway must maintain a **< 1% False Rejection Rate (FRR)** for high-quality images, ensuring the pre-flight check does not create friction for users with valid media.

**3. Quality & Workflow Adoption (Human-in-the-Loop)**
*   **Script Precision (Agentic Accuracy):** Achieve a **Script Iteration Velocity of $\le$ 2 cycles**. On average, users should not need to use the "Refine" loop more than once before finding a script suitable for rendering.
*   **Asset Intent Conversion:** Maintain a **Drop-off Rate of < 5%** between the "Script Approved" state and the final "Approve & Render" trigger. A high drop-off indicates that the agentic output is failing to build user confidence before the expensive rendering phase.
*   **Agentic Intelligence Utility:** Track the **Obsidian Knowledge Hit Rate**. The system must successfully inject structural metadata extracted from trending videos via the `yt-dlp` research agent into at least **80% of script generation cycles**, while ensuring "Graceful Degradation" (fallback due to agent failure or timeout) is triggered in **< 20%** of total cycles.

**4. Compliance & Risk Mitigation**
*   **Likeness Revocation SLA:** Achieve a **100% success rate** for the "Deep Purge" protocol, ensuring all biometric reference data is purged from internal storage and external API hooks (HeyGen/ElevenLabs) within **5 minutes** of an administrator trigger.
*   **Copyright-Safe Ephemerality:** Maintain a **0% retention rate** of raw copyrighted media. System audit logs must verify that all `.mp4` artifacts are purged from ephemeral storage immediately upon metadata extraction, ensuring the system never persists third-party IP.

## Dependencies

The successful delivery and operation of the CrewAI-Orchestrated Multi-Channel Video Avatar Platform relies on the following critical dependencies across third-party services, infrastructure, and organizational protocols.

### 1. Third-Party API & Vendor Dependencies
*   **HeyGen API (Enterprise Tier):** Essential for spatial video rendering and avatar modeling. The platform relies on their asynchronous webhook callbacks for job completion and heavily depends on their **Usage/Credit APIs** to query near real-time balances, satisfying the "Soft-Reserve" lock and Circuit Breaker logic (EC-004, NFR-015).
*   **ElevenLabs API:** Required for biometric voice cloning. Dependency includes their low-latency synthesis endpoints and rate-limit/credit metadata APIs to prevent programmatic exhaustion.
*   **Google Gemini API (Flash 1.5 & Pro 1.5):** The primary LLM workforce. Pro 1.5 is required for complex reasoning and intent parsing, while Flash 1.5 handles high-volume script iterations to keep operating costs under the $2.00/asset threshold.
*   **Slack API & Block Kit:** A hard dependency for the Phase 1 MVP UI. The platform requires strict adherence to Slack's **3.0-second webhook acknowledgment timeout** to maintain the decoupled architecture and prevent terminal event loops.

### 2. Identity, Access, & Communication
*   **Cloud Identity Provider (IdP):** A managed service (e.g., Auth0 or Google Identity Platform) is mandatory to satisfy the email-based account guardrail. We depend on a centralized IdP to manage persistent Gmail/Email accounts and map them to unique Slack Workspace `user_id`s, future-proofing the Phase 2 Web/Mobile expansion.
*   **Cloud Secret Manager (e.g., AWS Secrets Manager):** A hard dependency for enterprise compliance (NFR-016). All third-party vendor keys must be dynamically injected and rotated via a dedicated secrets service rather than hardcoded in environment variables.
*   **Transactional Email Service (e.g., SendGrid or AWS SES):** Required as a fallback communication layer. The system depends on this service to deliver account verification and "Critical System Failure" "Safe Harbor" notifications to the Marketing Ops Lead in the event of a Slack App uninstallation (EC-014).

### 3. Core Infrastructure & Persistence
*   **RabbitMQ (Hosted/CloudAMQP):** The central event broker. The system depends on RabbitMQ for asynchronous task offloading, "Leaky Bucket" rate limiting, and Dead Letter Queue (DLQ) retry management.
*   **MongoDB Atlas (M10 Tier+):** The multi-tenant data layer. Depends on Atlas for encrypted-at-rest biometric metadata, job state tracking, and organizational context isolation (NFR-011).
*   **Cloud Object Storage & CDN (e.g., AWS S3 or GCP Cloud Storage):** A critical dependency for delivering large video assets (>512MB) that exceed native Slack bot upload limits. We rely on this storage for generating **Pre-signed Secure Download URLs** (EC-013) and mandate **Bucket Lifecycle Policies** to automate the 24-hour purge of raw media, ensuring copyright-safe ephemerality (NFR-014).
*   **Distributed Markdown Vault (e.g., AWS EFS):** A shared file-system dependency allowing stateless CrewAI workers in a distributed environment to concurrently read and write to the Obsidian-formatted LLM knowledge graph without locking collisions.

### 4. Machine Learning & Model Inference
*   **GPU Compute Hosting (e.g., AWS G-Series or Lambda Labs):** Required for hosting the **Clip + ViT** models. The system depends on CUDA-accelerated environments to reliably meet pre-flight image validation latency targets (NFR-018).
*   **yt-dlp & Rotating Proxy Provider:** Used for trending metadata extraction. Depends on a commercial proxy service (e.g., Bright Data) and active open-source community updates to bypass constantly evolving social media anti-bot measures and minimize graceful degradation fallbacks.
*   **CrewAI Framework (Latest Stable):** The foundational orchestration layer. The platform depends on CrewAI for multi-agent collaboration, state transitions, memory management, and task routing.

### 5. Organizational & Legal Dependencies
*   **Biometric Consent Models:** Legal viability strictly depends on external HR and legal documentation of explicit consent for employee or influencer likeness synthesis prior to system ingestion.
*   **Platform Safety & ToS Compliance:** Ongoing dependency on the Terms of Service and AI-disclosure policies of target networks (TikTok, YouTube Shorts, Threads, and X) to ensure brand-safe, compliant content delivery and avoid shadowbans.

## Assumptions

### 1. Technical & API Viability
*   **Lip-Sync & Aspect Ratio Stability:** It is assumed that the HeyGen API natively supports high-fidelity, audio-driven lip-syncing for static photo-generated avatars using external ElevenLabs audio tracks. Furthermore, we assume this synchronization remains visually stable and professional across all four target aspect ratios (9:16, 1:1, 16:9) without requiring manual spatial adjustments by the user.
*   **Vendor Scalability (Concurrency):** It is assumed that the third-party API providers (HeyGen, ElevenLabs, Gemini) offer enterprise-tier access with internal concurrency limits high enough to support 50+ simultaneous video rendering requests (aligning with NFR-004) without triggering internal queuing or "noisy neighbor" throttling.
*   **Asynchronous UX Reliability:** We assume that the FastAPI and RabbitMQ decoupled architecture will successfully maintain a sub-2.5s acknowledgment response time for the Slack gateway, even under peak load, ensuring that Slack's strict 3-second webhook timeout never terminates a user’s session.
*   **Token Window & Refinement State:** We assume that the Google Gemini API's effective context window and reasoning stability are sufficient to manage up to 10 rounds of conversational refinement for four distinct script variants simultaneously, without degrading the platform-specific creative direction or losing the injected "Mood Parameters."
*   **Scraping Persistent Viability:** We assume that `yt-dlp`, supported by a commercial rotating proxy service, will remain capable of bypassing constantly evolving social media anti-scraping measures to extract structural metadata for the Obsidian knowledge graph.
*   **Structural Metadata Consistency:** It is assumed that the structural patterns of viral short-form content (hook timing, pacing) are stable enough across 30-day windows to allow the Obsidian knowledge graph to serve as a reliable reference for the script generation agent.
*   **Codec Compatibility:** We assume that third-party rendering outputs are delivered in standard formats (e.g., MP4) compatible with all target social media platforms without the need for additional server-side transcoding.

### 2. User Behavior & Adoption
*   **Conversational Workflow Acceptability:** It is assumed that the primary target personas (agency operators and marketing managers) are willing to adopt a text-based Slack interface for complex creative approvals, prioritizing speed and workflow unification over the visual control of a traditional timeline-based video editor.
*   **Creative Template Sufficiency:** We assume that high-quality, pre-defined rendering templates provide enough visual variety to satisfy professional B2B clients during the Phase 1 MVP lifecycle, negating the immediate need for granular, layer-based spatial editing.
*   **Minimum Input Context:** It is assumed that users will provide a "simple idea" constituting at least a single descriptive sentence (15+ words) or a set of 3+ specific keywords. This baseline context is assumed necessary for the intent-parsing agents to function without triggering infinite prompt loops or hallucinations.
*   **Executive UX Accessibility:** It is assumed that the Slack Block Kit interface provides sufficient clarity for executive-level personas (Sarah) to perform cross-workspace brand approvals without requiring specialized technical training or a traditional graphical user interface.

### 3. Legal, Compliance, & Brand Safety
*   **Structural Metadata Fair Use:** We assume that the temporary download and analysis of copyrighted videos—solely to extract non-copyrightable structural metadata (such as pacing and hook frameworks)—and the immediate deletion of the raw media constitutes "Fair Use" and protects the platform from IP infringement liability.
*   **Administrative Compliance:** It is assumed that organizational administrators will accurately and promptly trigger the Likeness Revocation protocol upon an employee or influencer's departure, as the system cannot autonomously determine the legal termination of a person's biometric likeness rights.
*   **Upstream Consent Management:** We assume that the clients (digital agencies and enterprise brands) are independently securing all necessary legal rights and written consent from individuals before uploading their photos and voice samples to the platform.

### 4. Operational & Cost Dynamics
*   **Baseline Validation:** It is assumed that the current manual process of scripting, generating audio, rendering video, and managing outputs for four disparate channels genuinely takes an average of four hours of active human input, validating the business case for our 15-minute fulfillment goal.
*   **Cost Efficiency of Refinement:** We assume that iterating through text-based scripts via Gemini (low-cost) prior to triggering video rendering (high-cost) will successfully prevent financial waste and maintain the target budget of $8.00 per 4-channel campaign batch.
