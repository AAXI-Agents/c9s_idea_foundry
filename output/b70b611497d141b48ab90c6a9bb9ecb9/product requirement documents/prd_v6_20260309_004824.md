# Product Requirements Document

## Executive Summary

**Problem Statement**
The digital marketing and creator economy sectors are severely bottlenecked by the operational friction and context-switching required to produce high-velocity, platform-native video content. While standalone generative AI tools for voice and avatar synthesis are abundant, B2B content teams lack a unified, brand-safe, and asynchronous workflow to efficiently transform raw ideas into compliant, personalized omnichannel video assets without risking algorithmic penalties or reputational damage.

**Target Audience**
This platform is built for Directors of Content Strategy, Scaled Media Operations professionals, boutique marketing agencies, and B2B founders who require scalable, high-fidelity video generation embedded directly into their existing operational workflows.

**Proposed Solution**
We are introducing an event-driven, multi-agent CrewAI orchestration platform delivered entirely via a Slack-first MVP. This system seamlessly transforms raw text concepts submitted in a Slack channel into fully realized, 15-to-60-second personalized talking-head videos. Key differentiators include:
* **Multi-Agent Orchestration:** Deploys specialized CrewAI agents for competitive visual research (via yt-dlp, Clip, and ViT), platform-native scripting (Gemini), and automated compliance. A dedicated Tone Agent cross-references all scripts against an Obsidian-backed knowledge graph to guarantee strict brand safety before user review.
* **Omnichannel Personalization & Variance:** Dynamically alters scripting hooks and ElevenLabs voice inflections to match the distinct vernacular of specific platforms (e.g., TikTok vs. LinkedIn) while utilizing pre-processed user avatars rendered via HeyGen.
* **Resilient Async Architecture:** Decouples high-latency, high-cost third-party API calls using a RabbitMQ pub/sub exchange and MongoDB Atlas persistence, safely managing retries and dead-letter queues to shield users from upstream API flakiness.
* **Frictionless High-Fidelity Review:** Bypasses Slack's native video compression by generating secure, time-expiring web-viewer links directly in the thread, enabling side-by-side variant reviews and Block Kit-driven approvals without leaving the chat interface.

**Business Value & Success Criteria**
This Slack MVP is phased to empirically validate visual fidelity standards and strictly measure unit economics before authorizing the development of a standalone React frontend. The initiative's success is tied to the following quantitative targets:
* **Performance SLAs:** Maintain a p95 webhook acknowledgment latency of <200ms, a strict 2-minute SLA for compliance-vetted script returns, and a maximum 12-minute end-to-end rendering SLA for a 60-second video.
* **Unit Economics & Reliability:** Enforce a hard API cost ceiling of $1.50 per final video generation while achieving a 95% overall pipeline completion rate.
* **Adoption:** Capture a 25% Daily Active User (DAU) engagement rate among installed Slack workspaces within the first quarter.

## Problem Statement

**Current State of Video Production**
The digital marketing landscape increasingly demands high-velocity, platform-native video content to maintain share of voice across networks like TikTok, YouTube Shorts, X, Threads, and LinkedIn. While generative AI models for scriptwriting, voice synthesis, and avatar rendering have successfully commoditized the individual components of video creation, the actual production workflow remains fundamentally broken. Currently, B2B content teams and boutique agencies are forced to manually string together campaigns across disjointed tools. Internal audits indicate that producing a single, cross-platform video campaign requires an average of 4.5 hours of manual assembly across 5 or more different AI and editing applications.

**Core Pain Points**
*   **Severe Operational Friction:** Content strategists face massive context-switching overhead. Current workflows require users to leave their primary communication hubs (e.g., Slack) to manually transfer prompts, scripts, and rendered assets between web-based dashboards. This causes "notification fatigue" and breaks the feedback loop between the strategist and the generation tools, turning what should be a rapid process into a multi-hour operational bottleneck.
*   **Brand Safety and Compliance Risks:** In B2B marketing, a single hallucinated product claim or off-brand tone can trigger legal scrutiny and severe reputational damage. Existing generative tools lack automated, asynchronous compliance guardrails, forcing teams to rely on manual human review cycles that delay time-to-market by up to 48 hours.
*   **Algorithmic Penalties and Visual Degradation:** Social media algorithms are actively cracking down on generic AI generation. Recent updates to TikTok and Meta algorithms prioritize "originality" scores; generic, unedited AI exports are frequently flagged as low-quality, resulting in up to a 70% reduction in organic reach. Furthermore, raw user image uploads frequently suffer from a lack of lighting and contrast normalization, as well as incorrect resolution. This results in "uncanny valley" avatars that see a 40% lower viewer completion rate compared to high-fidelity or human content.
*   **Lack of Omnichannel Variance:** Generating a single, monolithic video asset is no longer viable. However, manually rewriting visual hooks and altering voice inflections to match the distinct vernacular of a TikTok audience versus a professional X or LinkedIn demographic is prohibitively time-consuming, resulting in "lazy" cross-posting that fails to resonate.

**Business Impact**
*   **Inflated Unit Economics:** Marketing teams are burning significant operational capital on manual task orchestration rather than strategic content distribution. This drives the effective cost-per-video (inclusive of labor) up to ~$150, which is fundamentally incompatible with the target scaling threshold of $1.50 per unit.
*   **Reduced Market Agility:** The friction involved in moving from a raw idea to a finalized video prevents brands from rapidly capitalizing on 24-hour social media trend cycles.
*   **Eroded Brand Equity:** Publishing off-brand messaging or visually sub-standard avatars directly negatively impacts user engagement, algorithmic reach, and overall enterprise credibility.

**Urgency: Why Now?**
Social platform algorithms and the creator economy are evolving faster than traditional B2B production workflows can support. The fundamental market gap is no longer a lack of advanced generative models, but the absence of a unified, brand-safe orchestration engine embedded natively into where teams already work. Without a chat-based (Slack) solution that automates research, compliance vetting, and omnichannel rendering without disrupting daily workflows, scaling personalized media operations will remain structurally and financially impossible for lean marketing teams.

## Functional Requirements

### Epic 1: Workspace & Authentication

**FR-001: Google Single Sign-On (SSO)**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The system shall authenticate users via Google OAuth 2.0 to establish and map their user profile within the FastAPI backend.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   *Given* an unauthenticated user, *When* they invoke the Slack slash command for setup, *Then* the system returns a secure Google OAuth link.
    *   *Given* a successful Google login, *When* the OAuth callback is triggered, *Then* the system provisions a user account in MongoDB Atlas mapped to their Slack user ID.
*   **API Endpoint:**
    *   `GET /auth/google/callback`
    *   Request: `code`, `state`
    *   Response: `200 OK` (User mapped) or `401 Unauthorized`

**FR-002: Media Channel Configuration**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The system shall allow users to create and manage multiple distinct "Media Channels" (e.g., "Agency Client A") to maintain distinct platform configurations and avatars.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   *Given* an authenticated user, *When* they submit a channel creation command in Slack, *Then* the system initializes a new channel profile in MongoDB, supporting tagging for TikTok, YouTube Shorts, Threads, X, and LinkedIn.

### Epic 2: Avatar Personalization & Ingestion

**FR-003: Training Asset Ingestion & Validation**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The system shall allow users to upload exactly 5–10 baseline photographs and 1 voice sample directly into a Slack thread to initiate avatar personalization.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   *Given* a user uploads files in a designated Slack thread, *When* the FastAPI webhook catches the payload, *Then* the system validates that images are JPG/PNG (max 5MB each) and audio is MP3/WAV (max 30 seconds).
    *   *Given* valid files, *When* validation passes, *Then* the system persists the raw files to a secure cloud bucket mapped to the specific Media Channel ID.

**FR-004: Automated Image Pre-Processing & Calibration State**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The system shall execute automated pre-processing on image uploads and track HeyGen training status to prevent "uncanny valley" results and avoid generation failures.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   *Given* newly uploaded images, *When* the pre-processing worker triggers, *Then* the system normalizes lighting, corrects contrast, and upscales images to 1080p.
    *   *Given* processed images, *When* passed to HeyGen, *Then* the system updates the Media Channel status in MongoDB to `CALIBRATING`.
    *   *Given* a `CALIBRATING` status, *When* a user attempts to generate a video for that channel, *Then* the system blocks the request until the HeyGen status webhook returns `READY`.

### Epic 3: Iterative Multi-Agent Workflow

**FR-005: Slack Event Trigger & Orchestration Kickoff**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The system shall expose a webhook to catch raw content ideas submitted by users in Slack, translating them into a CrewAI execution payload.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   *Given* a user submits a raw idea via a Slack message, *When* the FastAPI webhook receives the payload, *Then* it acknowledges the request in <200ms and triggers the CrewAI event listener to initiate the agent workflow.
*   **API Endpoint:**
    *   `POST /slack/events`
    *   Request: Slack Event API JSON payload
    *   Response: `200 OK` (Acknowledged)

**FR-006: Visual Context & Research Agent (yt-dlp + Clip/ViT)**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** A CrewAI Research Agent shall fetch trending reference videos and extract visual hooks to inform script generation.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   *Given* a raw idea, *When* the Research Agent is activated, *Then* it uses `yt-dlp` to download 1-3 contextual reference videos and passes them to Clip/ViT models to output a structured JSON summary of visual pacing.

**FR-007: Omnichannel Script Generation (Gemini)**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** A CrewAI Scripting Agent shall draft 15-60 second video scripts tailored to specific platform vernaculars based on the visual context.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   *Given* visual context data, *When* Gemini drafts content, *Then* it outputs distinct script variants optimized for TikTok (hook-heavy) and LinkedIn (value-heavy).

**FR-008: Threaded Iteration Loop**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The system shall allow users to iterate on scripts through native Slack threaded replies before final approval.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   *Given* a drafted script posted in a Slack thread, *When* a user replies with feedback, *Then* the system passes the conversation history back to the Gemini agent to generate and post a revised script variant in the same thread.

### Epic 4: Compliance & Human-in-the-Loop Review

**FR-009: Automated Brand Compliance Agent (Obsidian)**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** A Compliance Agent shall intercept scripts and cross-reference them against an Obsidian-backed knowledge graph to enforce brand safety.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   *Given* a drafted script, *When* the Compliance Agent processes it, *Then* it queries the Obsidian knowledge graph and automatically rewrites sections that violate regulatory or voice constraints.

**FR-010: Block Kit Interactive Approval**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The system shall return vetted scripts to Slack using Block Kit buttons for user authorization.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   *Given* a vetted script, *When* pushed to Slack, *Then* the user is presented with "Approve", "Refine", or "Cancel" buttons. 
    *   *Given* a user interaction, *When* "Approve" is clicked, *Then* the system triggers the RabbitMQ rendering queue.
*   **API Endpoint:**
    *   `POST /slack/interactions`
    *   Request: Slack Block Kit action payload
    *   Response: `200 OK`

### Epic 5: Asynchronous Rendering & Delivery

**FR-011: Cost-Gated Rendering Orchestration (RabbitMQ)**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The system shall validate unit economics ($1.50 limit) using an internal Cost Mapping Table and decouple rendering by publishing to a RabbitMQ exchange.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   *Given* an approved script, *When* the rendering cost is estimated via a Cost Mapping Table (evaluating script character count vs ElevenLabs rates, and duration vs HeyGen rates), *Then* the system publishes the job to RabbitMQ only if the cost is $\le$ $1.50 per final video variant.

**FR-012: Conditional Voice & Avatar Rendering (HeyGen & ElevenLabs)**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The rendering worker shall handle conditional logic for Instant Voice Cloning (IVC) before executing voice synthesis and avatar video generation.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   *Given* an active rendering job, *When* the Channel Voice ID does not exist, *Then* the system triggers an ElevenLabs IVC task using the voice sample ingested in FR-003.
    *   *Given* an active rendering job with an existing Channel Voice ID, *When* executed, *Then* the system performs voice synthesis via ElevenLabs and passes the resulting audio to HeyGen to render the final variant using the mapped Channel Avatar ID.

**FR-013: High-Fidelity Web Viewer Delivery**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The system shall deliver rendered videos via secure, time-expiring web links to bypass Slack compression.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   *Given* a successfully rendered video, *When* the job completes, *Then* the backend generates a time-expiring URL and posts it to the Slack thread for side-by-side omnichannel review.

**FR-014: Omnichannel Finalization & Distribution**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The system shall provide finalization actions in Slack to allow the user to formally distribute or download the approved video asset.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   *Given* rendered video variants delivered via web link, *When* the user selects a "Finalize & Publish" Block Kit action for a specific platform, *Then* the system marks the job state as `FINALIZED` and returns a direct, uncompressed download link to the user.

## Non-Functional Requirements

### 1. Performance & Latency Requirements
*   **NFR-001: Webhook Acknowledgment Latency:** The FastAPI webhook intercepting Slack events SHALL respond with a `200 OK` within a strict p95 latency of **200 milliseconds** to avoid Slack retry-loop triggers.
*   **NFR-002: Agent Orchestration SLA:** The end-to-end multi-agent cycle (Research, Scripting, Compliance) SHALL return the initial vetted script to the user within a **p95 of 2 minutes**.
*   **NFR-003: Render Pipeline SLA:** The asynchronous rendering pipeline SHALL complete voice and video synthesis for a 60-second variant in a **maximum of 12 minutes**.
*   **NFR-004: Delivery Latency:** Web-viewer links SHALL be delivered to the Slack thread within **5 seconds** of the job state changing to `COMPLETED` in MongoDB.

### 2. Scalability, Reliability & Availability
*   **NFR-005: System Concurrency & Throughput:** The backend SHALL support a minimum of **100 concurrent agent orchestration cycles** and **50 concurrent rendering jobs**. The FastAPI application layer SHALL handle a sustained throughput of **50 Requests Per Second (RPS)** with a burst capacity of 100 RPS to accommodate high-volume Slack event ingestion without degrading performance targets.
*   **NFR-006: Pipeline Completion Rate:** The system SHALL maintain a minimum **95% successful completion rate** for all rendering jobs.
*   **NFR-007: Availability & Uptime:** The system (API and Web-viewer) SHALL maintain a **99.9% uptime SLA**, measured monthly, excluding scheduled maintenance.
*   **NFR-008: Resiliency (DLQ):** All third-party API failures (e.g., 429 rate limits, 5xx errors from HeyGen/ElevenLabs) SHALL be retried using an exponential backoff strategy for up to 3 attempts before being routed to a **Dead Letter Queue (DLQ)** for manual intervention.

### 3. Cost & Unit Economics
*   **NFR-009: API Cost Ceiling:** The orchestration engine SHALL enforce a hard-coded unit cost ceiling of **$1.50 per final video variant**. This estimate MUST be calculated pre-render based on script length and character counts against current vendor rate cards.

### 4. Security, Compliance & Data Privacy
*   **NFR-010: Data Encryption:** All user training assets (photos/voice) and rendered videos SHALL be encrypted using **AES-256 at rest** and TLS 1.2+ in transit.
*   **NFR-011: Data Retention & Lifecycle:** Raw training assets (unprocessed photos/voice) SHALL be automatically purged **30 days** after successful calibration to protect biometric privacy. Resulting Calibration Metadata (Avatar/Voice IDs) SHALL be persisted indefinitely for the duration of the account's active status to ensure a frictionless recurring user experience.
*   **NFR-012: Link Security:** Web-viewer links MUST be delivered as **signed, time-expiring URLs** with a maximum Time-To-Live (TTL) of 7 days to ensure data privacy and prevent unauthorized sharing.
*   **NFR-013: Compliance Gating:** No script variant SHALL be delivered to the rendering pipeline unless it has been cryptographically flagged as `CLEARED` by the CrewAI Compliance Agent querying the Obsidian knowledge graph.

### 5. Quality & Accessibility
*   **NFR-014: Visual Normalization (Uncanny Valley Mitigation):** The system MUST enforce a **minimum resolution of 1080p** and a **contrast-to-lighting variance of <15%** for all finalized avatars to ensure platform-native quality and prevent algorithmic penalization.
*   **NFR-015: Accessibility (WCAG):** The web-viewer interface and all Slack Block Kit interactions SHALL adhere to **WCAG 2.1 Level AA** standards, ensuring compatibility with screen readers and keyboard navigation.

### 6. Observability
*   **NFR-016: KPI Telemetry:** The system SHALL capture and store metadata for every generation attempt (duration, token usage, API cost, success/failure state) to MongoDB Atlas to support real-time reporting and auditing of the $1.50 unit economic constraint and the 95% completion rate SLA.

## Edge Cases

### 1. Slack Interaction & Input Boundary Conditions
*   **Empty, Ambiguous, or Unready Inputs:** User submits a `/generate-video` Slack command with no text, a highly ambiguous prompt (e.g., "Make a video"), or attempts to generate a video for a Media Channel that is currently in a `CALIBRATING` state.
    *   *Expected Behavior:* The Scripting Agent returns a Slack Block Kit message. For ambiguous prompts, it provides 3 auto-generated clarifying questions (Topic, Audience, Objective). For unready channels, it returns "Channel not ready - Calibration at X%," blocking the workflow and preventing premature API spend.
*   **Excessive Prompt Length:** User pastes a massive >5,000-word document into the Slack thread.
    *   *Expected Behavior:* The system truncates the input at predefined LLM context limits and uses the Gemini agent to summarize the core "hook" for user confirmation before proceeding to the research phase.
*   **Stale or Concurrent Block Kit Interactions:** User clicks "Approve" after 24 hours of inactivity, or multiple users in a shared channel click "Approve" simultaneously due to latency.
    *   *Expected Behavior:* For sessions >24h, the bot triggers a "Session Expired - Restart" button. For concurrent clicks, idempotency logic in the FastAPI backend SHALL discard duplicate events using the `job_id`, ensuring only one render is billed.
*   **Batch Alert Fatigue Prevention:** A user initiates a batch of >5 jobs simultaneously, and multiple jobs fail the cost gate or compliance check.
    *   *Expected Behavior:* The system SHALL consolidate the individual failure alerts into a single summary report posted to the Slack thread to avoid notification flooding.

### 2. Avatar & Media Processing Edge Cases
*   **"Uncanny Valley" Input Violations:** User uploads baseline photographs that fail the automated pre-processing step (e.g., extreme low resolution, poor lighting, or multiple faces detected).
    *   *Expected Behavior:* The pipeline halts before HeyGen ingestion. The system sends a Slack message detailing the specific failure (e.g., "Image 3 contains multiple faces") and requests replacement images.
*   **Voice Sample Quality Failure:** The uploaded voice sample is <10 seconds or contains a high noise floor.
    *   *Expected Behavior:* The system flags the audio before the ElevenLabs API call, prompting the user in Slack for a cleaner or longer sample.
*   **Omnichannel Format Validation (Aspect Ratio Mismatch):** A rendering job is requested for a specific platform (e.g., TikTok at 9:16) but the mapped Media Channel's avatar calibration is set to 16:9.
    *   *Expected Behavior:* The system SHALL apply automated smart-cropping or letterboxing based on predefined visual normalization templates, notifying the user of the formatting adjustment in the final delivery message rather than failing the render.

### 3. Agent Orchestration & Constraints
*   **Compliance Agent Infinite Loop:** Gemini drafts a script that is consistently flagged and rewritten by the Compliance Agent.
    *   *Expected Behavior:* After 3 failed internal iterations, the workflow halts and pushes the flagged draft to Slack with annotations detailing *why* it failed, requiring manual human-in-the-loop correction.
*   **Obsidian Knowledge Base Unavailability:** The API or sync service connecting the Compliance Agent to the Obsidian knowledge graph goes down.
    *   *Expected Behavior:* The system MUST fail-closed. The Compliance Agent SHALL mark the script as `RETRY_PENDING` and notify the Brand Guardian in Slack. It SHALL NOT bypass the compliance check or advance the script to approval.
*   **yt-dlp Reference Video Failure:** Reference URLs are geoblocked, age-restricted, or removed.
    *   *Expected Behavior:* The system catches the `yt-dlp` error and the Research Agent falls back to a pre-cached generic industry baseline for visual hooks to preserve the 2-minute script generation SLA.
*   **Cost Ceiling Breach Prediction:** Approval of a script where ElevenLabs + HeyGen costs are estimated to exceed the $1.50 hard cap.
    *   *Expected Behavior:* The RabbitMQ publishing event is blocked. The user receives a Slack message indicating the script is too long for the budget, providing a one-click option to auto-summarize it.

### 4. Output & Delivery Edge Cases
*   **Audio Length Mismatch (The 60s Limit):** Synthesized voice takes 65 seconds to read a script estimated for 55 seconds, risking a double HeyGen credit charge.
    *   *Expected Behavior:* The pipeline SHALL pause *before* initiating the HeyGen render. The system prompts the user via Slack to either "Auto-compress" (increase playback speed by 5% via `ffmpeg`) or "Manual Edit" to stay within the 1-credit ($1.50) limit.
*   **Partial Pipeline Failure (Sunk Cost Recovery):** ElevenLabs synthesis succeeds, but the HeyGen render fails due to a transient error.
    *   *Expected Behavior:* When retrying from the Dead Letter Queue (DLQ), the worker SHALL reuse the successful `audio_url` from MongoDB Atlas, bypassing the ElevenLabs synthesis stage to prevent double-billing.
*   **Vendor Hard Outage & Systemic Credit Failure:** HeyGen/ElevenLabs experiences a multi-hour API downtime, or the platform's vendor wallet returns an "Insufficient Credits" error.
    *   *Expected Behavior:* The system alerts the user via Slack: "Our rendering partner is currently experiencing high latency/maintenance. Your job is queued and will finalize automatically." Simultaneously, to prevent 429 rate-limit errors, the RabbitMQ worker SHALL implement a 'Vendor Concurrency Gate' that throttles outgoing requests to match specific vendor API tier limits. For credit exhaustion, IT Operations is notified immediately.
*   **Unauthorized Web-Viewer Access:** A user attempts to forward the secure web-viewer link to a public forum or accesses it after the 7-day TTL.
    *   *Expected Behavior:* The FastAPI backend validates the signed URL; if expired or malformed, it returns a generic 403 Forbidden page with no metadata exposed.

## Error Handling

**1. Error Taxonomy & Status Codes**
The system shall categorize errors into four distinct layers, each with specific handling and user feedback mechanisms:

*   **Category 1: Input Validation & User Errors (4xx)**
    *   *Examples:* File size >5MB, invalid format, ambiguous prompt, or uncalibrated channel.
    *   *Handling:* Instant rejection via the FastAPI webhook. Any partial uploads of biometric assets (photos/voice) MUST be automatically purged from cloud storage within 60 seconds of a validation failure.
*   **Category 2: Agentic Orchestration Errors (Internal 5xx)**
    *   *Examples:* Gemini API timeout, Obsidian knowledge graph sync failure, or `yt-dlp` download block.
    *   *Handling:* Research Agent falls back to pre-cached generic industry hooks. Compliance Agent MUST fail-closed (state: `RETRY_PENDING`) if Obsidian is unreachable.
*   **Category 3: Third-Party Generation Errors (External 429, 5xx)**
    *   *Examples:* HeyGen rate limit, ElevenLabs "Insufficient Credits", or rendering engine timeout.
    *   *Handling:* Asynchronous exponential backoff (base 30s, multiplier 2, max 3 attempts). Systemic vendor outages MUST trip a global circuit breaker to pause the queue.
*   **Category 4: Business Logic Constraints (402, 403)**
    *   *Examples:* Pre-render estimation >$1.50 cap, or access to an expired web-viewer link.
    *   *Handling:* Hard block with specific Slack Block Kit actions for auto-summarization or script editing.

**2. User-Facing Error Messaging (Slack Interface)**
All error messages surfaced in Slack MUST be actionable, professional, and non-technical.
*   **Input/Format Errors:** "We couldn't process one of your files. Please ensure images are JPG/PNG under 5MB and try uploading again."
*   **Compliance Block:** "Our brand safety checks are temporarily offline. To protect your brand, I've paused this generation. I will notify you when it's safe to resume."
*   **Cost Ceiling Breach:** "This script is highly detailed and will exceed your $1.50 per-video budget. Would you like me to auto-summarize it, or do you want to edit it manually?"
*   **Vendor Outage:** "Our video rendering partner is currently experiencing high traffic. Your job is safely queued and will process automatically as soon as capacity opens up."

**3. System-Level Error Handling & Resiliency**
*   **Webhook Resilience:** If the FastAPI layer cannot parse a Slack event, it SHALL return a `200 OK` while logging the parsing failure. Malformed user input SHALL trigger an ephemeral "Ghost Message" indicating the error to prevent the user from assuming the system is hung.
*   **Agent Graceful Degradation:** If `yt-dlp` fails, the Research Agent SHALL NOT fail the pipeline; it MUST fall back to a pre-cached industry-standard JSON baseline of visual hooks.
*   **Obsidian Fail-Closed Protocol:** If the Compliance Agent cannot reach the knowledge graph, the script state becomes `RETRY_PENDING`. It SHALL NOT bypass the check.
*   **RabbitMQ Retry Strategy & Circuit Breaker:** 
    *   For transient third-party errors (429, 5xx), the worker SHALL execute an exponential backoff (base 30s, multiplier 2, max 3 attempts).
    *   *Vendor Circuit Breaker:* If a Category 3 error rate exceeds 20% across all active jobs in a rolling 5-minute window, the system SHALL temporarily 'trip' the circuit, pausing all outgoing requests to that specific vendor and notifying IT Operations.
    *   Persistent errors (400, 401, 403) or exhausted retries SHALL route to a Dead Letter Queue (DLQ).
*   **Idempotency & Sunk Cost Recovery:** 
    *   All ElevenLabs synthesis requests MUST utilize a unique idempotency key (derived via a hash of `script_content` + `voice_id`) to prevent duplicate billing during transient network failures.
    *   When retrying from the DLQ, the worker SHALL query MongoDB Atlas using this idempotency key. If valid synthesis metadata (`asset_id` or `audio_url`) exists from a previous attempt, the worker MUST bypass the voice generation stage and proceed directly to rendering.

**4. Logging and Alerting Requirements**
*   **Error Logging:** Every error MUST be logged to MongoDB Atlas with the `run_id`, `job_id`, timestamp, error category, and the raw vendor error message.
*   **Threshold Alerting:** The system SHALL trigger a critical alert (PagerDuty/Internal Slack) if:
    *   Pipeline completion rate drops below 95% over a rolling 1-hour window.
    *   The DLQ accumulates >10 messages within 30 minutes.
    *   A "Systemic Credit Failure" (vendor wallet empty) is detected.
*   **Batch Alert Grouping:** To prevent notification fatigue for power users, if more than 5 errors occur for a single user/workspace within 1 minute, the system SHALL consolidate the alerts into a single summary notification in Slack.
*   **Cost Anomaly Alerting:** Any single generation attempt that bypasses the $1.50 cost-gate MUST trigger an immediate alert to the Director of Content Strategy.

## Dependencies

**1. Third-Party API & AI Vendor Dependencies**
*   **HeyGen Enterprise API:** The asynchronous rendering pipeline strictly depends on a **HeyGen Enterprise Plan**. This contractual dependency is required to secure the discounted per-credit rate necessary to achieve the < $1.50 per-video unit economic cap (NFR-009) and to unlock the API concurrency limits needed to support 50 simultaneous rendering jobs (NFR-005).
*   **ElevenLabs API:** Required for Instant Voice Cloning (IVC) and omnichannel voice synthesis. The system depends on a commercial ElevenLabs tier to handle the required low-latency audio generation without triggering aggressive rate limits.
*   **Google Gemini API:** Serves as the primary LLM engine driving the CrewAI Scripting Agent and orchestration flow. The workflow depends on Gemini's availability, context window limits, and sustained API uptime.
*   **Visual Context Scraping (`yt-dlp`) & Rotating Proxies:** The CrewAI Research Agent depends on `yt-dlp` to download contextual video data from external social platforms. Because platforms actively combat scraping, this introduces a strict technical dependency on a **Rotating Proxy Service** to bypass geo-blocking and IP reputation bans.
*   **Vision Models (Clip & ViT):** Depends on local or dedicated Hugging Face endpoint deployments of Clip and ViT to extract visual pacing data from the downloaded reference videos.

**2. Platform & Infrastructure Dependencies**
*   **Slack Events & Block Kit API:** As a Slack-first MVP, the core user experience depends entirely on the Slack API. Delivery relies on Workspace Administrators granting proper OAuth scopes (`chat:write`, `files:write`, `commands`) and Slack’s Block Kit rendering correctly across desktop and mobile clients.
*   **MongoDB Atlas & Vector Search:** The system depends on MongoDB Atlas for full state persistence, tracking jobs, and enforcing the cost-mapping logic. Crucially, the Compliance Agent depends on **MongoDB Atlas Vector Search** to efficiently query the brand safety guidelines.
*   **Obsidian Sync Worker:** Because Obsidian is a local-first markdown tool used by the Legal team, the backend depends on a dedicated **Sync Worker Service**. This service must continuously parse the local Obsidian vault via a secure tunnel and push embedding updates to the MongoDB Atlas Vector Index so the CrewAI Compliance Agent can query them asynchronously.
*   **Cloud Object Storage (AWS S3 / GCP):** The system depends on enterprise cloud storage buckets to persist user-uploaded biometric assets (photos and voice samples). This infrastructure must enforce bucket-level AES-256 encryption and automated 30-day lifecycle deletion rules to satisfy data privacy constraints (NFR-010, NFR-011).
*   **RabbitMQ:** Critical infrastructural dependency for event-driven decoupling. The workflow depends on RabbitMQ to manage message broker logic, cost-gated routing, and Dead Letter Queue (DLQ) retry mechanisms for third-party API calls.

**3. Internal Cross-Functional Dependencies**
*   **Legal & Compliance Team (Brand Guardians):** The system’s brand safety guarantee depends entirely on the Legal team actively maintaining, updating, and structuring the master Obsidian knowledge graph. Stale regulatory data will result in compliant but legally inaccurate scripts.
*   **Data & Analytics Engineering:** To empirically validate the Slack MVP, the product team depends on Data Engineering to configure Mixpanel/Amplitude tracking for the metrics defined in the Success Metrics section. This includes setting up 12-hour attribution windows for publishing conversion, tracking cost estimation variance (<5% target), and implementing Abandoned Flow USAT surveys.
