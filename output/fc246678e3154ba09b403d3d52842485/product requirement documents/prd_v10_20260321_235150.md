# Executive Summary

**Product Overview**
The Jurisdictional Compliance & Payment Orchestrator (JCPO) is an advanced RegTech and AccountingTech platform designed to automate the "long tail" of state and local entity compliance. Operating as an intelligent, digital Business Process Outsourcer (BPO), JCPO eliminates the manual administrative burden of managing municipal fees, property tax assessments, and Secretary of State annual reports.

**Problem Statement**
Mid-market and enterprise companies operating across multiple US jurisdictions face a highly fragmented and manual compliance workflow because local governments overwhelmingly lack modern APIs. Accounting teams are forced to manually parse unstructured paper notices and navigate archaic municipal web portals. This causes massive operational inefficiencies, error-prone manual ERP data entry, and a critical risk of missed deadlines—ultimately leading to severe statutory penalties or suspended business licenses.

**Target Audience**
*   **VPs of Finance & Corporate Controllers:** Seeking to eliminate expensive manual BPO overhead, mitigate legal liability, and ensure SOC 1/SOC 2 audit readiness.
*   **Tax Directors & Accounting Staff:** Requiring relief from manual data entry, legacy portal navigation, and tedious ERP ledger reconciliations.

**Proposed Solution & Key Differentiators**
Unlike standard API aggregators that fail in the absence of local government APIs, JCPO leverages an autonomous, agentic architecture to seamlessly connect unstructured municipal data with core financial systems.
*   **Agentic Data Extraction & Navigation:** Utilizing the **crewAI framework** and **Google Vertex AI**, autonomous agents scrape municipal websites and parse unstructured, multimodal documents (e.g., scanned paper notices) to extract critical compliance data.
*   **Native ERP Orchestration:** A robust **Python 3.11 and FastAPI** backend integrates directly with the **NetSuite REST API** and **QuickBooks Online (QBO) API**. When an AI agent stages a payment, the system automatically creates the corresponding "Bill" mapped to the correct General Ledger account and syncs payment statuses for automated bank reconciliation.
*   **Maker/Checker Risk Mitigation:** To strictly govern the risk of AI hallucination, JCPO employs a **React + TypeScript** frontend optimized purely for exception management. The AI acts as the "Maker" staging the data, while a human Controller acts as the "Checker." 
*   **Immutable Audit Trails:** Every AI decision, confidence score, and human approval is immutably logged in **MongoDB Atlas** to satisfy rigorous SOC 1 and SOC 2 compliance audits.

**Business Impact & Success Criteria**
By deploying JCPO, organizations will drastically reduce administrative compliance costs while mathematically minimizing the risk of statutory penalties. The initial MVP release will be evaluated against the following SMART criteria:
*   **Extraction & Staging Performance:** Given a complex unstructured document (e.g., a scanned Cook County property tax notice), Vertex AI must extract the Amount Due and Due Date with **99% accuracy**, and the FastAPI backend must stage a NetSuite Bill via the REST API within **5 seconds**.
*   **Zero-Touch Scale:** The crewAI agents must successfully navigate, extract, and stage status data from at least **50 targeted state Secretary of State web portals** completely without human intervention.
*   **End-to-End Reconciliation:** Upon "Execute & Sync" approval in the React frontend, the system must successfully post the payment to the QBO API and instantly update the MongoDB Atlas ledger status to 'Reconciled'.
*   **Strict Confidence Thresholds:** Any Vertex AI data extraction returning a confidence score below **95%** must be automatically flagged and routed to the exception-management dashboard for human intervention.

### 1.2 Technology Stack Decisions
*   **Backend:** Python 3.11 + FastAPI. *Rationale:* Native support for async I/O (perfect for AI and ERP API multiplexing), excellent ecosystem for AI SDKs (crewAI, Vertex).
*   **Frontend:** React + TypeScript. *Rationale:* Strict typing for complex nested financial data. Ecosystem support for robust PDF rendering (e.g., `react-pdf`) and canvas overlays (confidence bounding boxes).
*   **Database:** MongoDB Atlas. *Rationale:* Flexible schema is critical for storing highly varied and unpredictable scraped data from disparate government portals (`extracted_payload`), while native validation rules enforce structure on core entities.
*   **Async Task Queue:** Celery + Redis (or MongoDB change streams). *Rationale:* FastAPI must return `202 Accepted` immediately for document uploads and task schedules. Heavy LLM and scraping tasks must run in isolated worker processes to prevent event-loop blocking.
*   **AI/ML:** Google Vertex AI (Gemini 1.5 Pro) for multimodal extraction & entity resolution. crewAI for agentic portal navigation.

## 4. Data Model

### 4.1 MongoDB Schema Decisions
*   **NoSQL Rationale:** The `extracted_payload` from crewAI scraping is highly variable across 50+ jurisdictions. MongoDB handles this cleanly while using JSON Schema validation for strictly required fields.
*   **Transactions:** Multi-document ACID transactions will be used when saving a decision in `ApprovalRequest` and simultaneously appending the `AuditLog`.

### 4.2 Core Collections (with Constraints)

**`users`**
*   `_id` (ObjectId)
*   `email` (string, unique)
*   `role` (enum: `['uploader', 'controller', 'admin']`)

**`compliance_documents`**
*   *Validation:* `extracted_amount_due` must be `>= 0.00`.
*   *Indexes:* `{ "status": 1, "extracted_due_date": 1 }` (Compound, sorting for UI), `{ "ai_confidence_score": 1 }`.

**`portal_credentials`**
*   *Validation:* `encrypted_username`, `encrypted_password` must exist.
*   *Indexes:* `{ "jurisdiction_id": 1 }` (Unique).
*   *Security:* Application-level AES-256-GCM encryption before DB write. DEK (Data Encryption Key) rotated via KMS.

**`audit_logs`**
*   *Immutability Rule:* API has NO update/delete routes for this collection.
*   *Indexes:* `{ "entity_id": 1 }` (For fast retrieval of history per document).

## 5. Error Handling & Failure Modes

| Component / Action | Failure Mode | Classification | Handling Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Vertex AI Extraction** | API Timeout / 5xx | Major | Celery retry with exponential backoff (max 3). If fails, set state to `ManualReviewRequired`. |
| **Vertex AI Extraction** | Hallucination (Wrong Amount) | Critical | Mitigated by Maker/Checker UI. Approver corrects it. |
| **crewAI Scraper** | Portal UI Changed / Captcha | Major | 5-minute hard timeout. Catch exception, screenshot DOM, set to `FailedNavigation`, send Slack webhook. |
| **FastAPI -> ERP API** | ERP API Rate Limited (429) | Minor | Pause sync queue, retry after `Retry-After` header expires. |
| **FastAPI -> ERP API** | Accounting Period Closed | Critical | Catch 400 from ERP, set to `SyncFailed`, alert UI. Requires Controller to manually open period or shift date. |
| **Database Sync** | Concurrent UI Approvals | Minor | Optimistic Concurrency Control (OCC) using document versioning (`__v`). Second request fails safely. |

## 7. Security & Trust Boundaries

### 7.1 Attack Surface Analysis
*   **Malicious File Uploads:** A user could upload a payload masquerading as a PDF.
    *   *Mitigation:* FastAPI uses `python-magic` to strictly validate MIME type bytes (not just file extension). Files are stored in an isolated GCS bucket without execution permissions.
*   **Government Portal Credential Theft:** DB breach exposes local gov passwords.
    *   *Mitigation:* Credentials encrypted at rest via AES-256-GCM using Google Cloud KMS. The database never sees plain text.
*   **Webhook Spoofing:** Attacker sends fake `Paid` webhook to mark bills as Reconciled.
    *   *Mitigation:* All inbound ERP webhooks require HMAC-SHA256 signature validation against a stored tenant secret.

### 7.2 Data Classification
*   **Public/Internal:** Jurisdiction IDs, Task Types.
*   **Confidential:** Tax Amounts, Due Dates, PDF Scans.
*   **Highly Restricted:** `PortalCredential` plaintext, ERP API Keys.

## Problem Statement

**The Fragility of Corporate "Good Standing"**
For mid-market to enterprise companies operating across multiple US states, maintaining jurisdictional compliance is a highly fragmented, disproportionately risky operational nightmare. While transactional taxes (like sales tax) have been largely solved by platforms with modern APIs (e.g., Avalara), the "long tail" of entity compliance—encompassing hundreds of county property tax assessments, city business licenses, Secretary of State annual reports, and local franchise taxes—remains completely manual. Local municipalities overwhelmingly lack modern APIs, relying instead on physical mail, unstructured PDFs, and archaic, 1990s-era web portals.

Currently, accounting and tax teams manage this operational burden through sheer brute force: utilizing bloated spreadsheets, physical sticky notes, and expensive offshore Business Process Outsourcing (BPO) services. This reactive, manual approach fundamentally breaks down at scale and introduces severe systemic risks.

**Core Pain Points**
*   **The Black Hole of Unstructured Data:** Because local governments do not offer API integrations, accounting clerks are forced to manually monitor hundreds of disparate municipal portals or physically open mailed tax notices. Extracting critical metadata—such as Assessment Amounts, Due Dates, and Jurisdiction IDs—from these unstructured sources is tedious, error-prone, and impossible to scale dynamically.
*   **Manual ERP Ledger Orchestration:** Identifying a liability is only half the battle. Paying these localized fees requires a clerk to manually log into the financial system of record (e.g., NetSuite, QuickBooks Online), manually create a Vendor Bill, map it to the correct General Ledger expense account, route it for approval, execute the payment, and manually reconcile the bank ledger. This swivel-chair integration breaks the segregation of duties and introduces massive operational inefficiencies.
*   **The Existential Risk of Statutory Penalties:** The ultimate pain point is not just administrative annoyance; it is the asymmetric risk of failure. ## Functional Requirements

### 1. Document Ingestion & AI Extraction (Feature 1)

**FR-001: Asynchronous Document Upload**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The system must accept uploaded PDF and image files (compliance notices) and store them securely before processing to ensure the UI remains responsive.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   *Given* a user has selected a valid compliance document file, *When* they submit the upload form, *Then* the system must return a HTTP `202 Accepted` response immediately.
    *   *Given* the 202 response, *When* the backend processes the request, *Then* the file must be written to Google Cloud Storage (GCS) and a `ComplianceDocument` record created in MongoDB with the status set to `Uploaded`.
*   **API Endpoint:** `POST /api/v1/documents/upload`

    *   *Response Schema:* `{"document_id": "string", "status": "Uploaded"}`.

**FR-002: Multimodal AI Extraction (Vertex AI)**
*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The system must utilize Google Vertex AI to extract structured JSON data from the unstructured uploaded documents.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:**
    *   *Given* a document in the `Uploaded` state, *When* the Celery worker picks up the task, *Then* the state must transition to `Extracting`.
    *   *Given* the document is `Extracting`, *When* processed by Vertex AI, *Then* the system must extract the `Jurisdiction`, `Amount Due`, `Due Date`, `AI Confidence Score`, and the `Bounding Box Coordinates` for the extracted fields.

*   **Priority:** SHALL
*   **Description:** The system must automatically route documents based on the Vertex AI extraction confidence score.

    *   *Given* the extraction is complete, *When* the `AI Confidence Score` is < 95% OR a required field is null, *Then* the document status must transition to `ManualReviewRequired`.

## Non-Functional Requirements

### 1. Performance & Latency
*   **Asynchronous Ingestion Target:** The `POST /api/v1/documents/upload` endpoint MUST return a `202 Accepted` response to the client within 500ms (p95) to ensure the UI remains non-blocking for high-volume uploads.
*   **ERP API Staging Throughput:** The system MUST be capable of staging a NetSuite/QBO Bill via the ERP API worker within 5 seconds of a Controller clicking "Approve Execution" in the React frontend.
*   **AI Inference Tolerance:** Given the complexity of Vertex AI multimodal extraction, the Celery extraction worker MAY take up to 30 seconds per document but MUST enforce a hard timeout of 60 seconds per task before marking it as failed.
*   **UI Rendering:** The React frontend MUST load the split-pane exception dashboard (including the PDF canvas and confidence bounding boxes) within 2 seconds (p95) on standard broadband connections.

### 2. Security & Data Protection
*   **Credential Encryption:** All government portal credentials stored in the `PortalCredential` MongoDB collection MUST be encrypted at rest using AES-256-GCM. Decryption MUST only occur in memory within the isolated crewAI worker just-in-time for execution.
*   **Key Management:** The Data Encryption Key (DEK) used for AES-256-GCM MUST be securely managed and rotatable via Google Cloud Key Management Service (KMS).
*   **Data Isolation:** Uploaded compliance documents (PDFs/Images) MUST be stored in private Google Cloud Storage (GCS) buckets without public execution permissions. Access MUST be mediated via short-lived, pre-signed URLs generated by the FastAPI backend.
*   **Webhook Validation:** All inbound webhooks from external ERPs (NetSuite, QBO) MUST be authenticated via HMAC-SHA256 signature validation against securely stored tenant secrets.
*   **Authentication & Authorization:** API access MUST be secured via JWT-based Role-Based Access Control (RBAC), strictly enforcing roles (`uploader`, `controller`, `admin`). 

### 3. Compliance & Auditability
*   **SOC 1 / SOC 2 Mandate:** The system MUST maintain strict segregation of duties; the backend MUST mathematically guarantee that the user ID initiating a document upload or manual edit cannot be the same user ID that approves the payment execution.
*   **Immutability Constraint:** The `AuditLog` MongoDB collection MUST be append-only. The API MUST NOT expose any `PUT`, `PATCH`, or `DELETE` endpoints for audit records. 
*   **Transactionality:** State transitions for approvals (e.g., `Pending` to `Approved`) and the corresponding `AuditLog` creation MUST be committed within a single ACID multi-document transaction in MongoDB Atlas.
*   **Logging Hygiene:** Structured JSON application logs MUST NOT contain Personally Identifiable Information (PII), plaintext passwords, or raw base64 document payloads.

### 4. Reliability & Availability
*   **Uptime SLA:** The core FastAPI backend and React frontend MUST maintain a 99.9% uptime SLA.
*   **Asynchronous Resilience:** The Celery/Redis task queue MUST be configured for persistence to ensure no Vertex AI extraction tasks or ERP Sync tasks are lost during an unexpected worker pod restart.
*   **Graceful AI Degradation:** If Vertex AI experiences an outage, the system MUST gracefully degrade by moving all newly uploaded documents directly to the `ManualReviewRequired` state rather than failing silently or losing data.
*   **Scraper Circuit Breaker:** crewAI web scraping tasks MUST enforce a 5-minute hard timeout to prevent worker thread starvation caused by hanging local government web portals or unsolvable captchas. 

### 5. Observability & Monitoring
*   **AI Confidence Tracking:** The system MUST instrument the `vertex_ai_confidence_score` as a Histogram metric. An automated alert MUST be triggered to the engineering team if the p90 confidence score drops below 0.95 over a 24-hour rolling window.
*   **Agent Success Tracking:** The system MUST instrument the `crewai_navigation_success_rate` as a Gauge metric. An alert MUST be triggered if the success rate drops below 85% over a 24-hour period.
*   **Distributed Tracing:** Every incoming request MUST be assigned a unique `trace_id` which must be propagated through FastAPI, Celery workers, and all structured JSON log outputs to allow cross-boundary debugging.

## Edge Cases

### 1. Document & Extraction Edge Cases

*   **Zero-Dollar or "Information Only" Notices**
    *   *Scenario:* A jurisdiction sends an annual report notice that requires a filing but has no associated fee ($0.00 amount due).
    *   *System Behavior:* Vertex AI must be prompted to explicitly recognize $0.00 as a valid `Amount Due`. The system must skip the `PendingSync` ERP payment step (since there is no bill to pay) and transition the document state directly to `Reconciled` once the filing is confirmed via crewAI scraping or manual human review.
*   **Duplicate Document Uploads**
    *   *Scenario:* A clerk accidentally uploads the exact same physical scanned PDF twice, or uploads a document for a liability that crewAI has already scraped and staged.
    *   *System Behavior:* After Vertex AI extraction, the backend must query MongoDB for existing `ComplianceDocument` records matching the exact combination of `Jurisdiction ID`, `Amount Due`, and `Due Date` within the current fiscal year. If a match is found, the new document is flagged as a potential duplicate in the `ManualReviewRequired` state, allowing the Controller to merge or delete the redundant record.
*   **Multi-Entity or Multi-Parcel Tax Bills**
    *   *Scenario:* A single uploaded PDF contains tax assessments for three different corporate subsidiaries or three different real estate parcels in the same county, with a single consolidated total or multiple sub-totals.
    *   *System Behavior:* Vertex AI must be instructed to extract arrays of entities. If multiple distinct liabilities are detected on a single document, the system must split the record into multiple `ComplianceDocument` child objects in MongoDB, linking back to the parent PDF. The React UI must present these as distinct line items for approval, mapped to their respective ERP subsidiary accounts.
*   **Illegible or Damaged Scans**
    *   *Scenario:* A paper notice is uploaded with a coffee stain obscuring the due date, causing Vertex AI to return a null value or a confidence score below the 95% threshold.
    *   *System Behavior:* The system transitions the document to `ManualReviewRequired`. The React frontend explicitly highlights the missing/low-confidence field. The clerk must manually input the missing data, which transitions the document to `StagedForApproval` for the Controller.

### 2. Workflow & Concurrency Edge Cases

*   **Concurrent Approval Race Condition**
    *   *Scenario:* Two Controllers log into the React dashboard simultaneously and attempt to click "Approve Execution" on the exact same `StagedForApproval` compliance task at the exact same millisecond.
    *   *System Behavior:* The MongoDB `ApprovalRequest` collection must enforce Optimistic Concurrency Control (OCC) using a version key (`__v`). The first request successfully increments the version and commits the transaction. The second request fails the version check, the transaction aborts, and the FastAPI backend returns a `409 Conflict` to the second Controller's UI, instructing them to refresh the dashboard.
*   **Uploader/Approver Role Collision (Segregation of Duties)**
    *   *Scenario:* A user with the `Admin` role uploads a document and then immediately attempts to approve it, violating SOC 1/SOC 2 compliance rules.
    *   *System Behavior:* The FastAPI backend must enforce strict separation at the API layer. The `POST /api/v1/approvals/{id}/decision` endpoint must inspect the `uploader_user_id` of the document. If it matches the `user_id` in the JWT token of the current requester, the request is hard-rejected with a `403 Forbidden`, and an unauthorized attempt is logged in the `AuditLog`.

### 3. Portal & ERP Integration Edge Cases

*   **Closed Accounting Periods in ERP**
    *   *Scenario:* The Controller approves a $500 franchise tax payment in the React UI. The FastAPI worker attempts to POST the bill to NetSuite, but the current month's accounting period is already hard-closed in NetSuite, resulting in a 400 Bad Request.
    *   *System Behavior:* The sync task catches the specific ERP error code, transitions the voucher to `SyncFailed`, and surfaces the specific NetSuite error message in the React UI. The Controller is prompted to either manually shift the posting date to the current open period within the JCPO dashboard or reopen the period in NetSuite before clicking "Retry Sync".
*   **Fuzzy Vendor Matching Failure**
    *   *Scenario:* Vertex AI extracts the jurisdiction as "Dept of Rev, TX", but the internal NetSuite vendor list only contains "Texas State Comptroller". The AI fails to match the vendor with a high degree of confidence.
    *   *System Behavior:* The system halts the sync pipeline before calling the ERP API. The task is placed in a `VendorMappingRequired` exception queue in the UI. The Controller must select the correct vendor from a dropdown populated by the ERP API. Once selected, JCPO saves this mapping to MongoDB to automatically resolve future occurrences.
*   **crewAI Captcha / MFA Blockade**
    *   *Scenario:* A crewAI agent attempts to navigate a local county portal, but the county has recently implemented a complex image-based CAPTCHA or triggered an email-based OTP (One-Time Password) flow.
    *   *System Behavior:* The headless browser detects the blocker and halts. The Celery worker captures a screenshot of the DOM, transitions the task to `FailedNavigation`, and fires a webhook to the team's Slack channel. The system pauses future automated runs for that specific `Jurisdiction ID` until an administrator updates the scraping logic or manually resolves the credential block.

### 1. Error Taxonomy & System Behavior

| Error Category | HTTP Status / Code | Trigger Condition | System Action | User-Facing Resolution |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Validation Error** | `400 Bad Request` | Uploaded file is not a valid PDF/Image (fails MIME type check) or payload is malformed. | Reject immediately. Do not queue task. | "Invalid file format. Please upload a PDF, JPG, or PNG." |
| **Authentication Error** | `401 Unauthorized` | Invalid, missing, or expired JWT token. | Deny access. Log event. | Redirect to the Single Sign-On (SSO) login page. |
| **Authorization Guardrail** | `403 Forbidden` | User attempts to approve a document they uploaded (Segregation of Duties violation). | Deny request. Log unauthorized attempt in `AuditLog`. | "Action Denied: You cannot approve a document you uploaded to maintain SOC compliance." |

### 2. Autonomous Agent (crewAI) Error Handling

Because archaic government portals are inherently brittle, the system must handle scraping failures gracefully without crashing the worker or flooding users with false positives.

*   **Brittle Navigation Failures (CAPTCHAs & UI Changes):**
    *   *Trigger:* The crewAI headless browser encounters an impassable CAPTCHA, a 5xx error from the government server, or a fundamentally changed DOM structure, exceeding the 5-minute task timeout.
    *   *System Action:* The worker catches the `TimeoutException`. It captures a base64 screenshot of the final DOM state, transitions the `ComplianceTask` to `FailedNavigation`, and writes the screenshot to the database record. It then suspends future scheduled cron jobs for that specific `Jurisdiction ID` to prevent infinite failure loops.
    *   *User Resolution:* A webhook fires to the `#finance-ops` Slack channel: *"Action Required: The Texas Franchise Tax portal is unreachable or changed. Agent execution paused."* The user can view the DOM screenshot in the React dashboard to diagnose the issue (e.g., seeing a "Site Under Maintenance" banner) and manually trigger a retry when resolved.

### 3. Graceful Degradation Strategy

To ensure "Zero-Anxiety Corporate Good Standing," the system must never silently drop a compliance document due to downstream outages.

*   **Vertex AI Outage:** If Google Cloud Vertex AI services are down, the FastAPI backend will still accept document uploads (`202 Accepted`) and store them in GCS/MongoDB. The Celery worker will detect the continuous 5xx errors from Google, pause the extraction queue, and gracefully degrade by routing all new documents directly to the `ManualReviewRequired` queue in the React frontend. This allows clerks to continue processing urgent deadlines manually until the AI service is restored.
*   **ERP API Outage:** If NetSuite or QBO is down for maintenance, approved `PaymentVoucher` records will remain safely in the `PendingSync` state in MongoDB. The Celery worker will continuously poll the ERP health endpoint and automatically flush the backlog of approved payments once the ERP comes back online.

### 4. Logging and Alerting

*   **Audit Trail Imperative:** Errors involving the `403 Forbidden` (Segregation of Duties) or `409 Conflict` (Concurrency) must be immutably written to the MongoDB `AuditLog` collection, tagged with the `user_id` and `timestamp`, to ensure complete visibility during SOC 1/SOC 2 audits.
*   **Dead-Letter Queue (DLQ):** Any Celery task (Extraction or ERP Sync) that exhausts its maximum retry count must be routed to a Redis Dead-Letter Queue. An automated critical alert is then pushed via Datadog/Grafana to the engineering on-call rotation to investigate the dropped payload.

    *   *Metric Definition:* The median time a Controller spends in the React Dashboard resolving a `ManualReviewRequired` document (e.g., correcting a low-confidence Vertex AI bounding box).
    *   *Baseline:* Finding physical files and manual verification takes ~10 minutes per exception.
    *   *Target:* < 45 seconds per exception, aided by the visual Confidence Heatmap UI. 

### 4. Instrumentation & Analytics Plan

To reliably capture these success metrics without manual calculation, the engineering team must implement the following instrumentation:

*   **MongoDB Analytics Views:** Create materialized views grouping `ComplianceDocument` states (`ManualReviewRequired` vs. `StagedForApproval`) to calculate the Straight-Through Processing (STP) rate dynamically.
*   **Vertex AI Metadata:** Log the explicit `ai_confidence_score` float value returned by Google Vertex AI into Datadog/Grafana to track the p90 confidence threshold over time.
*   **ERP Sync Latency Tracing:** Utilize OpenTelemetry trace IDs to measure the exact millisecond duration between the `POST /api/v1/approvals/{id}/decision` endpoint and the subsequent NetSuite/QBO HTTP response within the Celery worker.

## Dependencies

### 1. External Vendor & API Dependencies
*   **Google Vertex AI API:** The document extraction feature (FR-002) strictly depends on the continued availability, latency, and multimodal capabilities of the Gemini 1.5 Pro model via the Google Cloud Vertex AI SDK. 
    *   *Risk:* High. If Google deprecates the specific multimodal JSON extraction capabilities, the core ingestion pipeline breaks.
    *   *Mitigation:* Isolate the Vertex AI SDK logic behind a clean interface in the Celery worker to allow swapping to an alternative LLM provider (e.g., OpenAI GPT-4o) if necessary.
*   **NetSuite REST API:** The ERP payment orchestration (FR-010) requires the target company to have the NetSuite REST API enabled and Token-Based Authentication (TBA) configured on their tenant.
    *   *Risk:* Medium. Target clients may use archaic NetSuite SOAP endpoints or lack the necessary license tier.
    *   *Mitigation:* The onboarding process must explicitly verify REST API access before full deployment.
*   **QuickBooks Online (QBO) API:** The alternative ERP sync relies on Intuit's QBO API and OAuth2 token refresh workflows.
    *   *Risk:* Medium. OAuth2 refresh tokens can expire or be revoked, requiring manual re-authentication by the Controller.
    *   *Mitigation:* Implement proactive Slack webhooks to alert the Admin/Controller 7 days before an expected token expiration.
*   **Local Government Web Portals:** The crewAI scraper (FR-005) depends on 1990s-era municipal websites remaining online, accessible, and free of unsolvable bot-mitigation tools (e.g., Cloudflare Turnstile, complex CAPTCHAs).
    *   *Risk:* High. Municipalities frequently change their UI DOM structures or introduce CAPTCHAs without warning.
    *   *Mitigation:* Implemented the 5-minute timeout circuit breaker and DOM screenshot capture (Error Handling) to alert human operators when a portal fundamentally changes.

### 2. Infrastructure & Orchestration Dependencies
*   **MongoDB Atlas:** The system's state machines, immutable `AuditLog`, and encrypted `PortalCredential` vault depend entirely on MongoDB Atlas multi-document ACID transactions and its native JSON Schema validation.
    *   *Risk:* Low. Atlas is highly reliable, but misconfiguration could break the Segregation of Duties logic.
    *   *Mitigation:* Enforce strict Infrastructure-as-Code (Terraform) for database provisioning and index creation.
*   **Google Cloud Storage (GCS) & KMS:** The secure ingestion and storage of sensitive tax PDFs depend on GCS, and the AES-256-GCM encryption of credentials depends on Google Cloud Key Management Service (KMS).
    *   *Risk:* Low. Standard cloud infrastructure.
*   **crewAI Framework:** The autonomous web scraping agents depend on the specific multi-agent orchestration paradigms provided by the `crewai` Python library.
    *   *Risk:* Medium. It is a rapidly evolving open-source framework that may introduce breaking changes in minor version bumps.
    *   *Mitigation:* Pin the specific version of crewAI in `pyproject.toml` or `requirements.txt` and establish a strict test suite for the `WebScraperAgent` before any dependency upgrades.
*   **Headless Browser Environment:** The crewAI workers depend on Playwright/Chromium binaries being correctly packaged within the Celery worker Docker containers.
    *   *Risk:* Low. Easily managed via standard Dockerfiles.

### 3. Cross-Functional Dependencies
*   **Corporate IT / SSO Administration:** The authentication model (JWT/RBAC) depends on the client's internal IT team configuring their Identity Provider (e.g., Okta, Azure AD) to pass the correct role claims (`uploader`, `controller`, `admin`) to the JCPO API Gateway.
    *   *Risk:* Medium. Delays in IT configuration will block user acceptance testing (UAT).
    *   *Mitigation:* Provide clear, standardized SSO integration documentation and SAML/OIDC configuration templates during client onboarding.

## Assumptions

### 1. User & Operational Assumptions
*   **Maker/Checker Acceptance:** We assume that VPs of Finance and Controllers will accept the "Maker/Checker" Exception Dashboard paradigm as sufficient risk mitigation for AI-driven financial orchestration, allowing them to sign off on system adoption.
*   **Exception Tolerance:** We assume the volume of documents returning a <95% Vertex AI confidence score (requiring manual intervention) will not exceed 25% of total volume. If this threshold is exceeded, the operational burden will mirror current state BPO processes, negating the ROI.
*   **Centralized Credential Access:** We assume the target enterprise users have the authority and ability to gather and centralize login credentials for the ~50 target local government portals into the JCPO Vault, overriding current decentralized (e.g., sticky notes, individual clerk password managers) practices.

### 2. Technical & Infrastructural Assumptions
*   **Vertex AI Multimodal Viability:** We assume Google Vertex AI's Gemini 1.5 Pro multimodal capabilities are sufficiently mature to extract unstructured JSON (Amount Due, Due Date, Jurisdiction) directly from scanned images with 99% accuracy via prompt engineering alone, without requiring custom-trained OCR models.
*   **Portal Scraping Feasibility:** We assume the targeted archaic (1990s-era) municipal web portals do not heavily employ advanced bot-mitigation software (like Cloudflare Turnstile or mandatory hardware-key MFA) that would fundamentally block the crewAI headless browser from navigating the DOM.
*   **Fuzzy Matching Viability:** We assume the extracted "Jurisdiction String" (e.g., "Cook Cnty Tax") is consistent enough that a secondary LLM prompt can reliably map it against the standardized list of Vendor IDs pulled from the client's NetSuite/QBO instance.

### 3. Financial & Integration Assumptions
*   **Standardized General Ledger (GL) Codes:** We assume that mid-market target companies use standardized General Ledger expense accounts for entity compliance across their subsidiaries, allowing the FastAPI backend to logically map staged bills without requiring complex, per-invoice accounting logic.
*   **Open Accounting Periods:** We assume that when JCPO stages a Bill in the ERP via API, the relevant accounting period in NetSuite or QBO is generally open. While we have edge-case handling for closed periods, we assume closed-period rejections will not be the standard path.

### 4. Regulatory & Compliance Assumptions
*   **Auditor Acceptance:** We assume that the immutable MongoDB `AuditLog`—combined with the strict segregation of duties via JWT roles (`uploader` vs. `controller`)—will definitively satisfy external SOC 1 and SOC 2 auditors assessing automated financial controls.
*   **Legal Liability Shield:** We assume that because the human Controller acts as the final "Checker" (explicitly clicking "Approve Execution" in the React UI), the legal liability for missed or incorrectly paid taxes remains with the user, shielding the JCPO platform from statutory penalty damages.
