# Executive Summary

**Problem Statement**
The current cap table management market, dominated by incumbents like Carta and Pulley, is overwhelmingly "founder-first." These legacy platforms treat back-office accounting, compliance, and multi-tenant portfolio management as afterthoughts. Consequently, financial professionals managing equity for multiple growth-stage startups are forced into highly manual, error-prone workflows. They waste hundreds of billable hours extracting data from complex legal PDFs, building fragile Excel models to project overlapping SAFE dilution, and manually reconciling static cap table exports with general ledgers for ASC 718 stock-based compensation expenses. This "Garbage-In, Garbage-Out" data ecosystem creates severe operational bottlenecks and significant compliance risks for accounting firms.

**Target Audience and Key Stakeholders**
*   **Primary Users:** Managing Partners at fractional CFO firms, internal Controllers, and Venture Accounting specialists who require a multi-tenant "single pane of glass" to manage dozens of client cap tables simultaneously.
*   **Secondary Stakeholders:** Startup Founders and Board Members (who consume the generated natural-language dilution analyses) and Big Four Auditors (who rely on the system's immutable audit trails).

**Proposed Solution and Key Differentiators**
To establish a highly defensible moat in the B2B FinTech space, we are building an **Accounting-Native Equity Management and Predictive Modeling Platform**. Rather than acting as a static tracker, this product operates as a forward-looking, automated financial engine built entirely around the accountant's workflow. The platform leverages our advanced tech stack to deliver three core differentiators:

*   **Autonomous AI Data Ingestion (crewAI):** Multi-agent workflows will autonomously ingest raw legal PDFs (e.g., post-money SAFEs, convertible notes), accurately extract critical variables (valuation caps, discount rates, vesting schedules), and stage them for review, eliminating the paralegal onboarding bottleneck.
*   **Predictive Dilution Modeling (Google Vertex AI):** An interactive modeling engine will dynamically calculate complex waterfall distributions during term-sheet negotiations. Vertex AI will translate this complex math into clear, natural-language impact analyses (e.g., explaining exact dilution percentages to founders triggered by anti-dilution provisions).
*   **Seamless ERP Automation (FastAPI & Python 3.11):** Native integrations will push and pull data directly from NetSuite and QuickBooks Online (QBO). The system will automatically calculate ASC 718 expenses and trigger webhooks to post corresponding journal entries straight into the client’s general ledger.
*   **Audit-Ready Compliance & Risk Mitigation (React/TypeScript & MongoDB Atlas):** To strictly mitigate the risk of AI hallucination and ensure auditability, the React frontend will mandate a "human-in-the-loop" approval flow for all AI-extracted legal terms before they are committed. The MongoDB Atlas backend will maintain an immutable, append-only transaction log to satisfy rigorous audit requirements.

**Business Impact and Success Criteria**
By shifting the focus from the founder's dashboard to the accountant's workflow, this platform will drastically reduce back-office overhead and eliminate manual data entry. Success for this initial release is aggressively quantitative and will be measured against the following SMART criteria:
*   **Onboarding Efficiency:** Achieve a **70% reduction** in new client cap-table onboarding time compared to traditional manual entry methods.
*   **Workflow Automation:** Attain **100% automated** monthly general ledger reconciliation for ASC 718 expenses via native NetSuite and QBO integrations.
*   **System Performance:** Maintain a **sub-2-second latency** for the dynamic regeneration of complex, multi-round waterfall scenario analyses.

### 1.2. Technology Stack & Rationale
*   **Backend:** Python 3.11 + FastAPI. *Rationale:* Native support for async I/O (vital for parallel AI/ERP calls), robust strictly-typed validation via Pydantic (vital for financial payloads), and native Python ecosystem for crewAI and mathematical modeling.
*   **Database:** MongoDB Atlas. *Rationale:* Flexible schema for unstructured extraction metadata, powerful compound indexing for multi-tenant isolation, and high write-throughput for immutable append-only `AuditLog` entries.
*   **Frontend:** React + TypeScript + Figma-based UI. *Rationale:* Strict typing prevents UI state drift; React component model perfectly suits split-screen provenance (PDF on left, extraction on right).
*   **AI/ML:** crewAI (Document Ingestion) and Google Vertex AI (Summarization/Modeling). *Rationale:* crewAI provides excellent orchestration for multi-step agentic extraction. Vertex AI ensures enterprise-grade data privacy (no training on customer data) and highly structured JSON output generation.

### 1.3. Data Flow Diagram: Ingestion & Ledger Extraction

```text
[Happy Path]

                     ──► crewAI extracts terms, confidence > 0.85

                     ──► CFO hits 'Approve'

[Error Path]
crewAI Timeout / Confidence < 0.85 
                     ──► Exception Handler catches
                     ──► DB Update `Draft` 
                     ──► UI flags doc with Warning: "AI Extraction Failed"
```

## 4. Data Model

### 4.1. Entity Relationships (NoSQL Document Strategy)
We use a denormalized approach for read-heavy operations, bounded strictly by `tenant_id`.

*   `LegalDocument` (1) ──► (1) `EquityGrant`
*   `EquityGrant` (1) ──► (*) `AuditLog`
*   `Tenant` (1) ──► (*) `ScenarioModel`
*   `Tenant` (1) ──► (*) `JournalEntrySync`

### 4.2. Database Schema & Indexes (MongoDB)
*   **Strict Multi-Tenancy:** Every collection MUST have `tenant_id`.
*   **Collection: `documents`**
    *   Index: `{ tenant_id: 1, status: 1 }` (Speeds up unparsed doc queries)
*   **Collection: `equity_grants`**
    *   Index: `{ tenant_id: 1, state: 1 }` (Vital for the "PendingCFOReview" queue)
    *   Index: `{ document_id: 1 }`
*   **Collection: `audit_logs`**
    *   Index: `{ tenant_id: 1, entity_name: 1, entity_id: 1 }` (Vital for rendering history for a specific grant)
*   **Collection: `scenario_models`**
    *   Index: `{ tenant_id: 1, created_at: -1 }` (Sorting descending by default)
*   **Collection: `journal_entry_syncs`**
    *   Unique Index: `{ tenant_id: 1, period_end_date: 1 }` (CRITICAL constraint: Prevents duplicate journal entries for the same month).

## 5. Error Handling & Failure Modes

| Component / Boundary | Failure Mode | Handling Strategy | Classification |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **crewAI Extraction** | AI hallucinates numbers / parses wrong clause. | `ai_confidence_score` acts as a circuit breaker. Falls back to `Draft`. HITL UI strictly enforces human validation. | Major (Mitigated by HITL) |
| **crewAI Orchestrator** | Agent loop times out processing a 100-page PDF. | Hard timeout at 5 minutes via Celery worker. State transitions to `Draft`. Alert UI to "Manual Entry Required." | Minor |
| **Vertex AI Summarizer** | Google Cloud API rate limit or outage. | Catch HTTP 429/503. Return deterministic waterfall math in UI immediately. Render `ai_impact_analysis` as fallback string. | Minor |
| **NetSuite / QBO API** | Destination ERP is offline or rejecting auth. | State transitions to `SyncFailed`. Implement Exponential Backoff (retries: 3, 10, 30 mins) via background worker. Surface error explicitly in UI. | Major |
| **Python Waterfall Math** | Complex SAFE stack causes division by zero. | Mathematical layer strictly wrapped in `try/except`. API returns HTTP `422 Unprocessable Entity` with explicit validation error. | Critical |

## 7. Security & Trust Boundaries

### 7.1. Attack Surface Analysis
*   **Multi-Tenant Data Leakage:** The biggest risk. Mitigated by a mandatory FastAPI Dependency (`get_current_tenant`) that automatically injects `tenant_id` into *every* MongoDB query context.
*   **Malicious PDF Uploads:** Mitigated by checking MIME types, scanning via ClamAV on upload, and executing crewAI parsing within sandboxed, ephemeral Docker containers (or gVisor).

### 7.2. Authorization Boundaries
*   **JWT Roles:**
    *   `ACCOUNTANT`: Can upload PDFs, create Draft Scenarios, trigger `PendingCalculation`.
    *   `CFO`: Required to execute `PATCH /commit` on Equity Grants and acknowledge `VarianceReview`.
    *   `ADMIN`: Cross-tenant read-only (Internal superusers).

### 7.3. Data Classification
*   **PII:** `holder_name` and employee details are encrypted at rest using MongoDB Field Level Encryption (FLE).
*   **Financial Ledgers:** Retained immutably for 7 years to satisfy Big Four audit trails.

## 8. Deployment & Rollout

### 8.1. Deployment Sequence
We utilize a Blue/Green deployment strategy on AWS/GCP Kubernetes (EKS/GKE).
1.  **DB Migrations:** Non-breaking schema additions applied to MongoDB via `alembic/motor`.
2.  **Green Backend:** Deploy new FastAPI Docker images alongside old ones.
3.  **Background Workers:** Drain Celery/crewAI queues on Blue, spin up new Green workers.
4.  **Traffic Cutover:** Load balancer points API traffic to Green.
5.  **Frontend:** Deploy new React build to Vercel/CloudFront.

### 8.2. Rollback Plan
1.  **Trigger:** HTTP 500 rate exceeds 1% or p99 latency exceeds 3 seconds.
2.  **Step 1:** Revert API Load Balancer pointer back to Blue cluster.
3.  **Step 2:** Revert Frontend DNS pointer to previous build hash.
4.  **Step 3:** DB modifications must always be forward-compatible; no immediate DB rollback required.

### 8.3. Post-Deploy Verification Checklist
- [ ] Run automated E2E sanity suite against Production.
- [ ] Verify crewAI agent queue is actively processing uploads.
- [ ] Validate MongoDB `AuditLog` writes are completing successfully.

## Problem Statement

The current market for cap table management—dominated by legacy incumbents like Carta and Pulley—is overwhelmingly "founder-first." While these platforms successfully digitize share certificates and provide basic dashboard visibility for founders, they treat back-office accounting, compliance, and multi-tenant portfolio management as afterthoughts. Consequently, corporate equity, a startup's most valuable asset, remains "dead data"—trapped in unstructured legal documents, siloed in static trackers, and completely disconnected from the general ledger.

Financial professionals—specifically Managing Partners at fractional CFO firms, internal Controllers, and venture accountants—are forced to bridge this gap manually. Instead of operating as strategic advisors, they are relegated to the role of data janitors. This systemic failure manifests in three critical operational bottlenecks:

**1. The Data Ingestion Bottleneck ("Garbage-In")**
Onboarding a client’s historical equity data relies entirely on manual transcription. Financial teams and paralegals spend weeks reading complex, unstructured legal PDFs (e.g., post-money SAFEs, convertible notes, option grants) to locate and extract critical variables like valuation caps, discount rates, and vesting schedules. This manual extraction is tedious, expensive, and highly susceptible to human error, creating a flawed foundation for all downstream financial reporting.

**2. The Predictive Modeling Nightmare**
During active term-sheet negotiations, founders require immediate answers regarding dilution. Because existing cap table platforms act merely as historical ledgers, CFOs must export static data into fragile, heavily manual Excel models. Calculating the cascading dilution impact of converting multiple, overlapping SAFEs alongside participating preferred equity rounds takes hours. The friction in modeling these complex waterfall scenarios prevents CFOs from providing real-time, actionable board-level insights when term sheets are live.

**3. The Reconciliation Gap ("Garbage-Out")**
At month-end, the disconnect between the cap table and the ERP system creates a massive compliance burden. Accounting teams must manually export cap table states, calculate ASC 718 stock-based compensation expenses in disconnected spreadsheets, and hand-key the resulting journal entries into NetSuite or QuickBooks Online (QBO). This lack of native integration wastes hundreds of billable hours every quarter and introduces significant audit risks regarding 409A valuations and ledger immutability.

**Business Impact Quantification**
The financial and operational impact of these fragmented workflows is severe. Fractional CFO firms managing dozens of growth-stage startups routinely waste up to 30% of their billable capacity on routine data extraction, cross-system reconciliation, and manual waterfall math. Furthermore, relying on human data entry for complex legal clauses creates a high-risk environment for catastrophic financial miscalculations, which can trigger Big Four audit failures or permanently misinform founder equity distribution.

**Why Now?**
The urgency to solve this problem is driven by the rapid growth of the fractional CFO industry and the increasing structural complexity of early-stage funding rounds (e.g., stacked SAFEs with hidden Most Favored Nation clauses). As portfolio sizes scale, throwing more billable accounting hours at manual data entry is no longer economically viable. The market requires a paradigm shift: abandoning the backward-looking, static compliance tracker in favour of an automated workflow that actively bridges the gap between legal documents and the general ledger.

## Non-Functional Requirements

### 1. Performance and Responsiveness

*   **NFR-001: Waterfall Simulation Latency:** The Python Waterfall Math Engine must execute and return deterministic multi-round scenario calculations (for up to 50 intersecting SAFEs and equity classes) with a p95 latency of `< 2.0 seconds`.
*   **NFR-002: AI Summary Latency:** The generation of the "Explain to Founder" natural language summary via Google Vertex AI must return a response to the UI with a p90 latency of `< 4.0 seconds`.
*   **NFR-003: UI Render Performance:** The React/TypeScript frontend split-screen PDF provenance viewer must load and render highlight overlays for a 50-page PDF in `< 1.5 seconds`.
*   **NFR-004: ERP Sync Throughput:** The background worker handling the ASC 718 NetSuite/QBO synchronization must be capable of processing a batch of 500 `JournalEntrySync` records per minute without triggering third-party API rate limits.

### 2. Security and Data Privacy

*   **NFR-005: Strict Multi-Tenancy:** The FastAPI backend must utilize dependency injection (`get_current_tenant`) to forcefully scope all MongoDB queries. A request originating from one `tenant_id` must have zero mathematical possibility of querying or mutating data belonging to another `tenant_id`.
*   **NFR-006: PII Encryption at Rest:** Personally Identifiable Information (PII) within the `EquityGrant` collection (e.g., `holder_name`, employee addresses, SSNs) must be encrypted at rest using MongoDB Field Level Encryption (FLE).
*   **NFR-007: AI Data Privacy:** Prompts sent to Google Vertex AI and crewAI LLMs must be explicitly configured under an enterprise agreement that strictly prohibits the use of submitted tenant data (term sheets, cap tables) for training external foundational models.
*   **NFR-008: Malicious File Sandboxing:** All PDF uploads must be scanned via ClamAV. The crewAI extraction workers must process PDFs inside ephemeral, strictly isolated Docker containers (or gVisor) to prevent malicious script execution.

### 3. Compliance and Auditability

*   **NFR-009: Immutable Audit Logging:** The MongoDB `AuditLog` collection must be strictly append-only. Any `PATCH`, `PUT`, or `DELETE` request executed against the `EquityGrant` or `JournalEntrySync` collections must automatically trigger a database hook that records the Before/After JSON states, the user's JWT ID, and the timestamp.
*   **NFR-010: AI Trust Boundary Constraint:** The system architecture must structurally prevent Vertex AI and crewAI from directly writing or mutating records in the `Committed` state. All AI inferences must be staged in the `PendingCFOReview` or `Draft` states.
*   **NFR-011: Ledger Retention:** To satisfy Big Four 409A and financial audit requirements, all committed cap table data, associated PDF source files in S3, and immutable audit logs must be retained securely for a minimum of `7 years`.

### 4. Reliability and Error Recovery

*   **NFR-012: ERP Sync Resilience:** Integrations with NetSuite and QBO must implement an Exponential Backoff strategy (retrying at 3, 10, and 30-minute intervals) when encountering `HTTP 429` (Too Many Requests) or `HTTP 5xx` (Server Error) responses.
*   **NFR-013: AI Extraction Circuit Breaker:** If the crewAI extraction pipeline returns an `ai_confidence_score` of `<= 0.85` for a document, or if the process times out after `5 minutes`, the system must immediately halt extraction, default the document state back to `Draft`, and prompt the user for manual entry.
*   **NFR-014: System Availability:** The core application endpoints (UI serving, Python Math Engine, Database) must maintain a minimum uptime SLA of `99.9%` outside of scheduled maintenance windows.

### 5. AI Accuracy and Determinism

*   **NFR-015: Mathematical Determinism:** The Python backend must be the sole authority for all financial arithmetic (pre/post-money calculations, option pool sizing, ASC 718 expense generation). AI agents are strictly prohibited from performing these mathematical operations.
*   **NFR-016: Financial Math Precision:** All deterministic math calculations executed by the Python engine must utilize the `decimal` module, calculating to at least `6 decimal places` of precision to prevent floating-point rounding errors during multi-round cascading dilution scenarios.

## Edge Cases

### 1. Document Ingestion and AI Extraction Edge Cases

*   **EC-001: Contradictory Legal Clauses within a Single Document**
    *   *Scenario:* A scanned PDF contains an amendment that explicitly overrides a valuation cap stated earlier in the same document. The crewAI parser may extract the original, superseded value.
    *   *System Behaviour:* The crewAI agent must be prompted to output a "confidence warning" flag if multiple conflicting values for the same key entity (e.g., `valuation_cap`) are detected. The UI must render a specific warning badge in the `PendingCFOReview` state, forcing the CFO to manually verify the correct clause using the split-screen provenance viewer before permitting the `PATCH /commit` action.
*   **EC-002: Unreadable, Corrupted, or Non-Legal PDF Uploads**
    *   *Scenario:* A user accidentally uploads a restaurant menu or a heavily corrupted, un-OCR-able PDF.
    *   *System Behaviour:* If crewAI fails to extract any mandatory `EquityGrant` schema fields (e.g., entity name, grant date) or the overall confidence score falls below 0.85, the system must abort the `ExtractionPending` state. The document state transitions back to `Draft`, and the UI displays an actionable message: "AI extraction failed: Document illegible or missing required legal terms. Manual data entry required."
*   **EC-003: Rapid Concurrent Approvals (Race Condition)**
    *   *Scenario:* Two `CFO` users simultaneously review the same `EquityGrant` in `PendingCFOReview`. Both make different manual edits to the AI-extracted fields and hit "Approve" at the exact same millisecond.
    *   *System Behaviour:* The FastAPI backend must implement Optimistic Concurrency Control using a `version` field on the MongoDB document. The first request succeeds and transitions the state to `Committed`. The second request fails with an HTTP 409 Conflict, and the UI prompts the second user: "This record was recently modified by another user. Please refresh and review the updated terms."

### 2. Predictive Scenario Modeling Edge Cases

*   **EC-004: Circular SAFE Dependencies (Division by Zero / Infinite Loops)**
    *   *Scenario:* A complex scenario model includes overlapping SAFEs where the conversion price of SAFE A depends on the option pool sizing of SAFE B, which in turn depends on the post-money valuation dictated by SAFE A.
    *   *System Behaviour:* The Python Waterfall Engine must implement a maximum iteration depth limit (e.g., 50 iterations) for resolving circular dependencies. If convergence is not reached, the engine must safely catch the exception, halt the simulation, transition the `ScenarioModel` state to `Failed`, and return an HTTP 422 Unprocessable Entity with a specific error: "Simulation failed: Unresolvable circular dependency in conversion logic."
*   **EC-005: AI Hallucination in Natural Language Summary**
    *   *Scenario:* The Python Math engine outputs correct JSON showing a 5% dilution, but Google Vertex AI hallucinates a completely different number (e.g., "This results in a 50% dilution") in the `ai_impact_analysis` summary.
    *   *System Behaviour:* The UI must establish a strict visual trust boundary. The deterministic math output (tables/charts) must be visually distinct and separate from the AI summary. A disclaimer must accompany the Vertex AI output: *"Summary generated by AI based on the data below. Always verify exact figures."* Furthermore, the system must strictly prohibit Vertex AI from injecting HTML or executable scripts into the summary string to prevent XSS attacks.

### 3. ERP Synchronization Edge Cases

*   **EC-006: Out-of-Period Accounting Sync (Closed Books)**
    *   *Scenario:* The month-end cron job attempts to push an ASC 718 `JournalEntrySync` for October, but the Accountant has already "hard closed" the October period within NetSuite. NetSuite rejects the webhook payload.
    *   *System Behaviour:* The FastAPI backend must capture the specific NetSuite rejection code. The state must transition from `Syncing` to `SyncFailed`. The UI must display the error: "Accounting period is closed in NetSuite." The system must then allow the user to manually override the `period_end_date` in the UI to the current open month (e.g., November) and re-trigger the sync.
*   **EC-007: Month-End Cron Job Interruption**
    *   *Scenario:* The server restarts or the pod is killed exactly while the month-end ASC 718 cron job is mid-execution, leaving some tenant records in `PendingCalculation`.
    *   *System Behaviour:* The cron execution logic must be idempotent. Upon restart, the system must query MongoDB for all `JournalEntrySync` records associated with the current month that are missing or stuck in `PendingCalculation`. It must resume calculations without duplicating entries, enforced by the unique compound index on `{tenant_id, period_end_date}`.
*   **EC-008: Fractional Cent Rounding Discrepancies**
    *   *Scenario:* The sum of all individual ASC 718 amortized expenses for a tenant equals $1,000.004. NetSuite only accepts two decimal places, potentially causing a $0.01 imbalance in the General Ledger if simply truncated.
    *   *System Behaviour:* The Python backend must sum all unrounded theoretical expenses first, round the final total to two decimal places, and then allocate any $0.01 remainder to the largest single `EquityGrant` expense line item in the journal entry payload. This guarantees the debits and credits perfectly balance before initiating the `POST` to the ERP.

## Error Handling

### 1. Error Taxonomy and Logging Requirements

All system errors must be caught by the FastAPI global exception handler, logged to stdout in strictly typed JSON (including `trace_id`, `tenant_id`, and `error_code`), and propagated to the frontend via standard HTTP status codes.

| Error Category | HTTP Code | Internal Code | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Validation** | `422` | `ERR_MATH_001` | Python math engine division by zero or invalid input (e.g., negative shares). |
| **AI Inference** | `400` | `ERR_AI_EXT_002` | crewAI extraction failed (confidence < 0.85) or timed out. |
| **Integration** | `502` | `ERR_ERP_003` | NetSuite or QBO API unreachable, timeout, or payload rejected. |
| **Authentication**| `401` | `ERR_AUTH_004` | Invalid, expired, or missing JWT. |
| **Authorization** | `403` | `ERR_AUTH_005` | `ACCOUNTANT` role attempting to `PATCH /commit` an `EquityGrant`. |
| **Concurrency** | `409` | `ERR_DB_006` | Document version mismatch (Optimistic Locking failure). |

### 2. Document Ingestion Failures (crewAI)

*   **Error Trigger:** The crewAI celery worker fails to extract the required JSON schema from the PDF (due to poor OCR quality, missing clauses, or reaching the 5-minute timeout).
*   **System Response:** Catch the timeout or low-confidence exception. Revert the `LegalDocument` and associated `EquityGrant` state in MongoDB from `ExtractionPending` back to `Draft`.
*   **User-Facing Experience:** The UI stops the polling/websocket loader and displays a highly visible, non-technical alert: *"We couldn't confidently read this document automatically. Please enter the terms manually to proceed."* The user is immediately presented with a clean manual data-entry form alongside the PDF viewer.
*   **Logging:** Log `ERR_AI_EXT_002` with the `document_id`. Page the Data Science team if this specific error exceeds a 20% failure rate over a 24-hour rolling window.

### 3. Predictive Modeling Math Failures (Python)

*   **Error Trigger:** The Python deterministic math engine encounters an impossible mathematical state (e.g., stacked SAFE conversion formulas resulting in a division by zero due to missing pre-money valuation inputs).
*   **System Response:** The Python `try/except` block must safely catch the `ZeroDivisionError` or custom `CapTableMathException`. The `ScenarioModel` state transitions to `Failed`.
*   **User-Facing Experience:** The UI prevents the scenario from rendering and displays an actionable error: *"Simulation failed: The provided term sheet variables result in an invalid conversion. Please check the pre-money valuation and option pool sizing and try again."*
*   **Graceful Degradation:** The UI retains all user inputs so they do not have to start from scratch, highlighting the fields most likely to have caused the mathematical conflict.

### 4. Generative AI Summarization Failures (Vertex AI)

*   **Error Trigger:** The Google Vertex AI API is down, rate-limited (`HTTP 429`), or returns an improperly formatted payload instead of the expected plain-English strings.
*   **System Response:** Catch the HTTP exception. Do *not* fail the entire `POST /scenarios` request, as the deterministic math has already succeeded.
*   **User-Facing Experience:** Render the standard numerical waterfall tables and pie charts. Hide the "Explain to Founder" button. Display a subtle, non-disruptive banner: *"AI summary generation is currently unavailable. The financial calculations below are accurate and complete."*
*   **Recovery:** No automatic retry required. The user can manually click a "Retry Summary" button once the API recovers.

### 5. ERP Synchronization Failures (NetSuite / QBO)

*   **Error Trigger:** The FastAPI backend attempts to post the ASC 718 journal entry payload, but the target ERP returns an `HTTP 5xx` (down) or `HTTP 401` (expired OAuth token).
*   **System Response:** State transitions from `Syncing` to `SyncFailed`. The background worker places the job in a dead-letter queue (DLQ).
*   **System Recovery (Retry Strategy):** Implement an Exponential Backoff strategy for `HTTP 5xx` or `HTTP 429` errors, attempting retries at 3, 10, and 30 minutes. If the error is `HTTP 401` (Auth), suspend retries immediately.
*   **User-Facing Experience:** The UI dashboard for the specific tenant highlights the month-end sync block in red. Error message: *"Connection to NetSuite failed. [If 401: Please re-authenticate your ERP connection in Settings.] [If 5xx: We will automatically retry in 10 minutes.]"* Users are provided a manual "Force Retry" button.

### 1. Primary KPIs (Operational Efficiency)

*   **KPI-001: Cap Table Onboarding Time Reduction**
    *   *Baseline:* Currently, a paralegal/accountant takes an average of 14 days (elapsed time) to manually ingest, transcribe, and reconcile historical legal PDFs for a new Series A client.
    *   *Target:* Reduce the end-to-end onboarding time by **70%** (to under 4 days) within 90 days of the V1 launch.
    *   *Measurement:* Track the timestamp delta in MongoDB between the first `LegalDocument` upload and the final `EquityGrant` transitioning to the `Committed` state for a given tenant.

*   **KPI-002: Automated GL Reconciliation Rate**
    *   *Baseline:* 0% (Currently, 100% of ASC 718 journal entries are hand-keyed into NetSuite/QBO at month-end).
    *   *Target:* Achieve **100% automation** for month-end ASC 718 syncs. 
    *   *Measurement:* Track the ratio of `JournalEntrySync` records that successfully transition to the `Synced` state via the FastAPI webhooks versus those that require manual intervention or remain in `SyncFailed`.

### 2. Secondary Metrics (System Performance & AI Trust)

*   **SM-001: Waterfall Simulation Latency**
    *   *Baseline:* Excel-based waterfall calculations require 30–60 minutes of manual setup and computation time per scenario.
    *   *Target:* Maintain a sub-**2.0-second** p95 latency for regenerating complex, multi-round waterfall analyses.
    *   *Measurement:* Datadog APM tracing on the `POST /api/v1/tenants/{tenant_id}/scenarios` endpoint.

*   **SM-002: AI Extraction Fidelity (Zero Un-flagged Errors)**
    *   *Baseline:* Human manual entry error rates on complex legal documents average 3-5%.
    *   *Target:* **0%** un-flagged AI extraction errors reaching the immutable general ledger.
    *   *Measurement:* Track the frequency of post-commit ledger corrections (i.e., when a `CFO` must reverse a `Committed` grant due to a missed error). This serves as the ultimate lagging indicator of the effectiveness of the "Split-Screen Provenance" and `PendingCFOReview` HITL workflows.

*   **SM-003: crewAI Confidence Fallback Rate**
    *   *Baseline:* N/A (New feature).
    *   *Target:* Less than **15%** of uploaded `LegalDocuments` should fall back to the `Draft` state (requiring manual entry) due to low AI confidence (<0.85) or parsing timeouts.
    *   *Measurement:* Track the state transition ratio of `ExtractionPending` -> `Draft` versus `ExtractionPending` -> `PendingCFOReview` via Mixpanel events.

### 3. Adoption & User Value Metrics

*   **AM-001: Feature Adoption: "Explain to Founder" AI Summary**
    *   *Target:* >60% of all generated `ScenarioModel` outputs should result in the user clicking the "Generate Board Summary" / Copy-to-Clipboard button.
    *   *Measurement:* Frontend event tracking (Mixpanel) recording button clicks relative to total successful scenario generations.

*   **AM-002: Portfolio Expansion Rate (The Moat Indicator)**
    *   *Target:* Fractional CFO firms onboard an average of 5 new tenant cap tables within their first 60 days on the platform.
    *   *Measurement:* Count of distinct `tenant_id` workspaces created by users with the `CFO` role. This is the primary leading indicator that the "1-to-many" B2B distribution strategy is succeeding against founder-first incumbents.

## Dependencies

### 1. Third-Party API & Service Dependencies

*   **DEP-001: NetSuite SuiteTalk API Access**
    *   **Reason:** Required to push ASC 718 journal entries directly to the client's general ledger and receive webhook confirmations.
    *   **Risk & Impact:** High. If a specific tenant's NetSuite administrator denies the OAuth scope or the API rate limits are exceeded, the core value proposition of month-end automation fails.
    *   **Mitigation:** Implement strict token lifecycle management, exponential backoff for `HTTP 429` errors, and a clear fallback manual-export CSV option in the UI.
*   **DEP-002: QuickBooks Online (QBO) Developer Approval**
    *   **Reason:** Required to push ASC 718 journal entries to QBO instances. Intuit requires a formal app review process before granting production API keys.
    *   **Risk & Impact:** Medium. Delays in Intuit’s app review process could block the release of the QBO integration.
    *   **Mitigation:** Initiate the QBO app registration and compliance review process immediately during Sprint 1.
*   **DEP-003: Google Cloud Vertex AI (Gemini 1.5 Pro/Flash)**
    *   **Reason:** Powers the dynamic, natural-language "Explain to Founder" dilution summaries and the AI Variance logic.
    *   **Risk & Impact:** Low likelihood, High impact. Service outages or sudden deprecation of specific model versions could break the summarization feature.
    *   **Mitigation:** Architecture must ensure the deterministic Python math engine can return quantitative scenario data even if the Vertex AI call times out.
*   **DEP-004: Amazon S3 (or GCP Cloud Storage)**
    *   **Reason:** Secure, highly available object storage is required to store the original raw legal PDFs uploaded by the `ACCOUNTANT` persona.
    *   **Risk & Impact:** Low. Standard infrastructure, but missing IAM permissions could break the document ingestion pipeline.
    *   **Mitigation:** Utilize Infrastructure as Code (Terraform) to ensure IAM roles and bucket CORS policies are perfectly mirrored between staging and production environments.

### 2. Infrastructure & Framework Dependencies

*   **DEP-005: crewAI Orchestration Framework**
    *   **Reason:** The designated multi-agent framework required to orchestrate OCR, parsing, and structured JSON extraction from complex legal PDFs.
    *   **Risk & Impact:** Medium. As an evolving framework, breaking changes in minor version updates could disrupt the background worker pipelines.
    *   **Mitigation:** Pin the crewAI dependency version strictly in `pyproject.toml` and maintain a robust suite of unit tests utilizing mocked PDF responses before upgrading.
*   **DEP-006: MongoDB Atlas (Database & Search)**
    *   **Reason:** Provides the persistence layer for the multi-tenant architecture, the immutable `AuditLog` via document versioning, and JSON schema validation.
    *   **Risk & Impact:** Low. Fully managed service, but complex compound indexing (specifically the unique `{tenant_id, period_end_date}`) is critical for data integrity.
    *   **Mitigation:** DBA review required for all `alembic/motor` schema migrations prior to production deployment.

### 3. Cross-Functional Dependencies

*   **DEP-007: External Legal & Compliance Review (Big Four)**
    *   **Reason:** To market the product as a "Truth Engine" and guarantee 409A auditability, the structure of the MongoDB `AuditLog` must be signed off by a certified auditor.
    *   **Risk & Impact:** High. If the audit trail does not meet SOC 1/SOC 2 or specific accounting standards, fractional CFOs will not trust the platform to replace their manual Excel ledgers.
    *   **Mitigation:** Engage a compliance consultant early in Phase 1 (Epic 1) to validate the Before/After JSON ledger schema and JWT timestamp logging.
*   **DEP-008: Data Science / Prompt Engineering Team**
    *   **Reason:** The success of the "Garbage-In" solution relies entirely on the accuracy of the prompts given to the crewAI agents for parsing complex SAFE clauses (e.g., MFN tripwires).
    *   **Risk & Impact:** High. Poorly tuned prompts will result in high fallback rates (confidence < 0.85), frustrating the user with manual entry and defeating the primary KPI.
    *   **Mitigation:** Allocate two full sprints for the Data Science team to construct a test suite of 500+ diverse, anonymized term sheets to benchmark and tune extraction accuracy before beta launch.

## Assumptions

### 1. User & Market Assumptions

*   **Willingness to Switch:** We assume that fractional CFO firms and venture accounting practices will prioritize back-office operational efficiency (and direct ERP integration) over the "founder-facing" dashboards provided by incumbents like Carta and Pulley.
*   **Pricing Power:** We assume that B2B accounting firms are willing to pay a premium SaaS subscription based on "managed entities" (cap tables), provided the platform verifiably reduces their unbillable data-entry hours by the targeted 70%.
*   **Legal Document Standardization:** We assume that while legal documents are "messy," approximately 80% of uploaded documents will loosely follow industry-standard templates (e.g., Y Combinator post-money SAFEs, standard NVCA term sheets) allowing the crewAI agents to extract terms with a baseline confidence score of >0.85 without requiring custom model fine-tuning per client.
*   **Validation Plan:** Conduct A/B prompt testing on 500 historical, anonymized SAFEs during Sprint 1 to validate the 80% baseline extraction accuracy assumption.

### 2. Artificial Intelligence (crewAI & Vertex AI) Assumptions

*   **Vertex AI Latency:** We assume that Google Vertex AI can reliably generate natural-language impact summaries based on provided JSON payloads within our 4.0-second p90 latency target, allowing the "Explain to Founder" UI to feel real-time during live negotiations.
*   **crewAI Concurrency:** We assume the Celery-backed crewAI workers can be horizontally scaled across container instances to process multiple 50+ page legal PDFs simultaneously without causing out-of-memory (OOM) errors or stalling the `ExtractionPending` queues.
*   **Prompt Stability:** We assume that the underlying LLM models used by Vertex AI and crewAI will not experience unannounced "model drift" that severely degrades extraction accuracy after launch. (Validation: Nightly automated CI/CD runs against a static benchmark suite of PDFs).

### 3. Infrastructure & Integration Assumptions

*   **ERP API Rate Limits:** We assume that the volume of month-end ASC 718 journal entries pushed via the FastAPI backend will not exceed the standard API rate limits of NetSuite (e.g., RESTlet limits) and QuickBooks Online, provided our Exponential Backoff retry logic is implemented.
*   **Target ERP Versions:** We assume we are integrating strictly with cloud-native versions of the ERPs (NetSuite Cloud, QuickBooks Online) and will *not* support on-premise QuickBooks Desktop installations.
*   **MongoDB Performance:** We assume that a denormalized NoSQL document strategy in MongoDB Atlas, utilizing the `{tenant_id, period_end_date}` unique compound index, will safely handle the transaction volume of 1,000+ concurrent tenant synchronizations at month-end without read/write locking issues.

### 4. Regulatory & Compliance Assumptions

*   **Audit Acceptance:** We assume that Big Four accounting auditors will accept an immutable, append-only MongoDB `AuditLog`—combined with the Split-Screen PDF provenance UI—as sufficient evidence of data integrity for 409A valuations and SOC 1/SOC 2 compliance, eliminating the need for external third-party blockchain ledgers.
*   **Data Residency:** We assume that storing US-based startup cap table data (including PII) in US-hosted AWS/GCP regions using MongoDB Field Level Encryption (FLE) perfectly satisfies current SOC 2 Type II data residency and encryption mandates.
