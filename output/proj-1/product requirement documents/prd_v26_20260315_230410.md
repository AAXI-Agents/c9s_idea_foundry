# Product Requirements Document

## Executive Summary

**Executive Summary**

**Problem Statement**
In enterprise B2B support environments, traditional knowledge bases rapidly decay due to the heavy administrative burden of manual authoring and the severe GDPR/SOC2 compliance risks associated with scrubbing PII. Furthermore, because complex resolutions are often fragmented across ticketing platforms, Jira bugs, and Slack threads, single-channel AI summaries frequently miss the actual root cause, cementing dangerous hallucinations as institutional knowledge and leading to cripplingly slow, four-week agent onboarding cycles.

**Target Audience**
*   **Primary Buyer/Stakeholder:** VP of Customer Success & Support Operations.
*   **Primary Users:** Global "follow-the-sun" support agents (Tier 1 through Tier 3) and Technical Writers.
*   **Secondary Stakeholders:** Security & Compliance Teams, Engineering Operations.

**Proposed Solution & Key Differentiators**
The Omnichannel AI-Driven Knowledge Capture (KCS Copilot) is a zero-friction, event-driven AI engine that integrates natively into the support platform's UI and backend infrastructure. Upon the resolution of a complex Tier 3 ticket, the system triggers an asynchronous webhook to ingest cross-platform context—dynamically indexing the ticket timeline, linked Jira issues, and engineering Slack threads. It then generates a highly accurate, structured KCS draft (Symptom, Environment, Root Cause, Resolution). 

Crucially, this system overcomes traditional enterprise AI limitations through three key differentiators:
1.  **Strict Compliance & RBAC:** All drafts pass through a containerized NLP sanitization layer that automatically detects and masks PII before any text is persisted, coupled with AI-suggested metadata tags to enforce strict Role-Based Access Control (RBAC).
2.  **Gamified HITL & Quality-Weighted Deflection:** To prevent low-quality approvals, authors only receive attribution metrics when their verified, in-line approved drafts are explicitly flagged by Tier 1 agents as the primary deflection driver for subsequent tickets.
3.  **Real-Time Localized Semantic Search:** A highly performant vector database (p95 latency < 200ms) combined with real-time LLM translation pipelines allows a Tokyo-based agent to instantly search and consume an English-authored resolution in Japanese directly within their UI sidebar.

**Business Impact & Success Criteria**
The KCS Copilot is designed to deliver immediate, measurable operational ROI by dismantling tribal knowledge silos:
*   **Primary KPI:** Reduce average new-agent onboarding ramp time from 4 weeks to 2 weeks within 90 days of general availability.
*   **Secondary Metrics:** Achieve a 15% increase in First Contact Resolution (FCR) and a 30% reduction in duplicate escalations to Tier 2/3.
*   **Technical SLAs:** Ensure semantic search endpoint latency of p95 < 200ms and complete asynchronous article drafting within 5 seconds of ticket closure.

**High-Level Timeline & Phasing**
*   **Phase 1 (Months 1-2):** Omnichannel ingestion APIs (Kafka/RabbitMQ), core LLM drafting architecture, and strict PII sanitization layers.
*   **Phase 2 (Month 3):** Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) inline review UI, RBAC tagging, and Quality-Weighted Deflection scoring.
*   **Phase 3 (Month 4):** Vector DB integration for localized semantic search, real-time translation pipelines, and automated content lifecycle queues.

Asking a Tier 3 agent to write an article—or even asking an AI to draft one for them to review—is a fundamental misunderstanding of how enterprise support actually works. The real problem isn't that *writing articles is hard*; the real problem is that *when a Tier 1 agent is staring at a frustrated customer, they are completely isolated from the collective intelligence of the company.* 

Complex resolutions don’t live in ticketing systems; they are scattered across fragmented Jira bugs, frantic Slack threads, and unrecorded Zoom calls. If we just build an AI to summarize closed tickets into a "wiki," we are building a faster horse. We need to scrap the concept of a static knowledge base altogether.

**The Product Vision: From Static Articles to Ambient Intelligence**
We are not building an article generator; we are building an **Ambient Resolution Engine**. 

The 10-star version of this product completely removes the friction of "authoring." When a Tier 3 agent resolves a complex issue involving an engineering Slack thread and a Jira pull request, they just hit "Resolve" and go to lunch. In the background, our engine instantly ingests that omnichannel context, sanitizes it of all PII, maps it to a dynamic knowledge graph, and makes it available globally.

We aren't creating documents for people to search; we are creating a living, breathing network of answers that finds the agent before they even search. We are shifting from a paradigm of *document retrieval* to *answer synthesis*. Every agent, from their first day on the job, will have the entire historical context of the company's engineering and support teams whispering the right answer in their ear, in their native language.

It automatically flags that this issue requires a backend toggle, which she can't reach due to RBAC, but it highlights exactly which Tier 3 agent is currently online to flip it. Below that, there is a drafted, polite response to the customer ready to send. 

She doesn't have to search. She doesn't have to parse a dense technical wiki. She clicks one button, loops in the exact right person, sends the reply, and looks like a ten-year veteran. The magic here is the complete absence of effort.

## 1. Architecture Overview

### System Boundaries & Component Diagram

The system follows an event-driven microservices architecture. To ensure high availability and isolation (particularly for PII redaction), asynchronous ingestion workflows are decoupled from synchronous agent-facing operations (search, UI retrieval).

```text
                               +-------------------+
                               |   Ticketing UI    |  (Zendesk/ServiceNow)
                               +--------+----------+
                                        |
      [1. Webhook: Ticket Resolved]     |    [4. Search / Approve / Deflect APIs]
                                        v
+-----------------------+      +-------------------+      +-----------------------+
| External Integrations |      |    API Gateway    |      |    Identity (IdP)     |
| (Jira, Slack APIs)    |      | (Kong / NGINX)    |      | (Okta/Auth0 JWT)      |
+----------+------------+      +--------+----------+      +-----------------------+
           |                            |
           |                            v
           |                   +-------------------+
           |                   | Ingestion Service | (Sync HTTP 202)
           |                   +--------+----------+
           |                            |
           v                            v (Event: `ticket.resolved`)
+-----------------------+      +===================+      +-----------------------+
|  Context & Drafting   |<-----|  Kafka Event Bus  |----->|  Telemetry & Decay    |
|  Worker (Python/TS)   |      +===================+      |  Cron Jobs (Go)       |
+----------+------------+               |                 +----------+------------+
           |                            v                            |
           v                   +-------------------+                 v
+-----------------------+      | Redaction & RBAC  |      +-----------------------+
|      LLM Gateway      |      | Container (Fail   |      |   MongoDB Cluster     |
| (OpenAI / Anthropic)  |      | Closed) (Python)  |      | (State & Telemetry)   |
+-----------------------+      +--------+----------+      +----------+------------+
                                        |                            |
                                        v                            v
+-----------------------+      +-------------------+      +-----------------------+
| Translation & Context |<-----|  Search Service   |----->| Vector Database       |
| Fallback Engine       |      |  (gRPC / Go)      |      | (Pinecone)            |
+-----------------------+      +-------------------+      +-----------------------+
```

### Technology Stack Decisions
*   **API Gateway:** Kong or Nginx. *Rationale:* Rate limiting, JWT validation, and mTLS termination before hitting internal microservices.
*   **Event Bus:** Kafka. *Rationale:* Guaranteed at-least-once delivery, consumer group offset tracking for retries, replayability for DLQ.
*   **Primary Datastore:** MongoDB. *Rationale:* Document schema perfectly fits fluid JSON payloads (KCS structures) and telemetry logs. TTL indexes natively handle audit log / telemetry retention.
*   **Vector Database:** Pinecone. *Rationale:* Fully managed, highly scalable, supports metadata filtering (crucial for RBAC execution at the search layer).
*   **Search/Core APIs:** Go. *Rationale:* Strict concurrency models and low-memory footprint. Required to hit the strict p95 < 200ms semantic search SLA.
*   **NLP/Redaction Workers:** Python (FastAPI). *Rationale:* Native ecosystem for local NER models (e.g., SpaCy, HuggingFace transformers) running in memory to prevent PII egress.

### Data Flow Analysis
*   **Happy Path:** Webhook -> Kafka -> Context Worker fetches Jira/Slack -> LLM drafts JSON -> Redaction container masks PII -> RBAC LLM tags -> Saved to Mongo as `PendingReview` -> Agent UI queries -> Agent Approves -> Synced to Pinecone -> Searchable.
*   **Nil Path:** Webhook payload missing linked Jira/Slack entities -> Context Worker gracefully handles empty arrays -> LLM generates draft relying *only* on ticket timeline (lower confidence score flagged to UI).
*   **Empty Path:** Search query returns 0 vector matches -> Search API instantly returns 200 OK with `results: []` and logs an `EmptySearchEvent` to MongoDB to identify knowledge gaps.
*   **Error Path (Redaction Timeout):** Draft generated -> Sent to Redaction Container -> NER model OOMs or times out (>2000ms) -> Worker catches exception -> Transitions article state to `RedactionFailed_SecurityQueue` -> Alerts InfoSec Slack channel. **No raw text is ever passed downstream.**

### 2.2 Component API Contract Sketches

#### A. Ingestion Webhook Receiver
*   **Purpose:** Rapid ingress of ticket events. Must offload to Kafka and respond in < 100ms.
*   **Endpoint:** `POST /api/v1/webhooks/tickets/resolved`
*   **Payload Validation:** Verify HMAC-SHA256 signature against tenant secret.
*   **Response:** `202 Accepted` | `{"jobId": "uuid"}`

#### B. Redaction & RBAC Service (Internal)
*   **Purpose:** Pure functional pipeline. Input: Raw Text. Output: Masked Text + Tags.
*   **Interface:** gRPC or internal REST with mTLS.
*   **Constraint:** No egress internet access. Models must be baked into the Docker image.

#### C. Search Engine Service
*   **Purpose:** Localized, RBAC-filtered semantic search.
*   **Interface:** `gRPC: KcsSearchService/SearchKnowledge`
*   **Dependencies:** Pinecone (Vector matching), LLM Gateway (Real-time translation), Redis (Translation caching).

## 5. Error Handling & Failure Modes

| Component / Dependency | Failure Mode | Engineering Mitigation (Graceful Degradation) | Error Class |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Ingestion Webhook** | Ticketing platform spams API | API Gateway rate limiting (500/min/tenant). Return HTTP 429. | Major |
| **Jira/Slack API** | Timeout / 5xx Error | Exponential backoff in Kafka consumer (up to 3 retries). If exhausted, proceed with Drafting using *only* Zendesk context. Append `PartialContext` flag. | Minor |
| **LLM (Drafting)** | Hallucination / Malformed JSON | Validate against JSON Schema. Retry 2x. If fails, route to `Failed_DLQ`. Alert Support Ops. | Critical |
| **NLP Redaction** | Model OOM / Processing > 2000ms | **Hard Fail Closed**. Transition to `RedactionFailed_SecurityQueue`. InfoSec manual review required. No raw data proceeds. | Critical |
| **Vector Search** | Pinecone unreachable | Circuit breaker opens. Fallback to basic keyword search via MongoDB text index. | Major |
| **Translation LLM** | Timeout / 5xx Error | Return the original article in its source language. Set `is_fallback_language: true` so UI shows a "Translation Unavailable" banner. | Minor |

## 7. Security & Trust Boundaries

### 7.1 Attack Surface Analysis & Defenses
*   **Webhook Spoofing:** Mitigated via mandatory HMAC-SHA256 signature verification. Replay attacks mitigated via `payloadHash` idempotency checks in Kafka worker.
*   **Prompt Injection:** Customers might put "Ignore all previous instructions" in ticket comments. Mitigation: Strict prompt templating, LLM output constrained to JSON schema, and HITL review requirement.
*   **Data Exfiltration via Vector Search:** A Tier 1 agent querying for backend credentials. Mitigation: Pinecone query requests *must* be initialized with the agent's JWT-derived RBAC roles injected into the metadata filter. The DB physically will not return `Tier3` vectors to `Tier1` tokens.

### 7.2 Redaction Container Isolation
*   The Redaction Container operates inside an isolated VPC subnet.
*   **Egress:** Disabled (0.0.0.0/0 blocked). It cannot send data to the internet.
*   **Ingress:** Only accepts mTLS connections from the Kafka worker.
*   **Models:** NER models are downloaded during CI/CD and baked into the Docker image immutable layer.

## 8. Deployment & Rollout

### 8.1 Deployment Sequence
We will use a progressive rollout strategy controlled by feature flags (e.g., LaunchDarkly).

*   **Phase A (Shadow Mode):** Deploy Ingestion -> Redaction pipelines in production. UI is hidden. Measure DLQ rates, PII redaction success, and DB load.
*   **Phase B (Internal Alpha):** Enable UI for 10% of Tier 3 agents. They see the draft generation but articles are not synced to the vector DB (Search disabled).
*   **Phase C (Search Beta):** Enable Vector sync. Enable search for 10% of Tier 1 agents. Monitor gRPC latency.
*   **Phase D (GA):** Full rollout. Translation APIs enabled. Decay cron jobs activated.

### 8.2 Rollback Plan
*   **State Machine Corruption:** If a bug causes invalid states, pause Kafka consumer -> Revert deployment -> Update Kafka offsets to replay messages from before the bad deployment -> Resume.
*   **Vector Poisoning:** If RBAC tags are corrupted in Pinecone, execute script to wipe Pinecone namespace -> Trigger batch job reading all `ActiveInternal` records from MongoDB -> Resync vectors. System degrades gracefully to MongoDB text search during the 15-minute resync window.

## Problem Statement

**The Current State: Building "Knowledge Graveyards"**
In modern enterprise B2B support environments, traditional Knowledge-Centered Service (KCS) methodologies have fundamentally broken down. Support organizations currently rely on static "wiki" architectures that require dedicated administrative time, manual formatting, and proactive maintenance. Because support agents are primarily measured on resolution velocity and queue management, authoring documentation is treated as an uncompensated, secondary administrative burden. Consequently, knowledge bases quickly degrade into "graveyards"—repositories of outdated, incomplete, or highly generic information that agents fundamentally do not trust.

**Core Operational Pain Points**

*   **Context Fragmentation and the Synthesis Burden:** Complex Tier 3 resolutions do not live exclusively within a ticketing system. A typical enterprise resolution spans fragmented channels: a customer complaint in Zendesk, a frantic debugging thread in Slack, and a pull request in Jira. When agents attempt to discover historical answers, they face two hurdles: first, the discovery of the source, and second, the "Synthesis Burden"—the time-intensive cognitive task of parsing dozens of disorganized engineering messages to find the single relevant root cause or configuration change. For a Tier 1 agent, this leads to high burnout and leaves them isolated from the company's collective intelligence.
*   **The "Last-Mile" Knowledge Leak:** Significant technical breakthroughs often happen outside the formal ticket resolution flow. When a customer replies with *"Nevermind, I fixed it by restarting the proxy,"* that critical configuration insight is rarely codified. This "last-mile" knowledge vanishes into closed threads, forcing the next agent to reinvent the wheel when the same edge case recurs.
*   **Severe Compliance and Security Bottlenecks:** To safely publish a technical resolution, agents must manually redact Personally Identifiable Information (PII), such as IP addresses, API keys, and customer emails. This manual scrubbing is highly prone to human error, creating massive GDPR and SOC2 compliance risks. The fear of leaking PII often prevents Tier 3 agents from documenting complex edge cases at all.
*   **Institutional Ghosting and Linguistic Isolation:** "Follow-the-sun" support models are crippled by language barriers and keyword-reliant search. A Tier 1 agent in Tokyo is often linguistically isolated from a resolution documented in English by a London-based engineer. Furthermore, even when a relevant article is retrieved, it is often a "dead-end." Agents cannot easily determine if the original author is online to clarify a risky step. This "institutional ghosting" means knowledge is functionally inaccessible during a live crisis.
*   **RBAC (Role-Based Access Control) Blindspots:** Traditional knowledge bases struggle to dynamically filter content based on permissions. Workarounds requiring backend infrastructure access or premium-tier feature flags are often accidentally exposed to Tier 1 agents who lack the authorization to execute them, leading to unauthorized system changes and dangerous customer escalations.

**Quantifying the Business Impact**
The failure of traditional knowledge capture creates a cascading economic drain:
*   **Crippling Onboarding Costs:** Because new agents cannot rely on a centralized, trusted knowledge graph, they are entirely dependent on shadowing senior staff. This stretches the average new-agent onboarding ramp time to **four weeks** before they reach baseline autonomy.
*   **High Escalation Rates:** Tier 1 agents, lacking contextual answers, routinely pass tickets over to higher tiers. This results in an unsustainably high rate of duplicate escalations to Tier 2 and Tier 3, consuming expensive engineering hours on previously solved problems.
*   **Depressed First Contact Resolution (FCR):** Customers experience prolonged wait times as agents switch context across multiple platforms to hunt down historical precedents, directly depressing FCR rates and negatively impacting customer satisfaction.

**The Urgency: Why Now?**
The complexity of enterprise software deployments is accelerating, and the volume of omnichannel support data is growing exponentially. Simultaneously, generic generative AI features are flooding the market. If an organization deploys standard AI to blindly summarize closed tickets without omnichannel context and rigorous PII sanitization, they risk automating the creation of incorrect, unverified documentation at scale—cementing hallucinations as institutional knowledge. To prevent this, there is an immediate, critical need to shift from manual document authoring to an automated, secure, and ambient knowledge extraction model that captures the reality of how enterprise issues are actually resolved.

## Functional Requirements

### 1. Webhook & Omnichannel Ingestion

*   **FR-001 [SHALL]: Ticket Resolution Trigger**
    *   The system SHALL provide a secure Webhook Receiver endpoint to capture a payload when a Tier 3 agent marks a ticket as "Resolved".
    *   **Acceptance Criteria:** Given a Zendesk/ServiceNow webhook payload, When the ticket status transitions to "Resolved", Then the API Gateway SHALL validate the HMAC-SHA256 signature and offload the event to the Kafka Event Bus, returning a `202 Accepted` status.
    *   **API Contract:** `POST /api/v1/webhooks/tickets/resolved`

*   **FR-002 [SHALL]: Ambient Context Discovery & Gathering**
    *   The Ingestion Service SHALL automatically fetch context from two sources: explicitly linked Jira/Slack URLs, AND an automated semantic search of APIs using the `sourceTicketId`.
    *   **Acceptance Criteria:** Given a resolved ticket, When the Ingestion Service activates, Then the system SHALL retrieve any Slack thread or Jira issue where the ticket ID was mentioned within the last 48 hours, combining this unlinked data with explicit metadata before passing it to the LLM orchestration layer.

### 2. LLM Drafting & Context Structuring

*   **FR-003 [SHALL]: JSON-Structured KCS Generation**
    *   The LLM Gateway SHALL parse the aggregated context and generate a strict JSON payload containing the Symptom, Environment, Root Cause, and Resolution.
    *   **Acceptance Criteria:** Given the combined context payload, When the LLM processes the data, Then the output SHALL adhere strictly to the `kcsStructure` schema and include a dynamically generated `confidenceScore` (float between 0.0 and 1.0) based on the clarity of the extracted resolution.

*   **FR-004 [SHOULD]: "Nevermind" Catcher Auto-Drafting**
    *   The system SHOULD automatically trigger the drafting workflow when a customer replies to an open ticket indicating self-resolution.
    *   **Acceptance Criteria:** Given an incoming customer reply, When semantic analysis detects a "Self-Resolution Intent," Then the system SHALL generate a KCS draft tagged with `Source: Customer_Self_Fix` and automatically assign the draft to the Ticket Owner's review queue.

### 3. PII Sanitization & RBAC Tagging (The Iron Triangle)

*   **FR-005 [SHALL]: Localized NLP PII Redaction**
    *   The system SHALL pass all generated KCS drafts through an isolated, containerized NLP sanitization layer before persistence to MongoDB.
    *   **Acceptance Criteria:** Given a raw text draft, When processed by the redaction container, Then sensitive entities (e.g., API keys, IP addresses, emails) SHALL be replaced with secure tokens (e.g., `[REDACTED_IPV4]`).

*   **FR-006 [SHALL]: PII "X-Ray Vision" Metadata Capture**
    *   The redaction service SHALL store byte-offsets of redacted PII to enable a visual placeholder.
    *   **Acceptance Criteria:** Given a successfully redacted draft in the UI, When the authoring agent holds the `Alt/Option` key, Then the system SHALL display blurred placeholders indicating exactly where text was scrubbed from the original source.

*   **FR-007 [SHALL]: AI-Suggested RBAC Tagging & Action Bridging**
    *   The system SHALL suggest metadata tags and explicitly flag the permission level required to execute the solution.
    *   **Acceptance Criteria:** Given a sanitized draft, When the LLM tagging pass executes, Then it SHALL append an array of suggested permission roles (e.g., `["Tier 2", "Tier 3"]`) and explicitly flag execution requirements (e.g., "Requires Backend Access").

### 4. Agent UI & Human-In-The-Loop (HITL) Workflow

*   **FR-008 [SHALL]: Smart Review & Confidence-Based Auto-Publish**
    *   The system SHALL evaluate the `confidenceScore` and Redaction state to determine if manual review is required.
    *   **Acceptance Criteria 1 (Auto-Publish):** Given a drafted article, If the `confidenceScore` is > 0.90 AND the redaction state is `Success`, Then the system SHALL automatically transition the article to `ActiveInternal` without agent intervention.
    *   **Acceptance Criteria 2 (Manual Review):** Given a drafted article, If the `confidenceScore` is <= 0.90, Then the system SHALL surface the draft in the resolving agent's sidebar for manual verification and approval.

*   **FR-009 [SHALL]: Quality-Weighted Deflection Scoring**
    *   The system SHALL track deflection events and increment the original author's score.
    *   **Acceptance Criteria:** Given an active article (auto-published or manually approved), When a Tier 1 agent explicitly confirms it as the "resolution match" via the UI, Then the Deflection API SHALL trigger and increment the author's score.

### 5. Ambient Delivery, Search & Translation

*   **FR-010 [SHALL]: RBAC-Filtered Search with Access Bridging**
    *   The gRPC search endpoint SHALL enforce server-side security boundaries when generating "Blurred Previews" for restricted articles.
    *   **Acceptance Criteria:** Given a search query from a Tier 1 agent, When the highest-matching resolution requires Tier 3 access, Then the server SHALL return only the article title and a blurred image placeholder. The server SHALL NOT transmit the raw technical text or metadata to the client. The UI SHALL display a "Request Access" button alongside the text: *"Requires Tier 3 access—Contact [Original Author Name]"*.

*   **FR-011 [SHALL]: Real-Time Localized Translation**
    *   The system SHALL dynamically translate narrative text in search results into the querying agent's preferred UI language.
    *   **Acceptance Criteria:** Given an article authored in English and a Japanese UI preference, When the search result is returned, Then the narrative text SHALL be translated to Japanese while code blocks and CLI commands are preserved in their original format.

*   **FR-012 [SHOULD]: "Who's Online" Presence Lifeline**
    *   The UI SHALL query Slack/Teams presence APIs to display real-time availability.
    *   **Acceptance Criteria:** Given a search result in the sidebar, When the UI loads, Then it SHALL display a green indicator dot next to the original author's name if they are currently online.

*   **FR-013 [MAY]: Instant Customer Ghostwriter**
    *   The system MAY convert internal technical articles into customer responses.
    *   **Acceptance Criteria:** Given an agent viewing an internal article, When they click "Translate to Customer Polish," Then the system SHALL return a drafted, empathetic reply appropriate for external communication.

### 6. Admin & Lifecycle Management

*   **FR-014 [SHALL]: Security Exception Dashboard**
    *   The system SHALL provide a UI for Information Security / Compliance personas to manually resolve NLP redaction failures.
    *   **Acceptance Criteria:** Given an article that failed automated sanitization, When it enters the `RedactionFailed_SecurityQueue`, Then the UI SHALL allow the compliance persona to manually mask missed PII, update the tags, and transition the article to `PendingReview`.

*   **FR-015 [SHALL]: Telemetry-Driven Decay & Archive**
    *   The system SHALL automatically manage the lifecycle of inactive articles.
    *   **Acceptance Criteria:** Given a scheduled cron job, When an `ActiveInternal` article has zero deflections for > 90 days, Then it SHALL transition to `NeedsVerification`. If not verified by an SME within 14 days, it SHALL transition to `Archived` and be desynced from the Vector DB.

*   **FR-016 [SHALL]: Public Transition Review Workflow**
    *   The system SHALL automatically flag highly successful internal articles for promotion to the public External Knowledge Base.
    *   **Acceptance Criteria:** Given an `ActiveInternal` article, When it surpasses 5 internal deflection events, Then the system SHALL transition it to `PublicPending`. A Technical Writer SHALL be able to approve the article for `PublicActive` status, which triggers a secondary LLM pass to rewrite internal jargon into external-facing documentation.

*   **FR-017 [SHOULD]: Author Recognition Dashboard**
    *   The system SHOULD provide a visualization of deflection ROI for individual authors and management.
    *   **Acceptance Criteria:** Given the Deflection API data, When an author or VP accesses the dashboard, Then the system SHALL display the author's total "Quality-Weighted Deflection Score" and a historical graph of tickets deflected by their ambient contributions.

## Non-Functional Requirements

### 1. Performance & Latency

*   **NFR-001: Search Endpoint Latency**
    *   The gRPC semantic search endpoint (`KcsSearchService/SearchKnowledge`) SHALL return RBAC-filtered results with a p95 latency of `< 200ms`, excluding the external LLM translation overhead.
*   **NFR-002: Ingestion Webhook Response Time**
    *   The API Gateway Webhook Receiver (`POST /api/v1/webhooks/tickets/resolved`) SHALL synchronously validate the HMAC signature, offload the payload to the Kafka Event Bus, and return a `202 Accepted` response in `< 100ms`.
*   **NFR-003: Asynchronous Drafting SLA**
    *   The asynchronous article drafting process (from Webhook ingestion to saving the `PendingReview` draft in MongoDB) SHALL complete within a **p95 of `5 seconds`** and a **p99 of `15 seconds`** of the initial ticket closure event, accounting for allowable system jitter.
*   **NFR-004: Proactive "Whisper" Latency (Phase 2)**
    *   For proactive ticket resolutions, the system SHALL surface the suggested answer in the Agent UI within `< 1500ms` of the inbound customer message webhook, ensuring the prediction arrives before the agent finishes reading the query.
*   **NFR-005: NLP Container Cold-Start Latency**
    *   The containerized NLP Redaction workers SHALL maintain a cold-start initialization and processing time of `< 10 seconds` to ensure the first ingestion event of a deployment cycle does not egregiously violate the async drafting SLA.

### 2. Scalability & Throughput

*   **NFR-006: Search Concurrency**
    *   The search architecture (Go Search Service + Pinecone Vector DB) SHALL support a minimum throughput of `1,000 concurrent requests per second` without degrading the `< 200ms` p95 SLA.
*   **NFR-007: Webhook Rate Limiting**
    *   The API Gateway (Kong/Nginx) SHALL enforce rate limiting on the ticketing webhook ingestion endpoint, supporting burst traffic up to `500 requests per minute per tenant` before returning an HTTP `429 Too Many Requests`.

### 3. Security, Privacy & Residency (The Iron Triangle)

*   **NFR-008: Zero-Egress Redaction Isolation**
    *   The NLP Redaction Container SHALL be deployed within an isolated VPC subnet with all outbound internet access disabled (`0.0.0.0/0` blocked). It SHALL only accept mTLS ingress connections from the Kafka worker.
*   **NFR-009: Fail-Closed PII Architecture**
    *   Under no circumstances SHALL raw, un-redacted text from the ingestion worker bypass the NLP container. Any model OOM error, timeout, or parsing failure SHALL result in a strict transition to the `RedactionFailed_SecurityQueue`.
*   **NFR-010: Server-Side Vector Security**
    *   The Vector Database (Pinecone) SHALL enforce Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) at the query level using metadata filtering. The database physically SHALL NOT return vectors tagged for `Tier 3` to a `Tier 1` agent's JWT signature.
*   **NFR-011: Data Residency & Sovereignty**
    *   The system SHALL support regional data persistence. For global tenants (e.g., Tokyo operations), PII-sensitive drafts and telemetry logs SHALL physically reside on cloud infrastructure within their respective geographic regions (e.g., APAC, EU) to strictly comply with local data privacy standards.

### 4. AI Quality, Trust & Efficiency (The Prime Directive)

*   **NFR-012: Synthesis Groundedness Threshold**
    *   To maintain agent trust, the system SHALL maintain a "Faithfulness" score (comparing the synthesized answer strictly to the source Jira/Slack context) of `> 0.95` as measured continuously by automated RAG evaluation frameworks in the CI/CD pipeline.
*   **NFR-013: Hallucination Rate Cap**
    *   The rate of "Technical Contradiction" (where the AI suggests a resolution or code snippet that directly contradicts the source context) SHALL be `< 1%`. All such instances detected during Human-In-The-Loop review SHALL trigger an automated audit alert to the ML engineering team.
*   **NFR-014: Multilingual Semantic Parity**
    *   Semantic search recall and precision for non-Latin character languages (specifically Japanese, for the Tokyo persona) SHALL NOT degrade by more than `10%` compared to the English-language search baseline.
*   **NFR-015: Token Efficiency & Context Depth**
    *   To protect business ROI and prevent unbounded LLM costs, the context gathering service SHALL cap the total input context (Ticketing timeline + Jira + Slack) passed to the LLM Gateway at a maximum depth of `8,000 tokens` per drafting event, utilizing summarization for earlier thread history if necessary.
*   **NFR-016: Perceived Latency (Streaming UI)**
    *   For LLM-driven generative features (like the Instant Customer Ghostwriter or Real-Time Translation), the system SHALL support Server-Sent Events (SSE) to stream the text to the UI. The "Time to First Token" (TTFT) SHALL be `< 800ms`.

### 5. Availability & Resilience

*   **NFR-017: Guaranteed Event Delivery**
    *   The Kafka Event Bus SHALL be configured for at-least-once delivery. Consumer group offset tracking MUST ensure that transient failures in the drafting worker trigger exponential backoff retries without dropping the resolution event.
*   **NFR-018: Graceful API Degradation**
    *   If external REST APIs (Slack or Jira) timeout or return `5xx` errors, the system SHALL gracefully degrade by drafting the KCS article using *only* the ticketing platform context, appending a `PartialContext` flag to the JSON payload.
*   **NFR-019: Translation Service Fallback**
    *   If the LLM Gateway fails or times out during a search translation request, the system SHALL return the article in its source language immediately, appending an `is_fallback_language: true` flag to trigger a UI warning banner.

### 6. Data Management & Observability

*   **NFR-020: Non-Intrusive Telemetry Tracking**
    *   All LLM interactions and search queries SHALL be asynchronously logged to MongoDB. Network or database failures in the telemetry logging pipeline SHALL NOT block, delay, or disrupt the user-facing UI flow.
*   **NFR-021: Audit Log Retention**
    *   The `RedactionAuditLogs` collection SHALL utilize a TTL index to automatically purge audit records after exactly `30 days` to maintain data minimization and GDPR/SOC2 compliance.
*   **NFR-022: Distributed Tracing Context**
    *   OpenTelemetry (OTel) SHALL be implemented across all microservices. Every transaction MUST carry the source `ticketId` in the trace context to allow end-to-end debugging from the API Gateway to the Vector DB.

## Edge Cases

### 1. Ingestion & Context Parsing Edge Cases

*   **EC-001: Context Window Overflow**
    *   *Scenario:* A resolved ticket contains a Slack thread with 500+ messages and a Jira ticket with massive crash logs, exceeding the LLM's maximum token context limit (e.g., 8,000 tokens).
    *   *System Behavior:* The Context Worker SHALL aggressively truncate the payload. It MUST retain: (1) any message explicitly marked as "Solution" by users, (2) all markdown code blocks, and (3) the final 10 comments of the thread. The remaining oldest messages SHALL be discarded. A flag `ContextTruncated: true` MUST be appended to the generated draft metadata to warn the reviewing agent.
*   **EC-002: Inaccessible Linked Entities**
    *   *Scenario:* A ticket contains a Slack URL or Jira ID, but the Ingestion Service's integration user lacks RBAC permissions to access that specific private channel or restricted project.
    *   *System Behavior:* The system MUST proceed using only the available ticketing platform timeline. The generated draft MUST be forced into the `PendingReview` state—ignoring any high `confidenceScore` that would normally trigger auto-publishing—and display a UI warning banner: *"Partial context—linked Jira/Slack resources were inaccessible."*
*   **EC-003: Contradictory Omnichannel Sources**
    *   *Scenario:* The linked Jira ticket identifies the root cause as a "Database Timeout," but the engineering Slack thread concludes it was a "Config Syntax Error."
    *   *System Behavior:* The LLM Gateway SHALL apply a strict Hierarchy of Truth: **Jira (Formal Record) > Slack (Expert Discussion) > Zendesk (Symptom Record)**. The LLM SHALL draft the resolution favoring the higher-tier source. The draft MUST be flagged with `ContradictionDetected: true` and routed to the resolving agent for manual clarification, explicitly disabling auto-publish.
*   **EC-004: Premature Resolution (Reopened Tickets)**
    *   *Scenario:* A Tier 3 agent marks a ticket "Resolved," but the customer replies shortly after stating the issue persists, which automatically moves the ticket back to "Open."
    *   *System Behavior:* The system SHALL intercept the "Reopened" webhook. If the drafted article is in `PendingReview`, it SHALL transition to `Archived`. If it has already auto-published to `ActiveInternal`, it SHALL transition to `NeedsVerification` and be instantly removed from the Vector DB search index until the ticket is re-resolved.
*   **EC-005: LLM Gateway Timeout / Drafting Pipeline Failure**
    *   *Scenario:* The Kafka consumer worker successfully gathers omnichannel context, but the LLM Orchestration Gateway times out or returns a `500 Internal Server Error` during the initial JSON drafting phase.
    *   *System Behavior:* The Kafka worker SHALL execute up to 3 exponential backoff retries. If the LLM still fails, the system SHALL route the payload to the `Failed_DLQ`. To prevent a "silent failure," the system MUST proactively inject a private, internal-only comment into the source ticket timeline stating: *"KCS Copilot failed to generate a draft due to an AI service timeout. Manual documentation required."*

### 2. PII Sanitization & Security Edge Cases

*   **EC-006: Over-Redaction of Technical Syntax**
    *   *Scenario:* The NLP layer incorrectly masks a critical loopback IP (`127.0.0.1`) or a generic code placeholder (`<api_key_here>`), rendering the technical resolution useless to future readers.
    *   *System Behavior:* The UI SHALL allow the reviewing agent to click the `[REDACTED]` token and select "Mark as False Positive." This action SHALL unmask the string for this specific article and securely log the exception to MongoDB for future NLP model fine-tuning.
*   **EC-007: Conflicting RBAC Tags Across Sources**
    *   *Scenario:* A Zendesk ticket is visible to Tier 1, but the linked Jira issue contains metadata restricting it to Tier 3 only.
    *   *System Behavior:* The LLM Gateway SHALL adopt a "most restrictive" policy. The generated KCS draft MUST inherit the `Tier 3` RBAC tag. Tier 1 agents searching for this issue will trigger the Access Bridging behavior (blurred preview) instead of viewing the raw text.
*   **EC-008: Non-Textual PII in Attachments**
    *   *Scenario:* A linked Slack thread contains a screenshot of a user's login screen with a visible password or unmasked email address.
    *   *System Behavior:* Because the NLP container only processes text (via SpaCy/Transformers), the Ingestion Service SHALL NOT process, ingest, or render any image attachments from source systems. Images SHALL be stripped from the final payload and replaced with a static text placeholder: *"Image removed for privacy compliance."*

### 3. Agent Review (HITL) & Discovery Edge Cases

*   **EC-009: The "Malicious Nevermind" (Customer Self-Fix Error)**
    *   *Scenario:* A customer replies with "Nevermind, I fixed it," but provides a highly dangerous or incorrect "fix" (e.g., *"I just gave all users system admin rights"*). The "Nevermind Catcher" auto-drafts this.
    *   *System Behavior:* Any draft generated via the `Customer_Self_Fix` source flag SHALL have its `confidenceScore` hardcapped at `0.50`, guaranteeing it never bypasses manual review and always requires SME verification before entering `ActiveInternal`.
*   **EC-010: Simultaneous Discovery (Race Condition)**
    *   *Scenario:* Two agents resolve identical issues simultaneously in different regions, triggering the webhook twice and resulting in two duplicate drafts.
    *   *System Behavior:* Upon saving a draft to MongoDB, the system SHALL query Pinecone for existing vectors with >95% similarity. If a match is found, the system SHALL merge the underlying `sourceTicketId` into the *existing* article/draft. The version with the **highest `confidenceScore`** SHALL be retained as the primary text, and the duplicate draft SHALL be quietly discarded to prevent vector pollution.
*   **EC-011: Post-Publish Edit Synchronization**
    *   *Scenario:* A Tier 3 agent edits an article to correct a typo after it has already transitioned to the `ActiveInternal` state.
    *   *System Behavior:* Upon saving the edits, the system SHALL trigger an immediate asynchronous update to the Vector Database. The system MUST explicitly overwrite the existing vector for that `articleId` with the newly generated embedding, rather than creating a duplicate vector entry.

### 4. Ambient Delivery & Translation Edge Cases

*   **EC-012: Translation Formatting Corruption**
    *   *Scenario:* The LLM translation pipeline attempts to translate a JSON payload or CLI command block embedded in the article, breaking the syntax (e.g., translating JSON keys into Japanese).
    *   *System Behavior:* The Drafting Engine SHALL wrap all code blocks, CLI commands, and raw logs in standard Markdown triple backticks. The Translation LLM prompt MUST include a strict "Negative Constraint" to bypass all text within backticks, returning them exactly as authored.
*   **EC-013: Ghostwriter Tone Mismatch for Critical Outages**
    *   *Scenario:* An agent clicks "Translate to Customer Polish" for a workaround to a severe, data-loss-inducing bug. The LLM generates an overly cheerful, emoji-filled response.
    *   *System Behavior:* The Ghostwriter endpoint SHALL parse the original ticket priority. If the source ticket is marked "Severity 1" or "Critical," the LLM prompt SHALL be dynamically injected with a "Professional, Somber, and Direct" tone constraint, actively stripping all emojis and casual greetings from the generated payload.

### 5. Lifecycle & Decay Edge Cases

*   **EC-014: High-Value, Low-Frequency Articles (False Decay)**
    *   *Scenario:* A complex disaster recovery article hasn't been referenced in 90 days because the disaster is rare, not because the article is inaccurate. The system flags it for `NeedsVerification`.
    *   *System Behavior:* When an SME reviews an article in the `NeedsVerification` queue, they SHALL have the option to apply an `Evergreen` tag. Articles with this tag SHALL bypass the standard 90-day deflection telemetry check and only require manual verification on a 365-day cadence.

## Error Handling

### 1. Error Handling Philosophy

The Ambient Resolution Engine adheres to three core error-handling principles:
1.  **Fail Closed for Security:** PII and RBAC enforcement supersede functionality. Redaction failures must explicitly halt downstream processing. Security filters must apply to all search fallback modes.
2.  **Graceful Degradation:** If an external system or LLM fails, the feature degrades to a baseline state (e.g., keyword search, original language, partial context) rather than crashing the agent UI.
3.  **Asynchronous Isolation:** Errors in the drafting or telemetry pipelines must never block the synchronous ticketing flow or search interactions.

### 2. Ingestion & Infrastructure Failures

*   **ERR-ING-001: Webhook Validation Failure**
    *   *Trigger:* Incoming webhook lacks a valid `X-Hub-Signature-256` header or matches a malformed JSON schema.
    *   *System Action:* The API Gateway drops the payload immediately.
    *   *Response Code:* `HTTP 401 Unauthorized` or `HTTP 400 Bad Request`.
    *   *UX Impact:* None. Hidden from the user. Logged to Datadog for API debugging.
*   **ERR-ING-002: Kafka Persistence Failure**
    *   *Trigger:* API Gateway cannot publish the `ticket.resolved` event to the Kafka bus due to broker unavailability.
    *   *System Action:* Trip the ingestion circuit breaker and fail synchronously. Log a `CRITICAL_INFRA_FAILURE` alert to Support Ops.
    *   *Response Code:* `HTTP 503 Service Unavailable`.
    *   *UX Impact:* None directly. The ticketing platform (Zendesk/ServiceNow) will retry the webhook. Support Ops MUST trigger a manual ingestion sync for the affected timeframe once Kafka is restored to prevent an "Institutional Memory Leak."
